Damn Hunter Life Chapter 187

Did it say I had to deduce it myself?

Then let me guess and try to get it right. I looked back on the path I had traveled so far. I recalled everything the status window had said to me all this time.

The status window had provoked me, mocked me, and said spine-chilling things…

“…There’s still something bothering me.”

But before dealing with the status window, an ominous thing I had forgotten came to mind.

The black entrance I had seen still hadn’t appeared. Not until now.

The tension that had barely relaxed became sharp again. I gripped Hyeong-woo’s arm tightly.

The stage inside the gate was mysterious enough, but there had been a triple entrance inside even that.

Suddenly Hyeong-woo turned his body while holding me. As if trying to protect me.

==

/Please enter.

==

In front of me and Hyeong-woo, a black-colored crack had formed. Like a door that suddenly appeared in mid-air.

Different text appeared in the status window.

==

<Stage 0>

Participant limit: 2 people

==

If there were also monsters and bosses inside this? We’re both exhausted, so could we really handle them? Although the preliminary information that just appeared didn’t mention monsters, you never know.

This was the logic Hyeong-woo had tried to use to persuade me for the tenth time.

However, I had no intention of turning away from the fate placed before me or pushing it onto someone else.

“That might have been the case until now, but not anymore.”

Hyeong-woo hesitated. Then he stared at me blankly for a while.

In the end, Hyeong-woo obediently followed along too.

And so, the inside of the gate we cautiously entered was.

“What is this place…?”

Everything was white and transparent. The ceiling, walls, and floor couldn’t be seen. We could only guess there was a flat floor since we were standing on something.

“Is this the gate’s concept?”

Despite entering with great tension, it didn’t seem like there were any other life forms here.

Anticlimactically, in front of us was placed the easiest boss monster in the world.

A sphere with no aggression and no life. A boss that gates just place there for courtesy, for formality’s sake.

I was engulfed in great doubt.

It’s just a place with a sphere, so why did the Hyeong-woo from a different timeline…

—Hyeon. What should I do with you.

Had that Hyeong-woo also entered and exited this place? If so, why did he end up calling Cheon Gang-hyeon’s name there of all places?

Could it be because things have changed from the original work now?

If so, then originally there should have been ‘something else’ here instead of a sphere.

Suddenly, I felt I should confess the truth to Hyeong-woo here and now.

No, it was almost a certainty. I had to do it. All the air remaining in this place seemed to be pushing me to do so.

I moved a few steps. I made Hyeong-woo, who was trying to follow closely like he was tied by a rope, stop in place.

Standing where I could see both the sphere and Hyeong-woo at a glance, I forced open my reluctant mouth.

“Listen carefully. I’m not Cheon Gang-hyeon.”

As if I had come a long way around a distant path, the burden that had accumulated in my heart fell with a thud. I felt both relief and fear simultaneously.

This was the first time, really the first time, I was saying this out loud.

“I’m… a possessor. I’m not the real Cheon Gang-hyeon. I’m a different person who possessed this body.”

If Hyeong-woo asked what I meant, I would drag out everything I knew to convince him.

But if Hyeong-woo really believed it, and thus learned that the real Cheon Gang-hyeon had died… the emotion he would feel was truly frightening too.

Hyeong-woo stared at me for a long time, as if facing a stranger for the first time. I endured that gaze with a miserable heart.

But Hyeong-woo’s mouth opened faster than I expected.

“By possession, you mean a different soul taking over a body, right?”

“That’s right, but…”

Hyeong-woo seemed to be taking it seriously. But far from becoming agitated, he was only very calm.

“I see.”

He only let out words in such a composed tone that it left me bewildered.

“You were thinking that way… so that’s why.”

He nodded with eyes that showed understanding. I couldn’t follow his thoughts at all.

Somehow the positions seemed reversed. I glared at Hyeong-woo, feeling like I was being left out alone. I waited for Hyeong-woo to explain.

“There’s something I haven’t told you. We agreed to be honest about everything, so I’m sorry.”

Unexpectedly, Hyeong-woo suddenly apologized. Then he started what seemed like a long story.

“But it would be strange to suddenly say it. I thought the time would come. I think that time is now.”

The atmosphere flowed like water, and I couldn’t catch the timing to interrupt.

“When we met as children, you know the relatives’ house where I ended up staying after separating from my mother. You kept saying you wanted to keep coming to play, but I said I couldn’t get permission and absolutely wouldn’t let you come.”

I clearly said I wasn’t the Cheon Gang-hyeon he knew. Why is he bringing up old stories?

I was about to feel frustrated, thinking he couldn’t believe me after all.

Regardless of what I was doing, Hyeong-woo looked straight into my eyes and continued speaking.

“Because that place wasn’t somewhere for kids to play. Actually… it was a shaman’s house.”

At the last words, I forgot whatever I had been thinking.

“Since then, living there and being exposed to those things, my eyes opened a little too. …I’ll say this as a premise. From what I can see, you are Cheon Gang-hyeon.”

Hyeong-woo approached me with one large stride in front of me, who was frozen and couldn’t move. His large hands grasped and wrapped around my cold hands.

“Because your soul is the same as when you were young and now.”

The conversation that followed, though I was speaking with my mouth, I couldn’t hear with my ears. My ears were buzzing too much. I couldn’t think of anything and felt like my head would explode.

I probably argued, asking if that meant he was a shaman too. I picked fights, being sarcastic and saying it was nonsense.

I couldn’t hear my own voice at all, but strangely, Hyeong-woo’s voice penetrated my blocked eardrums and clearly struck my mind.

“How do you think I saw those ghosts earlier? I’m not you, and I wasn’t looking through a familiar’s sight either.”

At the calm explanation, my thoughts suddenly came back a little.

I hadn’t realized it because the situation was urgent.

Come to think of it, we had encountered various spirit monsters before. Notably, water ghosts.

At that time, Hyeong-woo said he couldn’t see them because he didn’t have a familiar, but healers didn’t need to see them anyway, so just proceed with the strategy.

…That meant those were lies he made up.

I did think it was strange how he supported well even without seeing.

The reason he didn’t bother to mention it then was, as he said, due to his habit of not wanting to let others know he was different.

“I also recognized that Beom I-ryeong was heterogeneous. The fact that the mouse familiar had a personality, I thought was because of my background too. Even if I kept it secret, it was revealed in my skill and item names, but fortunately no one suspected anything.”

The willow branch that was said to chase away ghosts. The sacred tree where gods dwell. The rice wine that repels ghosts. The talismans he threw from his fingertips.

They were all related to shaman’s ritual implements.

At some point, Hyeong-woo was looking at me again with an upright gaze.

“Can I return to your story now?”

My head started ringing again. My heart was beating so loudly that it seemed to go up to my head and pound. I felt uncomfortable and nauseous.

“Your personality, way of speaking, preferences, habits like the order when eating. They’re all the same. I couldn’t fail to recognize you.”

The words Hyeong-woo said, trying to reassure me, spread through my chest like poison.

“You were Cheon Gang-hyeon from beginning to end. I don’t know what or how you’re confused about… but this is the truth.”

No.

That can’t be.

Hyeong-woo can’t accept the truth, so he’s making up an elaborate lie. A lie so plausible it could even fool me.

Hyeong-woo’s expression, watching me, gradually hardened. He reached out to me as if feeling sorry and flinched.

“Gang-hyeon.”

“No.”

“Gang-hyeon…”

“Shut up, don’t call me! What do you know to act like you know? Keep your mouth shut! Don’t tell me this or that!”

I twisted my body as if having a seizure. I screamed and threw a tantrum. I couldn’t control myself either.

Thinking that Hyeong-woo was at a loss and keeping his mouth shut in distress, hatred welled up toward me, not toward Hyeong-woo. Both my mind and vision shattered into chaos.

“No, it’s not true. I’m not Cheon Gang-hyeon.”

Then who are you?

A stone dropped with a thud in my chest. I stopped breathing from the emptiness, then repeated the same words like a madman again.

“It’s not true…”

Then I spat out with all my strength whatever was stuck in my throat and trying to block my windpipe.

“I don’t want to be Cheon Gang-hyeon!”

The echo rang a couple of times in the empty space. Suffocating silence flowed.

It felt like I had made a huge verbal mistake and come to my senses too late. I hated the dizzy emotions to death.

“Gang-hyeon.”

Hyeong-woo broke the silence and called with a trembling voice.

“You hated yourself that much…? Enough to think you didn’t want to be yourself?”
