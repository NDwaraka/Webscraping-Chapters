Damn Hunter Life Chapter 171

* * *

Heavy snow fell outside the training ground. It’s a quiet, nice scene, but my phone has been ringing since earlier.

‘Why are there phone calls from morning?’

I continue the 30-minute treatment per day. And today’s portion is strictly finished.

So Ja Hyeong-woo has no right to call at other times and hover around talking about trivial things.

[“If it weren’t for training, I would have watched the snow with you.”]

“I’m not going.”

[“Let’s watch together through the window at home. You can’t catch a cold.”]

“I’ll be alone at home—”

At this moment, Beom I-ryeong appeared before my eyes.

“Hey, hang up.”

Beom I-ryeong, who approached quickly, said the VR simulation was over and came to me, holding out a score sheet.

I read it briefly and clicked my tongue.

“You’re really naturally gifted.”

He’s 15 years old, a kid who hasn’t even been awakened for two weeks. Yet he’s showing adaptability as if he’d been training for months.

Honestly, even Woo Sae-geon probably wasn’t this good. Beom I-ryeong was a genius.

“If you’re okay with it, I’m thinking of setting up a D-rank gate practice. Want to try? If you do it now, you’ll be the youngest record holder.”

“Yes.”

Beom I-ryeong nodded obediently as if his own thoughts didn’t matter.

“Please come with me, hyung-nim.”

However, he set one condition.

Eyes too clear for a child stared at me for a long time. I agreed without difficulty.

“Alright. I’ll be your instructor.”

It’s not just because Beom I-ryeong follows only me that I decided to accompany him.

Recently, I also try not to leave Beom I-ryeong alone with other successors. The exception would be Myo Cho-hui at most. Even with Myo Cho-hui, I let her see him briefly then quickly bring him back to stay with me.

Before going to the D-rank gate, Beom I-ryeong sat at my house’s dining table and maintained his weapons—a spear and bow.

“I only want to handle the bow.”

“I told you. Your physical abilities are too good to waste on just archery.”

Beom I-ryeong had exceptional stat efficiency that increased just from basic physical training. You could call him naturally gifted with muscles that developed well even with the same exercise.

“I only shoot arrows because my body can’t keep up. It’s a waste for you to do that.”

He has a body that’s perfectly opposite to mine. The joke was a bonus.

“…Then you should just abandon that body.”

What? I turned around and asked, but Beom I-ryeong didn’t answer.

* * *

As soon as Ja Hyeong-woo heard that Gyeon Gang-hyeon was going to assist Beom I-ryeong’s gate practice, he came running.

He would have destroyed that D-rank gate preemptively if he could, but he had absolutely no intention of letting Gyeon Gang-hyeon go to a gate ‘alone.’

When he rushed to the place, Beom I-ryeong was there. Beom I-ryeong was gripping Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s hand and looking up at him.

With expectant eyes.

“You two are going together?”

Ja Hyeong-woo’s steps gradually slowed. He stopped and listened to Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s answer.

“Mm, no. I’m not entirely reliable, so I’ll take a healer along.”

It’s visible even from here. Beom I-ryeong’s complexion instantly turning bad.

The small face slowly turned. Beom I-ryeong’s eyes swept over the large man who could probably crush someone his small size in one go.

Blatantly revealed hostility.

Ja Hyeong-woo recalled people around Gyeon Gang-hyeon one by one, building foundation for his thoughts.

Me or Jin Seol-woon.

We had the same impure hearts. Therefore, we saw each other as eyesores, but didn’t touch each other. Either way, we were both allies helpful to Gang-hyeon.

Won Ha-ri or Hae Mi-jun.

They also followed Gang-hyeon. In this case, it was admiration and attachment. They burden Gang-hyeon with responsibility, but simultaneously these people also give something back to Gang-hyeon, so it’s a mutually beneficial relationship.

But Beom I-ryeong…

Throughout the gate practice, Ja Hyeong-woo watched Gyeon Gang-hyeon, who was so focused on Beom I-ryeong that he didn’t spare him a single glance. Along with Beom I-ryeong, who stubbornly held Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s hand.

‘I can’t see any emotions from Beom I-ryeong.’

He just mechanically, as if he must do so, blatantly follows only Gyeon Gang-hyeon.

Ja Hyeong-woo had an ominous premonition.

If left like this, he seemed like he would grow in the wrong direction and bind Gang-hyeon to take him away.

However, it’s also true that Beom I-ryeong is a ‘Tiger’ successor recognized by the system.

After returning from the gate, Ja Hyeong-woo was lost in deep thought all day. Even until bedtime after finishing his daily routine.

Sharp suspicion soon collided with fundamental concepts.

‘…What is this thing called the system? Can we trust what the system says in the first place?’

It might be that he’s taking a defensive attitude because he’s not good with computers and other electronic devices. But this thing called the system… isn’t it somewhat different from ordinary computers?

It’s closer to being an entity rather than a tool—can we completely trust such a thing?

How can we guarantee it won’t commit evil acts or be used as a means for evil?

Ja Hyeong-woo suddenly thought he should go to Gyeon Gang-hyeon. This time, whether he got severely cursed at or hit with an arrow, he felt he could only be at ease after confirming Gang-hyeon was sleeping well.

He raised his body from the bed. He planned to go directly to Gyeon Gang-hyeon wearing just a simple durumagi over his t-shirt and pants. He crossed the room and reached for the hanger.

Then, a strange tinnitus burrowed into his ears.

An eerie tension coagulated in the stagnant air of the room. Ja Hyeong-woo stopped moving. Someone was standing behind his stiffly frozen body.

In the sticky airflow that seemed touchable by texture, Ja Hyeong-woo picked out chilling murderous intent.

“…Who is it?”

Before being Hunter School, this place is a different dimension not defined by Earth’s rules—inside a gate.

As a healer, he couldn’t guarantee whether he could prevent something from trying to kill him.

“Disappear from beside Cheon Gang-hyeon.”

A clear voice.

The moment he sensed danger, Ja Hyeong-woo immediately used his power. Blue light protected his vital points—heart and neck.

A cold spear tip precisely stabbed the spot outside the blue light, beside his ribs.

Ja Hyeong-woo wasn’t surprised. He had already half-predicted the opponent would come at him like this.

However, when the spear stabbed deep, his arm instantly lost agility.

The sensation of metal penetrating his body was too vivid. Pain that was hard for even him to endure tore through his left side.

Ja Hyeong-woo gripped the spear shaft tightly while enduring the pain.

He planned to break the shaft to separate himself from the spear handle the opponent held. However, the spear didn’t break even with his strength.

A weapon, and S-grade at that.

A weapon created by a newly awakened successor who had mastered even weapon crafting with extraordinary growth speed.

The opponent possessed even greater monstrous strength. Beom I-ryeong twisted the spear while it was embedded. The sound of something cracking spread from inside his body.

“Urgh—”

Terrible pain that could make him lose consciousness struck. His knees felt like they’d buckle at any moment.

‘Request for backup…’

Ja Hyeong-woo didn’t even know how to get through this crisis, maintaining only the vivid will to endure.

His judgment remained unclouded. Therefore, he didn’t move the advanced heal he’d activated for his heart and neck to his injury site.

Because that’s exactly what the opponent wanted.

If his heart or nape became unprotected, the opponent would immediately target those spots.

Ja Hyeong-woo gritted his teeth and looked down at the spear that had stabbed him.

Beom I-ryeong took out another spear from his inventory. With a cruel smile, he tried to skewer the large prey with plenty of places to stab once more with his spear.

{Woof!}

If a translucent brown animal hadn’t burst out from the doorway, that attempt probably would have succeeded.

The dog familiar bit into Beom I-ryeong’s arm with sharp teeth and held on. The teeth were imbued with strong earth attribute skills. Beom I-ryeong’s arm was instantly stained with blood.

Thwack!

Simultaneously with a pure white figure appearing in the doorway, an arrow barely grazed Ja Hyeong-woo’s body and pierced through Beom I-ryeong’s neck.

“Huff, ha. This fucking bastard.”

Gyeon Gang-hyeon nocked another arrow to his bowstring. The arrowhead aimed at Beom I-ryeong’s neck.

‘Ah.’

Strength left Ja Hyeong-woo’s legs. Even though the spear was still vertically embedded in his side, he suddenly felt the pain lessening.

He knelt with a thud. Ja Hyeong-woo, who barely avoided collapsing, pulled out the spear with his freed hand and began treatment.

The dog familiar immediately returned to Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s side once Ja Hyeong-woo escaped from Beom I-ryeong’s hands.

“Ja Hyeong-woo, you… move to the 1st floor bedroom from now on.”

To grumble about being out of breath just from the 2nd floor stairs while fighting Beom I-ryeong on equal terms. Ja Hyeong-woo knew it didn’t fit the situation, but somehow he felt like laughing.

“Then should we just live in the same house? So this kind of thing doesn’t happen again.”

“Just shut your mouth. Is this the time to talk back?”

Beom I-ryeong looked at Gyeon Gang-hyeon nonchalantly even with an arrow embedded in him.

Wood attribute’s characteristic vitality. Though he couldn’t heal his body since he wasn’t a healer, he had the skill to distance himself from death.

“You’re late. I came slowly on purpose so you’d chase after me. I almost killed this person while waiting.”

Gyeon Gang-hyeon snorted.

“Hey, that guy doesn’t die from a little stabbing.”

Ja Hyeong-woo was rather relieved by those words.

Whether he was hurt from being stabbed or not, even if Gang-hyeon didn’t care, he was okay. It would have been harder if Gang-hyeon had collapsed from seeing his blood.

But with Beom I-ryeong’s next words, he felt distant again as if driven to a cliff’s edge.

“His heart is pounding wildly but only his mouth is alive.”

Gyeon Gang-hyeon stared at Beom I-ryeong without any reaction. The empowered arrow shaft vibrated considerably.

A hole was punched through Ja Hyeong-woo’s heart as he watched this. The brief relief he’d felt dried up mercilessly.

He remembered what Gyeon Gang-hyeon had said while dying in his arms underground.

“I’ll save you even if I have to trade myself for it.”

Even hearing such words from Gang-hyeon, he wasn’t moved at all. Rather, he was scared.

Ja Hyeong-woo felt fear now.
