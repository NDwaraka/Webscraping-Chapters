With that pathetic face, either not knowing he’d touched a taboo or knowing but acting clueless.

With his torso and chin completely fixed, only blade-like light planted in his black, venomous pupils.

Everyone at this table could tell that Woo Sae-geon had just dominated the table with his presence alone.

This wasn’t just about dropping some reckless spirit trying to climb onto the table back to the floor. It was pressure that could send them plummeting off a cliff and still have strength left over.

How can someone just two years older than me have that kind of intimidating presence?

Only now am I certain. Woo Sae-geon isn’t afraid or puzzled about being Korea’s number one hunter.

Behind Bootsman’s sigh, when no one could bring themselves to speak—

There was one person who unfailingly stepped forward.

“「Our leader is shy around strangers. Isn’t it cute」?”

“「Uh… yeah…」.”

Agni, who had been frozen stiff, trembled his eyes and agreed with difficulty.

Woo Sae-geon didn’t stop Sa Ye-ho at all. He withdrew his gaze, letting the snow mountain-like atmosphere at the table melt away.

‘…Are those two having a one-on-one chat right now?’

Now I think I understand. Why Woo Sae-geon always lets Sa Ye-ho be.

Even in what seems like Sa Ye-ho being the only one who can manipulate Woo Sae-geon, Woo Sae-geon’s intentions have always existed.

For the first time, I’m making a judgment about the relationship between these two characters. It’s a hypothesis about content that didn’t appear in the original work.

Woo Sae-geon and Sa Ye-ho are partners.

As the meal reached its final stages, the original tension gradually returned. It was because they remembered that in a moment, they’d have to cross swords instead of water glasses with the opponents in front of them.

“「The match rules might be too disadvantageous for the Korean side」.”

“Ah, we’ll know once we try, right? Some of us barely use familiars, or don’t use them at all.”

The first part refers to me, the latter to Ja Hyeong-woo. Someone who supposedly doesn’t have one.

Surprisingly, even Woo Sae-geon still doesn’t know that Ja Hyeong-woo has manifested a familiar.

I don’t know how Ja Hyeong-woo plans to handle the aftermath of this either.

‘I need to somehow induce changes in my body to become independent from the mouse familiar.’

I’ll definitely accomplish it and return the mouse familiar to Ja Hyeong-woo before the latter half of the original story.

I glanced sideways at Ja Hyeong-woo, whose eating pace had become vigorous since Agni quieted down. Fortunately, our eyes didn’t meet this time either.

‘Even if Ja Hyeong-woo faces some crisis…’

I know he’ll think it’s right for his familiar to protect me.

The problem is that I know this too well.

Ja Hyeong-woo was already uncomfortable, and now I felt even more awkward. My heart pounded anxiously inside my ribcage.

I set down my utensils with a clatter.

“Eat more.”

Ja Hyeong-woo’s voice suddenly pierced my right ear. All the fine hairs on my body stood up.

It was the first words Ja Hyeong-woo had said to me today.

And I demonstrated the stamina to not say a single word until the end.

“…Really, what’s wrong with you.”
