I steadied my trembling legs from the height and waited for monsters to appear.

“The terrain is beyond imagination, isn’t it?”

“Right. It’s dizzying.”

“This is something I’ve only seen in games…”

It was terrain that defied common sense. Several large stone islands were floating in mid-air, and we were standing on those stones. Even the stage entrance itself was created in mid-air.

Setting aside how these islands stayed afloat, it was spectacular how waterfalls were formed from the large lakes on each island, flowing endlessly down to the bottomless depths below.

I tensed my neck to avoid unconsciously looking down at the cliff bottom that looked pitch black and made me dizzy.

Meanwhile, Sa Ye-ho was clinging to the edge of the island, sticking his head out boldly over the gaping cliff and peering around.

“What would happen if we fell down there?”

“Why are you even thinking about falling?”

Just imagining it made my liver flutter, so I crossed my arms as naturally as possible.

I had no idea why Sa Ye-ho even entered this stage. The monsters here are water attribute.

What was Woo Sae-geon thinking bringing him instead of Yang Pyo who’s earth attribute?

I don’t know if Sa Ye-ho threw a tantrum to come or what, but looking at the stage situation, it was surprisingly a good choice. I lightly chewed my lower lip and opened the chat window.

==

Me: Doesn’t it seem like the stage is also predicting and sniping us?

==

If I said such ominous words out loud and it got filmed completely, it would only stimulate people’s anxiety for no reason.

If we’re going to be anxious, it’s better if only we’re anxious. We’re the ones fighting anyway.

Me: Since the monsters use water attributes, we’ll mainly come with earth. It seems like they knew this and completely removed earth from the terrain

Snake Successor: You’re right

Of all times, in Woo Sae-geon’s last stage before retirement…

‘Should I be glad that we’re experiencing this for the first time while Woo Sae-geon is still here, rather than after he’s gone?’

Hae Mi-jun, who was biting his lips, stuck closer to my side.

More than anything else, I trembled slightly because I thought the floating island had just swayed a bit. I pretended to be calm and thought carefully.

‘If stages come out this way… we should also consider returning to the balanced composition Woo Sae-geon used in the early days.’

But this should be something Ja Hyeong-woo thinks about, not me. As the next leader.

I glanced at Ja Hyeong-woo behind us, scanning the surroundings for monsters with an expressionless face.

‘Honestly, Ja Hyeong-woo isn’t particularly clever…’

Until now I thought Ja Hyeong-woo would just do well and let it pass, but I haven’t actually seen the latter part of the original work directly either. Moreover, this guy is different from the original. The current Ja Hyeong-woo is mentally at ease because Gyeon Gang-hyeon didn’t die, but in exchange he’s quite dull.

Would Ja Hyeong-woo be able to command battles well?

Even while I was having all these random thoughts, Woo Sae-geon remained silent. Since the leader who should have given instructions long ago was quiet, I ended up taking charge and calling out first.

“Leader. Leader?”

Woo Sae-geon lifted his head slightly. He glanced at me once then put on a thoughtful expression again.

Ignoring others’ words was the same as usual, but if something looked a little different, was it just my imagination?

Today’s Woo Sae-geon seemed a bit dazed.

“…The terrain is outside expected parameters. Be careful using earth skills. There’s a possibility the entire island could crash. I’m restricting the use of earth attribute skills I’m mentioning now.”

See? That’s exactly what I said earlier, but now he’s saying it as if his situational comprehension is delayed even after hearing it.

Woo Sae-geon’s eyes still looked sunken somewhere. Definitely, the recent disgrace he’d been covered with had dealt him a blow.

If he’s sluggish here, people will think ‘he’s flustered,’ and that becomes humiliating for Woo Sae-geon. The vicious cycle will repeat.

…Does that person also get conditioning problems when under a lot of stress?

I suspect so.

Since he’s someone whose mental strength stat was particularly low.

Among the skills Woo Sae-geon listed intermittently was my skill <Lonely Gravedigger>. The result of my abilities being almost completely excavated by Woo Sae-geon despite my constant efforts.

At that moment, another floating island rose up in front of the island we were on, attaching itself like a ship docking at a port.

Everyone was dismayed seeing the huge life form riding on that island.

‘A boss appears right from the start? Or does this stage have no small fry to begin with?’

This monster was the myodusa, a legendary animal known to possess water powers.

A very large snake with a cat’s head.

“Hey, Ye-ho. You have a lot of kinfolk. Like that four-headed insect before.”

The myodusa looked down at us one by one with that uniquely eerie and persistent gaze like a wild cat encountered at night.

Then blue smoke suddenly spewed from its body.

“Ugh!”

Jin Seol-woon, who was at the front, let out a short groan and pulled his body back. Jin Seol-woon, who had quickly increased the distance, was furrowing his brow as if in pain.

Jin Seol-woon’s arm had lightly touched the blue smoke. As soon as it touched that smoke, opaque steam rose up as if it was melting.

Jin Seol-woon’s clothes were melting.

Fortunately, since he pulled back immediately, only the sleeve of his clothes melted and his skin seemed unharmed. The damage was limited to slightly reddened bare skin being exposed.

“We can’t approach like that. What should we do? Leader?”

But the only sound Woo Sae-geon made was to not get close to the myodusa and maintain a certain distance. Since the myodusa doesn’t spew smoke beyond a certain range.

I felt not just bewildered, but even betrayed. I stared at Woo Sae-geon’s pale face as he narrowed his eyes.

‘Is he really in a panic state? Then what are we supposed to do?’

Even Sa Ye-ho, the next most reliable person, seemed unable to think straight as his attention kept going to Woo Sae-geon. He kept glancing at Woo Sae-geon with upset eyes, just frowning.

That’s when my eyes met Jin Seol-woon’s.

‘Why are you asking me that…?’

But Jin Seol-woon strangely, as if he’d been looking only at me from the beginning without looking at Woo Sae-geon… very naturally demanded an answer from me.

==

Dragon Successor>Me: What do you think would be good?

Damn. This cursed special vision is to blame.

I eventually acknowledged that the responsibility to find a solution after Woo Sae-geon fell on me and glared at the myodusa.

The myodusa is a legendary animal said to emit blue water energy containing healing power.

However, it seems that energy acts as deadly poison to humans.

‘Then should Hae Mi-jun use detox skills? No. The myodusa emits energy every moment without any restrictions, but Hae Mi-jun can’t continuously supply skills to six people including himself.’

Then what should we do?

There wasn’t much time, but this is exactly when you need to think calmly. I have to dig in from the essence. There must be a way.

The poisonous energy the myodusa used was basically in the form of thick fog. But when it stuck to Jin Seol-woon earlier, the flow changed as if coating Jin Seol-woon’s clothes and skin with a membrane… Huh?

I had seen an ability with a similar pattern somewhere before. I frantically racked my brain at the sense of déjà vu. What was it? What was it similar to?

‘Ah.’

As if suddenly pulling up a memory, I quickly turned to look at someone.

It was Ja Hyeong-woo, who was waiting for my words.

“Ja Hyeong-woo, take out the weakest of your curse skills.”

Immediately, bundled bells appeared in Ja Hyeong-woo’s hands. It was Ja Hyeong-woo’s attack skill following talismans and gourds. Was it a curse that made all senses more sensitive?

‘You want to use a curse on monsters in this situation?’ He might find it puzzling, but Ja Hyeong-woo was silent. I briefly found it strange that Ja Hyeong-woo listened to my words without questioning or arguing, but soon forgot.

“Use the curse skill on us.”

Before uttering my next words, I looked into everyone’s eyes one by one. Reluctantly, but including Woo Sae-geon too.

“From what I can see, the attack the myodusa uses is also classified as a curse skill. The energy looked exactly the same. But debuff skills can’t stack others once one is applied. So let’s experiment first, and if it’s as I think, let’s fight while covered in curse skills.”

The test subject was Woo Sae-geon, who though temporarily silent, always had a solid body. While Ja Hyeong-woo was going over there, I quickly reorganized the formation.

“Leader, move around freely like a midfielder and use your strength. That seems like it would be most helpful. Everyone else just fight while maintaining your positions. Be careful of the floor.”

Maybe because they’re used to taking orders due to Woo Sae-geon, these grown guys earnestly listen to even my shoddy briefing.

“Jin Seol-woon, I know you’ve been practicing attacks, but go back to being main tanker just for today. Protect me and Ja Hyeong-woo.”

“Understood.”

“Sa Ye-ho, your attribute directly opposes the myodusa’s, so stay far away for now. Why did you even come here? No, this isn’t the time for such talk. Still, among your skills there should be something that would work on the myodusa. Let’s use that.”

“Okay, sounds good.”

I sent Ja Hyeong-woo as sub-healer to maintain debuff skills. Even if healing was somewhat insufficient, I had to switch Hae Mi-jun to main healer instead of Ja Hyeong-woo.

“Hae Mi-jun, save enough to use detox skills once per person after everything’s over!”

“Yes!”
