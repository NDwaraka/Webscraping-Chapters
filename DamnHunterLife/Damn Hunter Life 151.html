Damn Hunter Life Chapter 151

‘Don’t know. At least I got a healer.’

Assuming I’d be the tanker myself… then it was time to find a dealer.

I thought of Sa Ye-ho, but since I didn’t know where he was, I used the communication system.

[“Eww, I don’t want to. I’ve been seeing too much monster blood lately. Sae-geon hyung isn’t around either.”]

But this damn bastard had another bout of his lazy disease. Just as I was suppressing my bubbling anger and wondering how to curse him out.

[“Who’s going?”]

“Ja Hyeong-woo.”

[“Mm, got it. How generous of you! I’ll go.”]

What the hell. I didn’t do anything and he changed his tune on his own.

Anyway, both Sa Ye-ho and I are poorly balanced, so it seemed like we’d need to bring one more person. Just in case.

‘If it’s a regular gate, we can get reinforcements later… but that would be embarrassing to watch.’

Therefore, we had to go in once, crush everything decisively, and come out. I’m serious about this.

‘Should I just bring Yang Pyo to steamroll everything? No wait, Yang Pyo needs to save his skills. And that bastard gets mentally weak if he uses them consecutively.’

Jin Seol-woon too… doesn’t have abundant skills. And his dragon familiar was particularly overworked in the last stage. It would be better for him to rest for now.

The moment I thought that, an uncomfortable feeling welled up in a corner of my heart.

I leaned my head back against the backrest and thought about whether the judgment I’d just made was truly objective.

‘It doesn’t seem like a judgment I made while deceiving myself because I wanted to avoid Jin Seol-woon. I looked at it as a leader.’

Then among the remaining successors, someone worth bringing would be…

==

Me>Rabbit Successor

==

I brought up the private chat and briefly pondered what to say. I must always stay alert and be careful with every word.

==

Me>Rabbit Successor: Myo Cho-hui Successor

Me>Rabbit Successor: I’ll request support for Cheonan Gate.

Me>Rabbit Successor: Me, Ja Hyeong-woo, and Sa Ye-ho will go.

Rabbit Successor>Me: Got it, call me later

==

Whew, she listens well. I secretly breathed a sigh of relief.

When I became leader, what I feared most was precisely Myo Cho-hui.

‘Could Myo Cho-hui really tolerate having me, not even Yoon Sae-geon, above her?’

So to win Myo Cho-hui’s favor, as soon as I became leader, I started tinkering with the system.

The function I messed with was the status window video call feature that could connect to family members’ and acquaintances’ phones.

I pestered the system to upgrade this video call function. I improved the video quality to high definition and extended the duration.

Even though it was a privilege given to all hunters, Myo Cho-hui was someone who would give back proportionally when she received what she needed. You could say her calculations were precise.

‘I did wonder why Woo Sae-geon never did things like this when the system made it possible…’

Maybe this too was a kind of preferential treatment the system showed only to me…?

After sending word that we’d depart for the gate in 10 minutes, I stared intently at the status window. Again, my lips moved involuntarily.

“Do you want something from me?”

I had no expectation of an answer, but.

At that moment, the status window blinked and displayed text.

==

/Please hurry with gate support.

==

“Ah shit, I’m going, I’m going.”

Thus began a dangerous journey with me and Ja Hyeong-woo at the front (I didn’t want to be side by side with this bastard like a pair), and Sa Ye-ho and Myo Cho-hui behind us.

It was an outing after a long time. Ten regular hunters were gathered at the gate entrance. They were skilled students I’d manually selected from the list I got by having the system do rematching.

“It’s Gyeon Gang-hyeon-nim.”

“Whoa, he’s coming in person…”

Since my senses had become uselessly sharp after awakening, I could hear all the students’ whispered conversations. As an awakened one, my five senses were somewhat better than ordinary people’s.

Sa Ye-ho asked, “Should I speak for you?” but I shook my head slightly.

When I stepped forward, the students instantly fell silent. The hem of my black durumagi fluttered in the cool breeze.

“Who’s your leader right now? Come out.”

“That’s me.”

A big guy emerged from among the regular hunters.

Regular hunters also had to form teams when entering gates, so during hunter education classes, there were people who chose and learned the ‘leader’ position. If I was the supreme commander, they’d be like deputy commanders.

“I’m under Ma Cheon-ah-nim of the fire attribute. My name is…”

After hearing just his name, I waved my hand to indicate he didn’t need to explain further. Instead, I spoke.

“You’re left-handed. You usually hold your sword with just that hand. The kids are usually accustomed to right-handed movement patterns, so be careful of collisions. And your right ankle—it seems like you overdid it during training, so don’t let your energy leak toward that side. Well, I guess you’d know better than me even if I didn’t explain all day…”

As I rambled about what I could see, I suddenly looked up.

All the regular students were staring at me with sparkling eyes.

“Why… why.”

“How did you know all that?”

The student in front of me asked loudly, as if amazed. I felt a bit awkward and tilted my chin diagonally. In my embarrassment, a sharp tone slipped out.

“I can see it through the flow of qi. Don’t you know that?”

Looking with my eyes, I could tell how this student had been training their fire attribute until now.

The red energy more concentrated in the left arm than the right. Moreover, by looking at how the energy bounced, I could gauge what weapon they used.

I could naturally also tell what trajectory and patterns they used to swing and defend with their sword. Following the grain of the flowing flames, a hazy image would emerge as if this student was actually demonstrating the swings in front of me.

It wasn’t like I was some master from a martial arts novel, and I still felt uncomfortable with this version of myself.

“Wow… that gave me chills just now.”

“I’ve heard about seeing through everything, having X-ray vision and such, but this is the first time I’ve seen it directly.”

Listening to the students exclaiming one by one, a strange feeling crept up along my skin.

I still remember it vividly.

On the first day I realized I’d possessed someone’s body, the praise from students pouring toward Ja Hyeong-woo as he passed by me in the cafeteria.

Back then, I was quietly eating my rice while listening to the harsh gossip stabbing into my back.

The words referring to me then were consistent. Scoundrel, trash, incompetent. Even the exhausted Ja Hyeong-woo had said to me:

“The current you would die instantly just from being grazed by a monster’s claw. Keep living pathetically like that. Reeking of alcohol.”

But now.

The students look up to me. The admiration they had for Ja Hyeong-woo is now directed at me.

And despite having his praise stolen away, Ja Hyeong-woo himself…

Ja Hyeong-woo is staring at me with his mouth agape like an idiot. As if he’s the one hearing the compliments meant for me.

Even when our eyes meet, far from being embarrassed, he smiles.

‘…Don’t look at me smiling in front of others!’

Ah shit, whatever. I’ll curse Ja Hyeong-woo out thoroughly tonight—. While I was at it, I went on talking, mentioning each of the other students one by one.

“The tall wood attribute kid over there should pair temporarily with this one. I think your combat styles will match well.”

“Yes!”

Somehow my insides were stirring. It wasn’t a bad feeling. Various emotions were tangled together, but still, one thing among my current feelings I could clearly explain.

‘The feeling that I don’t want to disappoint anyone anymore.’

For the first time since becoming leader, I was proud to be a leader.

The students behind me were expecting my performance.

Actually, among them, there were probably people mixed in who had opposed me becoming leader. According to the numbers written then, probably with a 60% probability.

‘So I need to show them a proper performance.’

My dog familiar sprang out from me. Immediately taking up my bow, I launched a preemptive attack against the monster horde.

[<Lonely Gravedigger (Earth Attribute)> is activated.]

After the surrounding mobs were cleared, I changed tactics.

“Myo Cho-hui Successor, please scout for the boss. Sa Ye-ho, you guard her. And command the students too.”

“Okie. But what about you?”

“I’m going to scout the perimeter and scrape up some magic stones.”

Having personally witnessed the power of my tanker skill and dog familiar, the students didn’t particularly object to me splitting off alone. Only Sa Ye-ho looked at me with suspicious eyes.

Ja Hyeong-woo naturally followed behind me.

I continued advancing through the forest. Until I was far enough away from the students.

When we entered a dim cave, Ja Hyeong-woo finally seemed puzzled and grabbed my shoulder.

I ignored him and pressed my hand against the wall. Only then could I squeeze my eyes shut. I’d been having dizzy spells that made my vision sway since earlier.

‘Ah shit…’

I really overdid it!

I’m from a delinquent background, so I thought feelings like wanting to show off in front of kids had long since disappeared.

Belated panting breath leaked out. After calling my doggy and using skills, my body was worn out.

“Gang-hyeon.”

Ja Hyeong-woo, sensing something was seriously wrong, lowered his voice and tried to turn my body around. I glared at him.

“Didn’t you put in enough yang energy?”

“Ah… sorry. Really—”

“Idiot, the gate itself was an unexpected situation. Can’t you even make that much of an excuse?”

Ja Hyeong-woo was briefly confused and couldn’t grasp the context.

I sighed deeply, as if the ground would cave in. Soon I looked up at the damn bastard and gave clear orders.

“Heal me now.”
