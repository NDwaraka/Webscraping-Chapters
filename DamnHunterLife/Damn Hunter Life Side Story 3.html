Damn Hunter Life Side Story Chapter 3

* * *

[“You were already a pervert, and now you’ve developed voyeurism too.”]

One month after the two separated, this was what Hyeong-woo finally got to hear from Cheon Gang-hyeon.

And rightfully so, because Hyeong-woo wouldn’t hang up the video call, making all sorts of pleas and requests saying he was dying to see him.

Thanks to that, Hyeong-woo could satisfyingly observe Cheon Gang-hyeon’s daily life.

Cheon Gang-hyeon, who woke up drowsily at 9 AM, first turned on the video call. He had no choice because he had fallen for yesterday’s coaxing and made a promise.

Under Hyeong-woo’s gaze, Cheon Gang-hyeon held his phone and shuffled to the bathroom. He set the phone behind the sink and brushed his teeth. He kept giving looks to Hyeong-woo on the screen that said ‘You’re really watching this much?’

Hyeong-woo watched Gang-hyeon put on a headband to wash his face, spread jam on bread at the dining table in his pajamas, and after eating breakfast, lie on the sofa for a moment scratching his belly while turning on the TV.

[“Stop watching, you crazy bastard!”]

In return, he got thoroughly cursed at.

“I’m worried you’ll become lethargic when you’re alone. When I’m watching, you at least move around. I’m watching so you’ll develop regular habits.”

There was some logic to those words. It was quite reasonable, and Hyeong-woo certainly had such feelings.

However, Cheon Gang-hyeon knew Hyeong-woo better than before.

[“You’re the kind of bastard who takes care of your own desires while pretending to look after me. Must be nice being so efficient, huh?”]

Hyeong-woo smiled sheepishly without any sign of rebuttal or denial. The angry Cheon Gang-hyeon gave the middle finger to the phone screen.

He already knew all of Cheon Gang-hyeon’s big and small habits, but he wanted to know even the most trivial things.

“When I retire, let’s live together.”

How he folded clothes, where he placed things. What he cared about in the house and what he somewhat neglected. He even carefully observed things like bedding that was never organized.

“You watch me too. Tomorrow I’ll do it, so you watch.”

[“Your pervert skills really develop day by day.”]

He was preparing so that when the two people who lived separately moved into one house, they could quickly adapt to each other without trial and error.

[“When did I say I’d live with you!”]

“It’s my life’s wish.”

When Cheon Gang-hyeon pretended to ignore him, Hyeong-woo’s voice gradually changed to a more desperately pleading tone.

Cheon Gang-hyeon shut his mouth. After a moment of silence, he spoke in a much smaller voice than before.

[“In the old days maybe, but now I’ve lost all my temper—do you still want to live together even like this?”]

Still, he was prone to getting irritated with Hyeong-woo. He did things to him that he didn’t do to other people.

Hyeong-woo let out a small laugh.

“Now it doesn’t feel like you’re being irritated. You’re just being affectionate.”

[“Even when I really try not to curse at you…”]

Cheon Gang-hyeon was truly disgusted every single day. Hyeong-woo had come to enjoy that.

Winter at age 21, when he had just failed Hunter School, came to mind. Hyeong-woo carefully recalled that time.

Overnight, the shaman’s house where he lived lost its worker. No matter how much of a shaman one was, they couldn’t go against the gate system, so his guardian would have to find another errand runner.

‘I won’t have to do shrine work anymore.’

The compensation for working as a successor was good. It was an amount that would let Hyeong-woo not work for the rest of his life.

He could pay his guardian back for all the food and care received. Then he wouldn’t need to do shrine work anymore.

‘Then what should I do with my life?’

When he retired, he should cut off this uncomfortable long hair that the shaman elder had made him grow. Besides that, he couldn’t think of anything he wanted to do.

Back then, Hyeong-woo thought his life was frustrating with no hobbies or interests.

‘But now I don’t need to feel frustrated. I have things I want to do now. So many.’

The moment Cheon Gang-hyeon appeared in his life again, Hyeong-woo was reborn.

He lay in bed before sleeping and told Cheon Gang-hyeon this story. Gang-hyeon seemed a bit sleepy but listened.

“I want to live in a house with a yard. I want to grow lettuce, herbs, and tomatoes in the garden.”

[“All food.”]

“And you have to live in that house with me too.”

Cheon Gang-hyeon fell silent for a moment as if at a loss for words.

[“Don’t you want to do anything other than gardening?”]

“I want to learn cooking.”

[“Yeah, make your own food to stuff your face with.”]

“And you have to be sitting at the dining table.”

The conversation kept going in circles. Cheon Gang-hyeon thought for a bit, then asked hesitantly in a small voice.

[“What happens if I’m not home?”]

Some silence occurred.

Hyeong-woo answered calmly, as if opening his chest and taking out his heart to look at it.

“If you’re not there… I think I won’t want to do anything. I might not be able to feel interest in anything.”

Though he made excuses whenever he got the chance, Hyeong-woo knew that Cheon Gang-hyeon’s attitude was gradually softening.

Or perhaps he couldn’t overcome Hyeong-woo’s persistence and had half-given up, thinking ‘This is my life, what can I do about it.’

One day, while the call was connected and they were each doing their own things without much conversation, Cheon Gang-hyeon suddenly muttered.

[“When you retire too…”]

Hyeong-woo held his breath and waited for his next words.

[“Who’s going to heal the kids?”]

…It wasn’t exactly something Hyeong-woo would be happy to hear.

[“But Hae Mi-jun has really grown a lot. He can do your part.”]

Cheon Gang-hyeon probably noticed too, but pretended not to know and kept chattering.

Like that, half a year passed.

Throughout that half year, the calls continued. Hyeong-woo often went overseas for support with other successors, and Cheon Gang-hyeon got his driver’s license. Hyeong-woo taught Hae Mi-jun all the healing techniques he knew, and Cheon Gang-hyeon passed his high school equivalency exam.

When Hyeong-woo said he would contract a rural house suitable for the two of them through his manager, Cheon Gang-hyeon didn’t stop him.

* * *

One year passed painfully slowly.

But from today, even that painful time could be made into memories.

Han Hyeong-woo, who had returned his hwarang clothes and put on thick padding and fleece pants, hurriedly got in the car without even tying his hair.

Sitting in the back seat, he repeatedly read the text Cheon Gang-hyeon had sent.

[I’m at the library]

[Drop off your stuff and come pick me up or don’t]

From today, he would live with Gang-hyeon.

After persistent pleading, Han Hyeong-woo had succeeded in preventing Cheon Gang-hyeon from renewing the contract on his original officetel.

Which meant…

Han Hyeong-woo tried to calm his pounding heart. He barely managed to lower the corners of his mouth that seemed ready to reach the top of his head.

He planned to roughly drop off the moving boxes from Han Hyeong-woo’s family home at the new house and immediately go pick up Cheon Gang-hyeon.

It had been a year. After a year, he could touch and embrace the real thing. His mouth was completely dry.

The rural house on the outskirts of Seoul was already all neatly organized. As soon as Han Hyeong-woo opened the front gate and looked inside, he suddenly felt his heart settle.

‘This is my home.’

It was ‘my home’ that he had never even imagined. The well-trimmed grass was lush and green. The small house painted neatly in white looked incredibly cozy. Han Hyeong-woo unconsciously stopped and stared at his new home.

Then he suddenly came to his senses.

‘Let me hurry and pick up Gang-hyeon first.’

The most important thing for this house to be perfect was missing. Han Hyeong-woo ran with the intention of throwing down his luggage as soon as he opened the front door and going right back out.

But the door opened from inside first.

“Hey!”

At the loud voice, Han Hyeong-woo stopped right there.

Gang-hyeon’s face, bursting out as if he had been waiting inside the house, colorfully filled his vision.

Cheon Gang-hyeon hugged him tightly with both arms. The luggage fell with a thud from Han Hyeong-woo’s hands.

Just yesterday he had been grumbling that he wasn’t coming, but he had already moved all his stuff here and was waiting for Han Hyeong-woo. The talk about being at the library was all a lie too.

“You’re warm.”

Cheon Gang-hyeon whispered in his ear with an indifferent tone.

The sweet warmth that clung to him felt like a dream. Han Hyeong-woo stood there dazed with his mouth open, while extending both arms to embrace him back.

After staying like that for a while, Cheon Gang-hyeon was the first to unwrap his arms from around his neck.

Suddenly embarrassed, he stood awkwardly at a distance. Not making eye contact and turning his back was a bonus.

“Come in and look… around the house.”

He entered the house with quick steps, but Han Hyeong-woo had taken off his shoes and wasn’t following him. Cheon Gang-hyeon looked back with a puzzled face.

The guy was just stretching out his arm from where he stood, as if asking him to hold his hand.

Cheon Gang-hyeon looked annoyed and snatched that hand as if stealing it. The smile on Han Hyeong-woo’s face deepened.

But even after holding hands, he followed for a few steps then stopped right in the living room.

“Ah, why again?”

“Let’s sit and rest for a bit. Come here.”
