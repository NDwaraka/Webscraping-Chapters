Damn Hunter Life Chapter 135

When explaining recent music trends, his eyes sparkle, but Jin Seol-woon never brings up entertainment industry news or anything like that. He seems to dislike gossiping.

“We’re planning to make one of the album tracks Christmas carol-style to match Christmas.”

Today’s Jin Seol-woon is spoiling Innate’s next album while placing fish meat on top of my rice.

Even though they’re making a comeback without him, he doesn’t seem particularly shaken by it now.

It was a welcome change, but I was actually getting a bit tired of listening. I figured Jin Seol-woon wouldn’t be hurt if I obviously changed the topic, so I casually cut him off.

“Hey, let’s talk about training instead. Isn’t it hard after taking on Woo Sae-geon’s affiliated hunters? How was your condition during morning training today?”

“Hmm… the serratus anterior above my waist has become more defined. Want to see? As leader, you should check on this kind of progress too.”

“Do I need to see that? Did you show Woo Sae-geon too?”

Then Jin Seol-woon, for some reason, briefly averted his eyes as if at a loss for words.

“…More than that, Gang-hyeon-ssi, you’re short on points right now?”

“Yeah. This is all because they got a delinquent like me as their person in charge…”

“You could just collect from the successors.”

“That’s a good idea… not.”

What kind of corrupt official-like talk is this guy casually spouting?

I stared at him for quite a while in disbelief, but far from avoiding my gaze, Jin Seol-woon stared right back at me. In the end, Jin Seol-woon, who made me blink first, leaned his upper body back and shrugged.

“Try asking once. Everyone will want to give.”

I don’t know what this guy knows to be so confident…

“Then you give first. Do you have some money?”

“I do. What I earned from training.”

The leader position isn’t bad either. Getting respect from a top idol and all.

* * *

Korean Hunter School, maintaining its daily routine as if something had changed yet not really, after the school’s head changed.

Here is a fifteen-year-old rookie hunter whose life has unwillingly shot up.

“Hey wow, that’s him.”

“The kid walking by right now?”

Every time Kwak Ji-hong went to and from the public areas for training attendance, he found it hard to handle the attention subtly focused on him. His face had gotten so red that now others probably thought the red face was his original skin color.

“But hey, who would have known? That Successor Gyeon Gang-hyeon would become leader, beating out Ja Hyeong-woo-nim?”

When he came to his senses, he had become the school’s only hunter directly under the leader…

“He must have had foresight?”

“He’d be good at stocks.”

That’s not it, I… Kwak Ji-hong had no choice but to swallow his wronged and awkward feelings today too as he entered the training ground.

“Oh, let’s watch him train once.”

“Since he belongs to the leader, he must be good at something.”

Please just leave me alone… Kwak Ji-hong had to lock the door to the ‘earth’ affiliated hunter training room every single day without fail. So no one could peek in.

‘I just… my mom and dad told me to live quietly without causing big trouble…’

There will be other people joining soon besides me, right…? Kwak Ji-hong only felt burdened and lacked the cunning to feel satisfaction toward the people who had pushed him into Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s affiliation back then.

Moreover, one more thing that was hard to just pass over was that personal messages from Successor Gyeon Gang-hyeon flew in occasionally.

==

[Sorry I’m a bit busy]

[I’ll start looking after your training by next week at the latest]

==

I don’t want to, no, you don’t need to look after me… Kwak Ji-hong couldn’t bring himself to say this either and struggled helplessly.

He was even too scared to look at the short spear weapon he’d given him, so he was carrying it around respectfully in his inventory.

‘If I can’t do it, the successor will give up quickly too. I just hope he won’t get angry…’

However, Kwak Ji-hong at this time didn’t know. That Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s training was the kind that had pulled up the hopeless Hae Mi-jun and even raised Won Ha-ri who had no metal attribute senior.

At the same time, in the annex of the central building where the Twelve Zodiac meeting room was located.

With Woo Sae-geon’s retirement, all the hunters under him were at risk of losing their abilities entirely. Like transplanting plants to different pots, they had to immediately move to Yang Pyo or Jin Seol-woon, who had the same attributes.

The problem was that there were too many of them.

Since it was an unfavorable situation, even Gyeon Gang-hyeon couldn’t just stand by and watch. Exactly ten people—no, even just five people—needed to share the burden of earth attributes.

In the past, even if he’d brought it up, he might have been ignored… but now he was the leader. He could find about five volunteers who were either adventurous or originally had weak abilities and weren’t particularly attached to great power.

And on the day they were to officially receive power from Gyeon Gang-hyeon, Gyeon Gang-hyeon unexpectedly saw a face he already knew.

“It’s been a while.”

Gyeon Gang-hyeon looked up at him in confusion as he stood last among the five.

Choi Jung-do, who had been Woo Sae-geon’s close associate. It was the first time since he had barged into Gang-hyeon’s room looking for records of school violence incidents.

He had even volunteered to go directly under Gyeon Gang-hyeon.

Considering his power as an A-rank hunter, it was almost wasteful. …Unless he was hiding some other ulterior motive beneath that bland expression.

Choi Jung-do matched the height of Gyeon Gang-hyeon, who was kneeling. Then he took off his shirt and bared his chest.

At the location of the heart, or the place closest to the heart, skin touches skin to absorb energy. Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s long, white fingers touched his chest first, then his palm smoothly attached.

Even in moments when most people would look at the ground or close their eyes because they felt embarrassed or the sensation was strange, Choi Jung-do only looked at his face with peculiar eyes. Gyeon Gang-hyeon didn’t notice as he was concentrating while breathing heavily.

As soon as it ended, Choi Jung-do lightly moved his body, which had slightly changed energy, then spoke first in a smooth tone.

“May I continue working in the discipline committee as before? If there are any issues related to that, I’ll come report directly to Gyeon Gang-hyeon-nim.”

The other party, whose small face was filled with fatigue, gave casual approval then suddenly said something out of the blue.

“You don’t still contact Woo Sae-geon, do you? Monitoring me to report back?”

Choi Jung-do answered calmly without changing his expression. It was a pointless misunderstanding.

“No. I have no relation whatsoever to Woo Sae-geon… Yoon Sae-geon-nim. Yoon Sae-geon-nim said when he retires, everything ends. To be honest, I don’t think he’d even acknowledge me if we passed each other on the street.”

Even after that, seeing Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s still dissatisfied expression, Choi Jung-do added more unprompted words.

“It’s only for a couple months anyway. After that, I’ll retire too when I come of age. My birthday is in December.”

“Ah, right.”

“So before then…”

Choi Jung-do, who had trailed off, looked at Gyeon Gang-hyeon for a while.

Before then, what? Gyeon Gang-hyeon asked back, but Choi Jung-do said “Never mind” and moved past the topic.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon, who was getting up, suddenly swayed greatly from dizziness. Choi Jung-do’s support immediately came in. His movements supporting the waist and holding the opposite hand were quite skilled.

“Be careful.”

“It’s fine, let go.”

Even after obediently letting go, Choi Jung-do’s gaze swept over Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s thin body for a while. His steps that were still slightly staggering despite pretending to be fine.

“I’m worried—may I escort you? Where are you going?”

“I’m going home, home! Don’t follow me.”

Of course it was a lie. There was one more place he needed to stop by.

The isolation dormitory behind the former Ox Successor’s quarters, more often called ‘prison’ by students.

The very place where students previously classified as school violence perpetrators were staying.

Supervising the students here was also part of Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s duties. Originally.

“No. Absolutely not. Don’t even come near here.”

That is, it would have been if Ja Hyeong-woo hadn’t shown stubborn opposition and intercepted this task.

“I look intimidating so it’s fine for me, but you might hear bad words from people who hold grudges for no reason. Go on. If you come even once more, I won’t let it slide.”

‘What are you going to do if you don’t let it slide? No matter how much you’re handling it, I’m still the leader, so does it make sense to ignore me completely? At minimum, an inspection should come, right?’

It was afternoon training time now. So thinking it was a time when Ja Hyeong-woo wouldn’t be there, Gyeon Gang-hyeon had stealthily visited the isolation facility…

Sure enough, he ran right into the navy blue hwarang outfit at the entrance.

As soon as he saw Gyeon Gang-hyeon, Ja Hyeong-woo’s expression hardened. His eyes immediately blazed fiercely.

“I told you not to come.”

Ja Hyeong-woo walked toward him steadily. Watching the large build getting closer and closer, the words ‘I won’t let it slide’ seemed to come to mind.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon tensed his body, thinking the guy might snatch him up like a sack of rice. When Ja Hyeong-woo’s hand came up, he was almost certain.

But.

The raised hand cupped one of Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s cheeks.

The moment the hot palm’s heat covered his skin, that side of his eyes wrinkled.

He should have slapped it away immediately, but Ja Hyeong-woo withdrew his hand faster, so he couldn’t do either. Something worse attacked the ear of the flustered Gyeon Gang-hyeon with dilated pupils.

“Your body is too cold. Go inside quickly.”

An almost whispered low voice.

Crazy bastard. Barely managing to throw that one curse, Gyeon Gang-hyeon turned around sharply.

‘Was that just now what you meant by not letting it slide? Because you do that…’

In his head as he hurried his steps to get away from Ja Hyeong-woo, the shocking words he’d seen in the community played back intact.

The things about Gyeon Gang-hyeon being the husband, Ja Hyeong-woo being the wife.

I should never look at the community again. Even after completely turning off the internet on his phone, his anger didn’t subside.

Actually, he desperately wanted to grab Ja Hyeong-woo by the collar and confront him saying “It’s because of you!” Or at least say “Do you even know?” to remind him of how things looked from the outside.

However, he couldn’t do any of those things when actually facing him.

Even slow Gyeon Gang-hyeon had a feeling that he wouldn’t be able to properly handle the reaction the guy would show after such words.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon deliberately stomped louder as he left the building. His head was completely blank.
