Damn Hunter Life Chapter 163

Even at this moment, the sleeping one was tightly holding Sa Ye-ho’s hand.

Sa Ye-ho gestured lightly toward Gyeon Gang-hyeon as if showing off, his lips twitching.

“It wasn’t me. Gang-hyeon grabbed it, right?”

He was a human who had survived even under Yoon Sae-geon. The jealousy of some same-age muscular guy was just laughable.

“……”

No, his face is even more awful than Sae-geon hyung’s. Sa Ye-ho turned his gaze away with an “ugh.”

“Smooth out your expression. Did you think you’d get it?”

Only then did the murderous aura flowing from Ja Hyeong-woo subside.

“You’re not the one holding it?”

“Really not.”

Ja Hyeong-woo approached with heavy steps. He tried hard to replace Sa Ye-ho’s hand with his own without waking Gyeon Gang-hyeon.

Three men’s hands got tangled. Sa Ye-ho pushed him away with his foot, hating to touch Ja Hyeong-woo even if he died. Ja Hyeong-woo lunged as if to cut off Sa Ye-ho’s hand. The traditional Korean clothes rustled as they wrestled back and forth.

“Hey, he’ll wake up! Move, damn it!”

“You’re the one being loud and waking him. You’re the one who should get lost.”

“Can’t you see Gang-hyeon is holding it?”

Then Ja Hyeong-woo froze first. Huh? Sa Ye-ho turned around.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon, who had woken from sleep, was staring blankly at the two guys.

“Good morning~.”

Sa Ye-ho, who smiled brightly, hugged Gyeon Gang-hyeon as if pouncing on him.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon only made an “uh…” sound and didn’t push Sa Ye-ho away. Only Ja Hyeong-woo, who couldn’t say anything, turned red and blue in the face.

* * *

Sa Ye-ho lay next to me and chattered by himself for a while (I guess he’s not even afraid of Ja Hyeong-woo), then when I complained of fatigue, he squeezed my hand once and left.

“Our Gang-mouse, tell oppa if you need his hand again~.”

“Get lost.”

Me and Ja Hyeong-woo said it at the same time, exactly the same. I was flustered by that, and Ja Hyeong-woo was satisfied.

Sa Ye-ho laughed at me with a “puhak” and then left.

As soon as the door closed, I collapsed onto the bed with a thud.

‘Still, having Sa Ye-ho visit gives a little vitality… what vitality.’

Ja Hyeong-woo’s presence staying beside me is so overwhelming that I can’t think of anything else.

I had grabbed Ja Hyeong-woo’s hand in my sleep earlier.

It was Sa Ye-ho, not Ja Hyeong-woo, but anyway.

I had unconsciously sought him out.

Just like if someone stabbed something, even if it was a mannequin, that intent would be recognized and judged as attempted murder.

I was so disturbed that I thought of such an inappropriate analogy. It was uncomfortable how Ja Hyeong-woo wouldn’t leave my side as if it were natural.

I had no idea whether that bastard had eaten anything proper or slept during this time. First of all, the dark circles under his eyes were too deep.

After maintaining silence quietly, I opened my mouth and barely managed the first words.

“Won’t you go? Don’t you have work to do?”

Ungratefully, I snapped at the healer who had been nursing me with utmost devotion all this time.

“Nothing’s more important than this. I’m not going.”

Ja Hyeong-woo spoke firmly, as if expecting me to say more. He didn’t seem like he’d back down easily.

“You’ll serve me for life?”

It was really meaningless, just something I said to be annoying.

But Ja Hyeong-woo looked at me with a serious face.

“…If you’ll allow it.”

I deliberately avoided his gaze.

The words I just heard were pounding in my head, crushing all other thoughts.

* * *

The S-grade sword broke, and the sensation that followed crushed and twisted my consciousness.

The cold flames that had burned even my heart were extinguished, and what remained was a body exhausted like ashes.

And thoughts. Thoughts about essence.

I had avoided the answer to who I am for far too long.

While sleeping as if dead, I reached out and struggled to find anything I could remember.

That’s why I was tired when I woke up. I had no strength in my hands and feet.

Honestly, I’m not even sure if I’ll be able to use earth attribute skills again. Can a body really be this heavy?

‘If Ja Hyeong-woo hadn’t deliberately woken me up and fed me, I would have died long ago.’

In the dark night, I woke to a loud sound. It was the sound of me coughing. A sudden fit of coughing burst out and I clutched my chest while hacking.

I think I had a dream. I don’t remember the content.

It’s frustrating. Even though there’s actually nothing stuck in my throat, I kept trying to cough something up.

“Cough, hack, gasp.”

Far from subsiding, the coughing got worse as time passed. The sound of struggling for breath grew louder. I coughed dryly while lying face-up, then turned over with difficulty.

A warm wall touched in front of me.

While crouched against the wall coughing, I realized it was Ja Hyeong-woo’s body.

Only then, while gasping and trying to turn my body again, an arm wrapped around from behind. I was held firmly in that state.

Hot hands gently stroked my back. I finished coughing against Ja Hyeong-woo’s chest.

“Ha, huk.”

Physiological tears welled up. While wiping them with my sleeve, Ja Hyeong-woo moved in the darkness. He slowly sat me up and gave me warm water to drink.

I hadn’t even known Ja Hyeong-woo was lying beside me.

Even after my breathing difficulties were resolved, our position didn’t change. A clean, good smell came from Ja Hyeong-woo’s embrace.

I should get up. I should get up… My body relaxed from the warm body temperature.

I shouldn’t get used to this.

He didn’t ask a single question like why I was coughing when I didn’t even have a cold. Without making a fuss, he skillfully gave me what I needed.

This sense of security is like poison.

I rested my head against Ja Hyeong-woo’s chest as if I were in a grave.

* * *

Ja Hyeong-woo maintained strength in his arms so Gyeon Gang-hyeon could calm down and fall asleep.

For the peace of the being in his arms, he would be fine dying torn to pieces.

He wished some omnipotent being would crush his heart and give it to Gyeon Gang-hyeon as life.

Holding him brought back the sorrowful memories from inside the stage. Ja Hyeong-woo faced the painful fragments of the past that his mind dragged up.

When he was desperately doing embarrassing things to extract yang energy to give to Gyeon Gang-hyeon…

“Han Hyeong-woo.”

Gyeon Gang-hyeon, who had suddenly come to his senses, called Ja Hyeong-woo. Then he looked into his eyes and said:

“Even if I fail and can’t save anyone… I’ll save you even if I have to trade myself for it.”

What kind of talk is that? Ja Hyeong-woo asked in surprise.

He should have pressed him right there, but there was no time for that. Gyeon Gang-hyeon fainted again. Ja Hyeong-woo had to continue what he was doing with confused feelings.

Even after regaining consciousness later, Gyeon Gang-hyeon seemed not to remember what he had said at the time.

Though he had barely slept, he only felt his eyes were slightly tired but wasn’t sleepy. Ja Hyeong-woo quietly held Gyeon Gang-hyeon until dawn broke.

Suddenly Gyeon Gang-hyeon stirred.

Ja Hyeong-woo loosened his arms slightly. He didn’t look down but remained lost in thought a little longer. Letting his mind drift to the pale dawn light coming through the curtains.

A long silence stretched. Gyeon Gang-hyeon remained trapped in his embrace, only wiggling his fingers slightly.

Only when Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s voice flowed out cutting through the darkness did Ja Hyeong-woo look down at the round crown of his head below his chin.

“Before, when I cursed at you, told you to just train, and didn’t pay attention to you… It wasn’t because I hated you. Of course, it’s not like you never made me angry or annoyed me…”

After rambling quietly, he soon added that he was sorry.

It was Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s second apology.

Ja Hyeong-woo thought of the young Gyeon Gang-hyeon from the time he had missed.

Also the Gyeon Gang-hyeon from a year ago, whom he had turned away from.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon noticed that Ja Hyeong-woo’s chest was rising and falling more greatly and unstably than before.

“You don’t need to apologize.”

The air entering and leaving Ja Hyeong-woo’s lungs became more turbulent. Gyeon Gang-hyeon raised his eyes, accustomed to the darkness, to look at his face.

Then he was startled and immediately snapped his head down.

He didn’t have the courage to look up again. His heart was pounding wildly.

“Why can’t you understand. If I can’t make you understand because I’m not good with words, I wish someone else would appear and tell you. That you’re someone who deserves to be worried about. That it’s okay, if someone could make you understand…”

His words trailed off and cracked roughly. But they didn’t break.

Ja Hyeong-woo was consuming enormous energy to spit out each and every word.

“I said I did it on purpose because I wanted you to become a good healer. But I never wanted to be a hero. If becoming strong meant receiving your hatred, I’d absolutely never do it. Even if the world ended, I want to win your heart. That’s the kind of person I am.”

It was a confession staking his entire life, and a yearning directed at Gyeon Gang-hyeon.

“You who say hurtful things are also you. I love all of it.”

Gyeon Gang-hyeon listened to those words silently. They were words that demanded as much mental strength from the listener as from the speaker.

Even without answering, he chose to listen.

“I used to hate you sometimes. But now, everything except you is resentful. Why do you have to hurt? What should I do so you never hurt again? I’m going crazy… I have to see your face.”

Silence pressed down on the room.

Ja Hyeong-woo wasn’t disappointed in Gyeon Gang-hyeon for not saying a word. He wiped his face with his sleeve and smiled gently.

“Don’t you need to sleep more? Want to get up?”

“…Yeah.”

Ja Hyeong-woo prepared an early morning meal. Meanwhile, Gyeon Gang-hyeon carefully opened an internet portal. It was the first time in five days.

The moment he skimmed news and column titles expecting all sorts of bad words like controversy, demands for explanation, staying holed up, seclusion.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon threw his phone onto the bed with a loud thud.

“…?”

His head was full of question marks.
