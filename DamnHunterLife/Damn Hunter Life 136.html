Unusually today, when I opened the office door, Sa Ye-ho wasn’t there.

Maybe because of the habit formed during this time, whether he came to play games or whatever, Sa Ye-ho always arrived first and opened the locked door.

“Hi…”

Only much later did he appear, opening the door with a shuffling gait. With a face that had completely lost its mind overnight.

‘What’s wrong with him?’

It doesn’t seem like he gamed all night. After I became leader, he didn’t just do random stuff like that.

Then there must be someone capable of making the carefree Sa Ye-ho skin and bones.

“What did the Association nag about this time?”

“Huh? Our puppy’s intuition is level 100…”

“What kind of fight did you have? If you’re in that state, Kim Jae-moon must have bandages around his ears.”

Sa Ye-ho sprawled on the sofa and laughed weakly. Yet he still didn’t open his mouth first.

“Your mouth is unnecessarily heavy only at times like this.”

Only when I snapped at him irritably did he finally speak.

“Just… how Gang-hyeon is doing, whether he’s doing well… that kind of thing. Since he’s too busy to ask you directly.”

I weakly kicked under the desk and removed my fingers from the keyboard.

Sa Ye-ho has the ability to smoothly brush off the Association’s nagging with his slick responses. That it was still hard to shake off was probably because his opponent was someone who’d been entrenched in a government agency for a long time.

Therefore, it seemed like several hours of fruitless argument had continued.

Something Woo Sae-geon probably went through once in the very early days of Hunter School, when young people under 24 took their first steps in a situation where they were responsible for Korea’s safety.

—The public’s concerns are endless. Of course, the Association will fully support Successor Woo Sae-geon’s actions.

—What will today’s schedule be like? How about broadcasting part of it publicly?

Young people were trapped in unconfirmed gates, and families clamored outside demanding their return. People were dying throughout Korea, and young people doing dangerous training without adult intervention had to step up and save them. Given the situation and the hunters’ age group, it could be seen as natural worry and intervention.

But actually, age was just a superficial reason.

‘It’s noise generated because the higher-ups can’t accept hunters as special beings. It would have been the same even if battle-hardened adults had ended up at Hunter School. It probably would have been the same even if someone like a police chief had been the school head.’

Gates and monsters from another world, unexplainable by modern science, were exactly that kind of shock to people. A time when they just spat out worries not knowing what to do, pushing their anxiety and burdens onto someone else.

In that sense, creating and utilizing the existence of ‘successors’ was an excellent choice for calming chaos. Because people want heroes while finding them awkward.

‘But the most outstanding among them, Woo Sae-geon, is gone?’

He even defeated all the Myodusa in recent stages himself, leaving an unforgettable impact before leaving like that.

Only I, a former delinquent, was left here alone.

‘Really, Woo Sae-geon passed leadership to me to screw me over…’

All domestic and international news talked about Korea’s #1’s retirement, and most of them mentioned my name.

‘The original work’s Ja Hyeong-woo just cut himself off from the world, so while others were frustrated, he himself was very stubborn.’

But I’m not Ja Hyeong-woo, right? I’m concerned that Sa Ye-ho’s energy is being drained.

‘The Association is above successors, but the public is above the Association.’

I decided to put the idea I’d been thinking about into practice.

“Ye-ho. There’s The Hero broadcast team, right? Do you have their personal number?”

“Yeah. PD Hong Jeong-hyeok.”

Due to Yoon Sae-geon’s exclusion policy (plus my track record), that place was virtually the only one exclusively covering successors.

“Is that person the head? Contact them and suggest doing a special feature.”

I’m a bit worried about giving them too much power… but anyway, Association permission is needed to film the training grounds.

If that’s the case, it’s better to include the broadcast team and split it with the Association.

“Peeking at Successor Joint Training, something like that.”

This was something the upright Woo Sae-geon would never have suggested first, even if it killed him. Sa Ye-ho’s squinted eyes opened wide.

Sa Ye-ho seemed more interested than worried. Getting up from the sofa, Sa Ye-ho leaned his upper body toward me.

“Are you confident?”

Of course, I’m also in charge of the group training that Woo Sae-geon used to lead.

The wild horse-like Ma Cheon-ah, Yang Pyo who doesn’t understand well, and Hae Mi-jun who’s so gentle he’s soft—I have to command all of them.

Woo Sae-geon had the presence to control and lead all of them. But me?

‘How easy must the new leader look? Gyeon Gang-hyeon teaching someone? Everyone’s probably just putting up question marks.’

Plus I have no authority and my attitude is endlessly light. Will group training led by such a me actually work?

‘That’s why I’ll show them. Just how it really is.’

I’ll make that Kim Jae-moon guy shut his mouth tight.

Literally a sneaky feeling like peeking at joint training through door cracks was the concept of this coverage.

The successor training ground, revealed publicly for the first time in months, glittered with five-colored attack effects.

Familiars were attacking successors.

A fresh green rabbit, a heavy brown dragon, and a house-sized boar with blue waves.

When the video was first released, the chat window was filled with ‘…?’

It was an even more shocking sight because these were successors and their familiars known to be model students or colleague-friendly.

====

‘Is it okay for familiars to attack the kids?’

And in the center of the space where they were rampaging was the new leader, pale as his white clothes…

Commanding the familiars while emitting a sharper aura than imagined. The three unnaturally moving familiars looked as if they were his minions.

A short video was inserted explaining how the familiars could attack their owners’ colleagues.

“Today, rabbit, dragon, and pig will be used as monster roles.”

The voice that had been evaluated as clear but somewhat weak in successor cheering songs was quite resonant.

“But that doesn’t mean the owners need to give bad commands directly.”

Considering he was broadcasting, he suddenly stopped talking, inserted a brief pause, then spoke again.

“I’ll control them, and I’ll also play the villain role. You just need to immerse yourselves and fight like it’s real combat.”

Since VR simulation had limitations, Gyeon Gang-hyeon utilized familiars as fake monsters.

With his affinity ability that made even others’ familiars serve him as master.

====

‘Is that possible?’

‘Gyeon Gang-hyeon seems more like a bug character as time goes on’

“If I use others’ familiars too much, their connection with their original owners might weaken, plus it drains a lot of my stamina. Real combat training will always be short. Within 20 minutes.”

To extract maximum efficiency in a short time, he drove them as roughly as possible to produce results.

The rabbit leaped up as if to trample people, and the boar charged frighteningly to instill terror. The dragon stirred between them.

With his mouth firmly shut, Gyeon Gang-hyeon pressured the young successors without blinking an eye.

Myo Cho-hui participated in training together, assisting the monster roles with vines. Surprisingly, she stood side by side next to Gyeon Gang-hyeon. It wasn’t just for the broadcast.

“Yang Pyo! Not watching behind you?”

His gaze became much gentler. His eyes softened with affection. Thanks to this, for almost the first time, the broadcast could capture Ja Hyeong-woo’s appearance to its advantage.

“Now other people are seeing Gang-hyeon correctly.”

That appearance as if he were enchanted by someone.
