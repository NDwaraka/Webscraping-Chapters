The camera assigned to Ja Hyeong-woo must have been filming only his peacefully closed-eyed face for an hour now. Pure tranquility that wouldn’t even produce 10 seconds of footage.

‘But now I’m going to doodle on him.’

Trusting only the information I’d seen in the original work that once he falls asleep, he doesn’t wake up easily, I stealthily approached Ja Hyeong-woo.

Holding a magic marker and looking down at the dark-colored canvas—

“…!”

Ja Hyeong-woo’s eyes suddenly snapped open as he jerked his upper body up and grabbed my wrist.

“Ah!”

When Ja Hyeong-woo got up, the entire hammock lurched. Suddenly weight was put on my caught wrist and my body collapsed forward.

Just as I was about to headbutt Ja Hyeong-woo, I desperately turned my head to the side.

“…”

The entire hammock tilted to one side. Ja Hyeong-woo put one leg on the ground to stabilize the hammock, then caught me against his chest to stop the series of accidents.

I growled quietly in Ja Hyeong-woo’s ear so the status window cam wouldn’t pick it up.

“Fuck, they absolutely won’t edit this kind of thing out on broadcasts…”

I put special emphasis on the “fuck.” Reducing my voice so only air came out.

I tried to get up immediately by gripping the hammock, but it kept swaying so it wasn’t easy. Ja Hyeong-woo, who had been fidgeting, soon slowly lifted me up.

Standing on both legs again and awkwardly tousling my hair, I realized I was still holding the magic marker in my hand.

And that Ja Hyeong-woo had already seen the magic marker.

Wait, it didn’t say to doodle on his face ‘secretly’, did it?

“Hey, the broadcast team wants me to doodle on your face. Close your eyes and go back to sleep.”

I went out brazenly with a straight face. Isn’t a delinquent image for times like this?

“Alright.”

This works?

Shameless audacity really does pay off. Delinquents are the best.

“That’s permanent marker so it won’t come off.”

The mission I was going to do with Jin Seol-woon was number 6: hearing him say “I like it.”

Jin Seol-woon, who had been sitting in the pavilion plucking guitar strings and humming to himself, looked up when he sensed my presence.

“Hello.”

After giving an oddly formal greeting, he didn’t look back down at his guitar but kept staring at me quietly. As if waiting for my lips to open.

I tried a light lead-in. I pointed at the guitar and sheet music with my finger.

“How’s that going?”

“Composing? It’s fun.”

He said “fun” instead of “I like it.” I need to make him say “I like it.”

“You’re good at combat but you really should stick to your main job. A guitar suits you better than a sword. Why didn’t you just make your weapon guitar-shaped? I rambled on with lengthy nonsense while leading Jin Seol-woon toward the mission. Jin Seol-woon tilted his head and thought carefully.

“How long… it’s been almost 10 months…”

“So how do you feel about it? How’s composing?”

Ugh, that was a bit too obvious just now. Having gotten a bit frustrated, I was being suspiciously persistent to anyone watching.

Jin Seol-woon suddenly opened his eyes wide. Then he chuckled and looked up at me knowingly as he parted his lips.

“I like it.”

For about a second, silence flowed.

“…Okay, success! Nice.”

I waved my hand lightly and left the pavilion to do other missions. I easily succeeded at Hae Mi-jun and Won Ha-ri’s missions next, then reconnected with Sa Ye-ho.

‘Obviously Woo Sae-geon.’
