Damn Hunter Life Chapter 157

* * *

“We’re suspending stage broadcasts.”

That announcement was already several days ago.

I didn’t check public opinion. I told Sa Ye-ho to look at it appropriately and just let it pass if the reactions were within expected range. Since he hasn’t said anything, fortunately it seems there haven’t been calls for my impeachment or anything like that.

Thump, thump.

Even though I was just standing on a hill I used to visit often, looking at the moon in the night sky, my heart beat unstably. To the point where I worried it might strain my entire body.

When I close my eyes, it feels like I can hear that ringing—beep! from that time.

I picture Ja Hyeong-woo standing next to pools of blood, looking up at the sky with empty eyes.

The S-grade staff that had been broken and reduced to mere twigs. The traces of kids who had stained the area with blood, whom that staff couldn’t save.

When I thought those bloodstains belonged to ordinary citizens, I committed the pathetic act of mindlessly self-harming to try to make Ja Hyeong-woo grow.

But when I realized they might belong to Hae Mi-jun, Won Ha-ri, or other successors… I couldn’t maintain my sanity at all.

As a result, I didn’t do any other ridiculous things.

I’m just preparing for the stage that’s coming in two days.

A gust of wind blew. As I shrank into my padding from the winter cold, I suddenly looked down the hill as if someone had pulled me.

A guy who wasn’t even cold, wearing only traditional warrior clothes, met my eyes while his long black hair fluttered.

I frowned slightly from déjà vu.

But the current Ja Hyeong-woo, instead of turning away, came up toward me.

Ja Hyeong-woo, standing close, opened his palm.

Squeak.

A familiar shape that seemed like it had been mine. Seeing the blue mouse, the trembling around my eyes subsided.

The mouse familiar hopped up and climbed onto my shoulder.

“I hate you. Aren’t you leaving?”

The mouse familiar pretended not to know and burrowed into my padding.

“Honestly, both master and familiar…”

The shameless creature was acting cute. I frowned because the sensation of it moving around tickled me.

For some reason, Ja Hyeong-woo didn’t nag me to go inside because the night air was cold.

Unless he had unwound his scarf and wrapped it around me.

‘Did he bring it out because of me…?’

No, how does this bastard know to follow me like a ghost when I’m feeling sorry for myself? Even the mouse familiar doesn’t stay attached to me anymore.

“A water attribute student saw you and told me.”

“I didn’t ask!”

The warmth of his heated body temperature and Ja Hyeong-woo’s body scent wafting out. More than that, I was more annoyed that my troubled inner thoughts had been completely exposed. My cheeks subtly heated up.

Even without words, Ja Hyeong-woo’s gaze made me uncomfortable. I chose to stare at the sky to avoid his eyes.

‘If I hadn’t saved the original Gyeon Gang-hyeon and wasn’t here now.’

Would all of this now be weight that you, Ja Hyeong-woo, would have to carry alone?

“Aren’t you scared?”

Before I knew it, a rambling question had slipped out of my mouth.

And Ja Hyeong-woo was quietly looking at my profile.

“I’m scared.”

That big guy, who was as fearless as Yoon Sae-geon, readily admitted it.

“Because you’re scared.”

The original story’s lone, lofty healer wasn’t there. The current Ja Hyeong-woo was just a bastard who always followed me with his eyes and was dying to rub against my lips.

So in a way, it was right for me to become leader instead of Ja Hyeong-woo.

Even if Ja Hyeong-woo didn’t make up his mind, if I looked toward my comrades’ safety and peace, Ja Hyeong-woo would follow and look there too.

“Look at this.”

The status window Ja Hyeong-woo had come to show me while I was racking my brains over stage 33.

==

<Prayer That Draws Up Purifying Water (Water Attribute)> — Advanced

Remaining uses: 3

==

Ja Hyeong-woo’s precious but extremely difficult-to-activate advanced heal skill.

‘You have to feel like the world would collapse just thinking about the person you’re trying to heal dying, and be so desperate you’d want to give your own life.’

An advanced heal that could only be used on me.

But below that, an unfamiliar message had appeared.

==

*Existing activation conditions have been erased.

==

Now Ja Hyeong-woo could cast his advanced skill without any conditions.

Skills could change for better or worse, they said. This case followed a change of heart.

‘Did Ja Hyeong-woo change his mind after seeing me accept the leader’s responsibility and not leave my desk?’

The entire time I was looking at the moon, Ja Hyeong-woo never even glanced at the sky from start to finish.

I didn’t feel the winter wind on my right cheek, where Ja Hyeong-woo’s gaze had been resting the whole time.

“I want to become close enough to kiss and embrace you even when it’s not for healing.”

…What?

I’d just given him a good evaluation, and that’s what he says now? I was dumbfounded and stared at him with my mouth open.

He didn’t back down and sought my eyes with his pupils.

“It’s true that I try to heal you, but I really get excited. …I want to do it.”

He threw the words while looking straight into both my eyes. Feelings that felt more impure for being honest. Like a heavy shield rather than a pebble. As if I were a fortress wall, he crashed into me straight and true.

He’d frequently said things like liking me before. He’d persistently repeated it, afraid I might ignore it, forget it, or pretend it never happened.

But this was a statement of a slightly different nature.

‘He wants to hold me—’

No, I shook my head vigorously. I was just confused for a moment because of Ja Hyeong-woo’s gaze and voice, but there’s no need for such polite expressions between men.

‘He said he wants to do it.’

Could it be, right now…? Fortunately, that didn’t seem to be the case. Ja Hyeong-woo’s condition was calm and when I sneakily looked, the front of his pants was fine too.

Ja Hyeong-woo smiled softly at my appearance of rolling my eyes around in confusion like I’d been struck by lightning.

“I’m just saying this so you won’t be too nervous the day after tomorrow. Try to distract yourself by thinking about me.”

No, he was serious. Perhaps to a considerable extent, I should consider that Ja Hyeong-woo had become cunning.

‘I don’t have to give some answer, do I? Like yes or no.’

As soon as that thought reached me, my feet moved on their own. I ran down the hill with thundering steps.

“Don’t follow me! I’m going to sleep.”

Right. I also took out the mouse familiar from inside my padding and threw it at him. I ran away again.

I didn’t throw it hard enough, so whether the mouse rolled on the ground or not wasn’t my concern. My survival was more important.

* * *

Even though I tried not to show my anxiety to the kids as much as possible.

The different stage number from before. And the powerful fire attribute spirit monsters and A-grade rating that were announced to appear there seemed to have affected the kids.

Ah, I don’t mean someone got scared and tried to run away. Even Yang Pyo didn’t say such things.

The limit was 7 people, but the problem was that everyone wanted to go.

‘Am I actually the biggest coward?’

When did everyone become so brave?

I thought choosing who to take among nine people would be a headache since I had to pick seven, but it turned out deciding who to exclude was the bigger problem.

After much deliberation, I chose not to take Won Ha-ri.

Jigwi are fire demons from the Queen Seondeok legend. Won Ha-ri was too vulnerable to fire by nature, making it too dangerous.

However, she was also the successor with the strongest killing power. Our combination without Won Ha-ri was also dangerous. But I had already made my decision firm.

“When we figure out what’s behind the stage numbers, I’ll use you again.”

“No. Let me go with you now. I’ll work harder. I can do it more stably than before. I won’t make Gang-hyeon oppa worry…”

A sixteen-year-old girl begged to please be allowed to go to a dangerous place. For this young kid, neither achievements nor fear were important.

The impatience of having to wait while letting me go into a fire pit alone.

I smiled gently and patted Won Ha-ri’s shoulder.

“Watch the house.”

Won Ha-ri froze at the casually thrown words.

Her body suddenly stiffened, and soon her brown eyes became moist with moisture. A dazed voice flowed from Won Ha-ri, who had deeply bowed her head.

“Okay. Oppa.”

I didn’t ask why she answered informally. I pretended not to know and left, showing her my straight back even if it was gaunt and unsightly.

And the next successor I excluded was.

“It’s not because you’re lacking somewhere. I won’t say you’re too aggressive anymore either. This is just your turn to rest. You need to recharge your skills, don’t you?”

Ma Cheon-ah threw a fit, but I didn’t reverse my decision. She struggled for a long time, saying to exclude Mi-jun or exclude snake oppa, trying somehow to secure her spot.

Then she deflated on her own.

After glaring at me like an enemy, the next moment she relaxed the strength in her eyes and whispered in a small voice.

“…I’m anxious when you’re not in front of me.”

I also patted Ma Cheon-ah’s shoulder, saying I’d sweep everything up so if she was anxious, she should sleep in the meantime. I boasted loudly that everything would be finished when she woke up.

‘I have to succeed. I have to pull it off.’

Returning my thoughts to the present, I took a deep breath. Then I looked ahead.

Everything visible was burning. It was a hellish scene.

I could see burning spirits rising from all directions to devour the living.
