The shocking article that first appeared on internet portal sites was finally reported on cable news as well.

These past few days, the atmosphere at Hunter School has been uncontrollably turbulent.

Having learned about another side of their stern leader, whom they had feared yet followed, in the worst possible way.

The kind of material that could make even a successful celebrity fall into ruin in an instant.

The kind of material that could completely destroy all reputation in one blow, no matter how sincere and good a person had been evaluated to be until then.

Taking a half-bath, I crossed my feet the opposite way in the steaming bathtub.

I recalled the American news I had frantically searched as soon as I learned about Woo Sae-geon’s allegations.

The reporting had been mixed over there too.

‘Articles claiming Woo Sae-geon isn’t Korea’s #1 but an American hunter.’

The claim that if Woo Sae-geon hadn’t happened to be visiting Korea and ended up at Korean Hunter School, he would have been an American awakener. It seemed like a scheme to steal Korea’s #1 position.

And the other side—

‘Provocative articles claiming Woo Sae-geon is indeed a Korean hunter, but might be a criminal who previously dealt drugs. Articles that excitedly wrote gossip saying he wasn’t their country’s hunter even before investigation results came out.’

If it’s not true anyway, Woo Sae-geon just needs to quickly undergo police investigation and be cleared of charges…

The problem is that Korean hunters trapped in Hunter School can’t contact outside people.

This incident turned what should have been the tragedy of Korea’s #1 disappearing in just 2 weeks into the disaster of still having to wait 2 whole weeks for Woo Sae-geon to undergo investigation and clear his name.

Moreover, since it’s a case reported from a foreign country rather than mainland Korea, it’s difficult to immediately receive updates on the investigation status of the drug trafficking organization that fingered Woo Sae-geon. Therefore, results and explanations continue to be delayed.

[“It seems the American friendship match broadcast became the root of trouble. I heard Successor Woo Sae-geon proceeded knowing there might be people who would recognize him, but to get involved in such an unsavory matter…”]

That’s what Manager Park Yoon-seok told me in a subdued voice when I called him.

On one hand, I can finally understand Woo Sae-geon’s behavior now.

The reason the former tattooist deliberately adopted a concept with the image most opposite to himself, and absolutely never showed his hair untied.

It must have been to prevent acquaintances in America from recognizing him.

‘Honestly, the past photos in the articles were startling. I really thought he was a gang member.’

It seemed like photos taken by tattooist colleagues in back alleys, where Woo Sae-geon appeared only as a distant side profile showing his back, as if he hadn’t wanted to be photographed.

Long black hair loosely tied in one, with a strangely exotic feel. Muscular shoulders coolly revealed by a sleeveless shirt. Chaotic tattoos densely covering his neck and arms.

‘It’s radical, really. Isn’t this the scholar’s shocking past?’

Moreover, the people standing in front of him added to that shock.

Though their faces were covered, they all basically had menacing tattoos on their bodies, and were either more burly than Woo Sae-geon or American women dressed provocatively, so it was natural if you think about it.

Come to think of it, this is Woo Sae-geon’s first past photo.

No wonder the broadcast PDs couldn’t get any. He was someone who lived in America.

‘But then, if he also grew up in America, why was he so hostile during the friendship match? Was it all acting? Or does he not like where he grew up…?’

Anyway, this is why people’s opinions were divided from the very early days of the successors’ appearance. About Woo Sae-geon and Ja Hyeong-woo’s pasts.

And among them, Woo Sae-geon’s secret has been revealed.

‘Rather than revealed… it was exposed. Against his will.’

At an official press conference a few days ago, Woo Sae-geon only briefly stated, ‘Everything except the drug matter is true,’ and immediately cut off the video call screen.

Only I was frustrated watching it.

‘Why is his way of speaking like that? He should say it backwards! He should emphasize that while he is Korean-American and a tattooist, he was absolutely never involved with drugs!’

It’s still frustrating to think about. I got angry and moved my body, causing the bathwater to splash.

Eventually I got tired and leaned back against the bathtub again, sighing.

I didn’t see this kind of thing in the original work either.

I realize that as my original work information reaches its end, more and more things I don’t know will happen.

‘There’s nothing I can do to help either…’

No wait. Even if there were something I could do, why should I do anything for Woo Sae-geon? I’m a delinquent, aren’t I? And what have I ever received from that guy?

“……”

I turned on my phone.

At some point, I had everyone’s phone numbers except Myo Cho-hui and Woo Sae-geon. I entered the group message window and typed.

[You know that news is bullshit I wouldn’t even bother with, right?]

[Bastards with grudges are excited that Woo Sae-geon is retiring and acting up]

[You absolutely cannot show it]

[If you think you can’t manage your expressions in front of Woo Sae-geon, avoid him instead]

I almost dropped my phone in the water once, then carefully set it down safely near the bathtub edge.

Maybe because I was angry, even though the water had cooled, I felt too hot instead of cold. I fanned my face with my hand.

‘So there are bastards who don’t care that they’ll get cursed at in reverse when he’s cleared, and just decided to drag someone down.’

What I’ve been cursed at for until now was just because I deserved to be cursed at…

Just as I was about to slowly pull the bathtub drain and get out before my hands and feet got wrinkled, my phone rang.

It was a call from Sa Ye-ho. I was too lazy to dry my hands again so I ignored the call and asked via chat.

20 minutes later, Sa Ye-ho who came to my house was dangling a bottle of alcohol in his hand.

He didn’t even offer me a drink, then sprawled on my sofa and blew into the wine bottle. After gulping it down for a while, Sa Ye-ho went “Pah,” pulled his lips away and grinned at me.

A guy who’s such a heavy drinker he doesn’t even get drunk on stuff like that, why is he acting like this? Ah, he was always like this.

Or is he also troubled?

I plopped down next to him and cursed while checking the alcohol content of the wine bottle Sa Ye-ho was holding.

“You have a stage in three days, you crazy bastard.”

“Our puppy is worried about oppa~.”

For a moment, only the sound of Sa Ye-ho’s throat gurgling could be heard. In the rare silence, I quietly opened my mouth.

“Give me some Woo Sae-geon TMI today.”

Sa Ye-ho answered as if he’d been waiting. As expected, without any trace of laughter.

“He’s in a bad mood. The worst I’ve seen.”

The fact that he won’t let me into his office means it’s really serious. Sa Ye-ho muttered unconsciously.

Silence flowed again. We both sat side by side just staring straight ahead. Neither of us suggested turning on the TV.

“…Want me to tell you something interesting?”

I didn’t react at all but Sa Ye-ho started rambling on and on. Drinking in between, with a dazed face like he was drunk.

“Remember what I told you before? When Sae-geon hyung first summoned his ox familiar.”

That incident I call the rodeo incident.

The incident where the ox familiar didn’t obey orders and went berserk, so Woo Sae-geon got beaten to a pulp for 3 hours while subduing his familiar.

Sa Ye-ho stopped talking, took out a new bottle from inventory, and burned off the cork with his bare hands. After cleaning up the ash completely, he took several gulps.

His eyes had sunk even deeper by then.

“The ox familiar was following Sae-geon hyung’s orders from the beginning.”

At first I didn’t understand, then I realized and got goosebumps all over my body.

“What kind of person is Sae-geon hyung that some little thing wouldn’t follow him?”

The rodeo incident was actually Woo Sae-geon’s self-directed play.

Woo Sae-geon ordered the ox familiar to rampage like a demon, then ordered it to attack him and fought it.

He deliberately made his body bleed. And he made the students see that entire spectacle.

…That’s how he instilled fear in the students and seized power.

‘No wonder controlling the ox familiar was easy for me.’

I expected it might have been something he deliberately showed to students… but this isn’t something a normal person would do.

But Woo Sae-geon’s such behavior didn’t stem from malice.

Because that was the easiest way to control students. Because he thought only by controlling everything himself could the chaotic early Hunter School and hunters become stable.

The confidence of someone who was a tattooist believing he should do something he’d never tried before.

Moreover, in that process, he doesn’t consider at all that he might get hurt or suffer.

“That’s why he makes people fucking worry.”

Hearing Sa Ye-ho’s muttering, I canceled my previous thought that Sa Ye-ho was the type to readily reveal his inner thoughts.

If that weren’t the case, there’s no way Sa Ye-ho could have perfectly hidden it when he was capable of making such eyes.

Suddenly Sa Ye-ho’s pale irises glanced back at me.

“I told you. That Sae-geon hyung and you are similar.”

It wasn’t like a joke. So this time I tried to think about it as seriously as possible.

…Don’t tell me that time I self-harmed saying I’d develop Ja Hyeong-woo’s abilities?

“Hey, is that the same level?”

“The mindset is similar.”

What Sa Ye-ho was worried about was Woo Sae-geon’s plan that had completely collapsed when his retirement was almost here. His plan to finish activities safely and return to America to live quietly.

The current Woo Sae-geon has had everything collapse. His plan, the impression he’d built up until now. …And probably his pride too.

In such a situation, the day of his final stage battle had arrived.
