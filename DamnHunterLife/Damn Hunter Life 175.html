Damn Hunter Life Chapter 175

* * *

When more than half of Hunter School’s students had gone out, returned exhausted, and were repeatedly alternating with other waiting teams.

Kwak Ji-hong was holed up in his room, trembling.

‘T-thank goodness. I didn’t get matched…’

Even though more than half a day had passed since the clearing began, there had been no gate notifications looking for him.

The hunters under Gyeon Gang-hyeon who had entered after him were all currently out in the field. Yet he alone remained, just shaking his head frantically.

‘Mom and Dad told me not to do it too.’

Every time he turned on the news, chaotic scenes passed across the screen. He could also see hunters busily moving around sites in disheveled states from handling gates.

‘It’s not my business, it’s not my business.’

He tried hard to believe that. From when he first awakened, he’d continued with the same thought never going out.

It was peace chosen with an uncomfortable heart.

If contact with his parents hadn’t suddenly been cut off an hour ago, he would have continued to settle for peace.

“Mom, Dad!”

They clearly said they’d safely arrived at the shelter… Kwak Ji-hong’s heart pounded and shook.

Both parents’ phones had only been giving dial tones without connecting since earlier. Did they drop their phones? Both of them?

Until about 30 minutes later, when he learned that a monster had attacked the phone company, temporarily cutting off that carrier’s service.

For Kwak Ji-hong, it was hellish time.

“Huuk… huff.”

Kwak Ji-hong, who had been sniffling and crying, raised his head and wiped his tears.

Even though the worry filling his head had finally subsided, something stirred in his heart. The muscles that had only been used for trembling somehow twitched.

‘Pathetic. Really pathetic.’

I also have two legs and arms.

In his inventory was a short spear used for training that he’d approached like studying—something he didn’t want to do but had to.

It was a spear that looked shabby at first glance and frail like its creator…

But strangely, this spear seemed alive.

Rather than the user taming the weapon, the weapon flexibly moved on its own to match the user’s habits.

From the first time he swung it, the spear gently absorbed Kwak Ji-hong’s clumsy earth energy and regulated it so the power wouldn’t flow backward. Even when Kwak Ji-hong swung it clumsily, it didn’t make even one wrist sore.

What abilities would this mysterious spear display if it stabbed monsters in real combat?

[“…Why did you contact me. If you called a busy person, there should be a good enough reason.”]

Huh, Kwak Ji-hong gasped.

He seemed to have unknowingly made a subordinate hunter communication call to the weapon creator. And it was the first time he’d initiated contact.

His teeth chattered from tension. However, this time Kwak Ji-hong didn’t apologize submissively and hang up.

Since the call had already connected, then.

“I’m really lacking, aren’t I? I’ve never been gate matched anyway…”

Even if it originated from a mistake, he spoke the words.

The person on the other end of the communication was silent for a while.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon guessed Kwak Ji-hong’s confusion. He soon made a decision.

[“It’s not that you didn’t get matched.”]

He decided to tell him the truth.

[“I blocked it with my authority.”]

As soon as those words fell, an unlocked status window appeared above Kwak Ji-hong’s head.

Traces of gate matchings that had been made then canceled, traces of rejected personnel assistance requests… besides that, countless notifications were there. Matching, matching, matching. Past notifications poured out.

Kwak Ji-hong looked at his blinking status window with a dumbfounded face. Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s voice continued to reach his ears.

[“Even if your will is tiny as a bean, you have good motor skills and decent learning ability. Most importantly, you’re my direct disciple, though you don’t know it. There’s no other kid I’ve personally looked after for affinity as intensively as you.”]

The gate situation that occurred throughout Korea still hadn’t been completely resolved. Ding, another new matching notification appeared on Kwak Ji-hong’s status window.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon read that in the other person’s silence, anxiety was decreasing and hesitation was filling its place instead.

[“What will you do? Want to go out?”]

The answer from the person with the most timid personality he’d ever seen was:

“Could I… do it?”

The answer to that question was actually already being given by the system first. The automatic matching system’s notification sound rang noisily.

[“If you can’t do it, say so.”]

To Kwak Ji-hong’s ears, that sounded exactly like Gyeon Gang-hyeon didn’t care at all what he did or didn’t do.

[“But once you go out, you can’t return until your assigned gate is cleared. I won’t tolerate going there and doing nothing while dragging down other hunters either. If you’re going to disgrace my face, give up.”]

At that moment, an emotion he’d never felt before colored Kwak Ji-hong’s five senses.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s light tone stirred up resentment. He felt sorry toward him who had no expectations of him, but on the other hand, he was angry.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon, who had been sharply awakening him to reality, exhaled once then suddenly changed his tone.

[“I got cursed out like hell a year ago too. No matter what you do, you’ll be better than I was. And even if things go wrong, I’ll cover for you.”]

Kwak Ji-hong wanted to ask him at this moment:

How did you climb from a year ago to the current Successor Gyeon Gang-hyeon?

He didn’t voice the question. That would really look stupid. Kwak Ji-hong took a deep breath.

Just once, let me break away from being a foolish person.

“Simulation… I did it every week. I want to try going. I want to try doing it.”

From the other end of the communication, a clear, cool smile appeared at Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s lips.

Although right now he was even getting Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s permission for ‘matching consent’ that he could accept by his own will.

Kwak Ji-hong would change even more in the future.

Beep—Suddenly alarm sounds overlapped from Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s status window.

A comment appeared before his eyes. Slightly tired eyes quickly read through the sentences.

Immediately the fatigue completely disappeared from his pupils. Gyeon Gang-hyeon, who had suddenly become alert, unconsciously patted Hae Mi-jun’s arm beside him. He connected communication with someone through his status window.

“Successor Myo Cho-hui. Please return. You’ll switch places with me.”

Surprised Hae Mi-jun looked at Gyeon Gang-hyeon. Even in the midst of this, he couldn’t do things like holding the palm that had been placed on his arm.

“What about me?”

“Sorry. You stay for now. The boss monster’s location is a bit screwed up. I think we’ll have to drill through the ground to get there. Yang Pyo and I will go.”

* * *

Bang! Bang! Outside, the monster’s grotesque arm pounded on the basement level 1 storage room door. The iron door bulged out according to the monster’s arm marks.

“P-please save me. Please save me…”

Park Ho-gyeong, who had nowhere to go with the only escape route blocked, cried under the desk.

He shouldn’t have come to school today. Dormitory life was over and it was vacation, so why did he show off saying he’d meet up with classmates at school?

He resented the dean who had stuck the art college up on a mountain. He also resented that the storage room was underground.

Among them, he resented even more that monster that had dug through the ground like a mole, discovered underground prey, and even broke through the wall to enter.

And most of all, he resented the hunters who hadn’t come to help yet more than anything in the world.

Whoosh!

Something came halfway through the iron door. Park Ho-gyeong made a “hyaak!” sound and collapsed.

So the monster’s arm had finally broken through the door. He squeezed his eyes shut.

But… suddenly the surroundings became quiet. Unbelievably quiet for a monster having squeezed its body into the storage room.

Park Ho-gyeong gulped. He stealthily looked over the desk.

What had come through the door.

That was… a small but sharp arrowhead.

Soon the locked door burst open. Not because all the hinges were torn off, but through the fairly normal method of breaking the door handle to enter.

“Why is this building so deep in the mountains? Are you crazy?”

A loud grumbling voice came inside.

Two earth-covered successors. One was Yang Pyo. And next to him, the other white successor who was quite tall but skinny…

Park Ho-gyeong covered his mouth and listened to their conversation without tension.

“Hey, what do we do about this water pipe and everything? We came through digging ground because it was an emergency. Do we have to pay for this?”

“T-that’s why we came through the path the monster took, right? Then it’s the monster’s fault so it’ll be handled with national funds, right?”

“As expected, we’re a crime combo.”

“Ugh, I don’t like it! I forgot about that incident, okay? And someone’s listening over there!”

“I know too, man.”

As if the joking was just over, Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s expression became serious again.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon approached him closely. Park Ho-gyeong barely faced him while supporting himself on the desk with trembling arms.

“Are you injured anywhere?”

“N-n-no…”

“Then I’ll guide you to the shelter. Are you alone?”

Gyeon Gang-hyeon speaking familiarly according to rescue manual.

He didn’t look ridiculous like Park Ho-gyeong had always thought, nor did he look like an unqualified person wearing a position he didn’t deserve.

He had repelled the boss monster with an arrow and walked in unharmed to save Park Ho-gyeong.
