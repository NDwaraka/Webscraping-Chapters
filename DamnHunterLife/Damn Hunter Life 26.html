As soon as Hae Mi-jun’s healing skill recharged, he teleported to Gang-hyeon’s house.

‘Hyeong-woo hyung said he was taking care of him…’

After the world-spinning sensation passed and he opened his eyes, Hae Mi-jun was in Gang-hyeon’s room.

“You came.”

Hae Mi-jun was startled looking at the bed. Ja Hyeong-woo had removed Gang-hyeon’s upper clothing and laid him down.

He glanced at Ja Hyeong-woo, but his expression was calm. It seemed like Hae Mi-jun was the only one feeling awkward here.

‘If it was someone other than Gang-hyeon hyung, he definitely wouldn’t have touched their clothes…’

Moreover, skin contact isn’t particularly necessary for using healing skills.

Even when Hae Mi-jun trained with Ja Hyeong-woo before and needed contact for attribute guidance, Ja Hyeong-woo politely suggested undressing and kept touching to a minimum.

‘Then is it because he’s close with Gang-hyeon hyung?’

But for that, Ja Hyeong-woo’s expression wasn’t good. Rather than being because Gang-hyeon hyung was hurt, there seemed to be some fundamental distance.

Hae Mi-jun sneakily raised his gaze to face Gang-hyeon again. After all, he was a patient who needed treatment, so he should look properly. That’s how he convinced himself.

The upper body he saw for the first time was heartbreakingly thin.

Yet the body’s form and pale skin tone didn’t make him think it looked bad. Rather—

‘Pink…’

Hae Mi-jun was startled by his own unconscious thought. He lowered his head to hide his rapidly reddening cheeks.

Ja Hyeong-woo seemed not to notice Hae Mi-jun’s condition as he stepped back slightly and gestured forward.

“I healed the damaged insides with my skill. It’s your turn.”

If Ja Hyeong-woo had treated the severely damaged interior, Hae Mi-jun had to restore the depleted stamina so Gang-hyeon could open his eyes and move.

Hae Mi-jun held his palm above Gang-hyeon’s upper chest. Blue light spread evenly over Gang-hyeon’s body.

“Uh…”

But Hae Mi-jun soon looked puzzled. The stamina recovery skill didn’t work on the target.

Ja Hyeong-woo, frowning deeply, stepped closer to examine. Hae Mi-jun trembled like someone who had committed a crime.

“Is, is it because I’m lacking? Then Gang-hyeon hyung because of me…”

Tears welled up instantly, but faster than they could flow down his cheeks, Ja Hyeong-woo identified the cause.

“Look carefully. The light from the skill isn’t absorbing, it’s just floating on top.”

He pointed with his finger and explained quietly.

“It’s not that he’s not getting better despite using the skill, but that it’s not your jurisdiction.”

He was a good healer teacher. Even in this situation, he was calmly giving guidance to Hae Mi-jun as a senior.

According to Ja Hyeong-woo’s words that he’s not a target for skill use, Gang-hyeon’s current state is one of two things. Either Gang-hyeon is already in a healthy state. Or Gang-hyeon’s body is fundamentally in a realm where skills can’t boost vitality…

“I don’t know.”

Unfortunately, there was no way to know the answer right now.

Ja Hyeong-woo judged that instead of skills, nutritional IV drip injections purchasable from the Hunter School shop would be effective. Even though there was nothing else he could particularly do, he stayed by his side the whole time.

Fortunately, Gang-hyeon regained consciousness on his own in less than three days.

My head was too hazy, like I had died and come back to life.

‘It’s because I did too much.’

It was also the first time I had vomited blood like that.

Now having Ja Hyeong-woo by the bedside wasn’t a surprising phenomenon anymore. Ja Hyeong-woo just stared silently as if waiting for me to notice him.

I tried moving my shoulders a little and realized. My upper body was bare.

I flinched and pulled the blanket around my waist to cover my body. Then Ja Hyeong-woo’s expression became strange.

“…”

I know I just did something weird between guys, but it’s embarrassing to be seen by that bastard of all people. Because I’m too white and thin.

“You seem fine with moving. Say anything. Can you speak?”

Maybe because I was a patient, his tone was quite gentle even to me who had that kind of conversation with him in front of the gate.

‘Hmm…’

As the events in front of the gate came back to me, my body, which had seemed fully recovered, trembled minutely again.

The moment I accepted the weight that successors bear in Korea as my own.

The moment I desperately tried to get hurt instead of Ja Hyeong-woo… things like that came to mind.

I turned away from Ja Hyeong-woo’s face.

‘You can curse me as an ungrateful bastard, a bad bastard, I don’t care. Even if you hold a grudge and try to get revenge on me later, I’ll accept it.’

Right now I can’t handle your existence.

“Get out.”

Covering my tired eyes with my arm, I inevitably brought out the most vicious and annoying tone.

“Aren’t you going to train? How long are you going to rest?”

The effect was certain as always. Ja Hyeong-woo’s face, which had been gently relaxed, immediately hardened.

“And I’m telling you again, sleep properly. Don’t collapse again and cause trouble then say it’s because of me.”

Ja Hyeong-woo, who had come close, suddenly pulled the blanket off my body.

“What are you—”

My body briefly shrank from the cold air touching my skin. I tried to cover myself with my arms but Ja Hyeong-woo covered my arms with the blanket too and pulled it up to my neck. Then he glared at me and spoke clearly.

“Even if the sky falls I’ll sleep 6 hours a day, so speak nicely.”

“…This rat bastard.”

Half reflexively, half sincerely, I spat out even harsher words.

As Ja Hyeong-woo left the room, he glanced back from the doorway. His gaze was off to be looking at me, but his eyes were too fierce to be looking somewhere else entirely.

“I’ll wait at the successor training ground.”

I was wondering what he meant and watching quietly when I heard the next words too.

“You awakened. Come try it once. That thing called training.”

…This bastard holds a grudge against me.
