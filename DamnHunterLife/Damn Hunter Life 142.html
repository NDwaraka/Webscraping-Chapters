Damn Hunter Life Chapter 142

* * *

About a month ago, when Woo Sae-geon was still at school.

Woo Sae-geon’s combat instruction at the earth attribute training ground led to healer training to quickly treat Jin Seol-woon, who had become injured from being brutally beaten up.

After Ja Hyeong-woo finished healing at maximum speed and left.

Someone’s presence lingered outside the training room door where Gyeon Gang-hyeon and Ja Hyeong-woo were for quite a while.

Jin Seol-woon had come with the intention of barging in midway, but when it came down to it, he couldn’t bring himself to do it and just paced around in front of the door.

“……”

Through the crack in the door, he had caught sight of the way Gang-hyeon gazed at Ja Hyeong-woo while receiving healing.

‘It wasn’t just Ja Hyeong-woo being one-sidedly swayed around.’

An expression of being stirred up by the other person. Jin Seol-woon knew very well what that was. Even if he hadn’t known before, he knew now.

Because every time Gang-hyeon looked weak and pitiful, he himself wore exactly that expression reflected in the mirror.

Jin Seol-woon looked down at his own innocent hands and thought.

‘If only I were a healer……’

Jin Seol-woon, who had been staring blankly at his hands, bit his lip. His fingertips grew cold from the rising sense of deprivation. But instead of letting his hands fall, he clenched them into fists.

* * *

He lost track of Gang-hyeon midway.

The day after conquering Stage 44, Ja Hyeong-woo was wandering around the school having stayed up all night.

His long black hair, left unbound, fluttered in the cold wind. He wore a black jeogori—or in local terms, just underwear. All the students who passed by and encountered him were startled and whispered among themselves.

Regardless, Ja Hyeong-woo was solely focused on gauging what Gang-hyeon’s physical condition might be.

He had abandoned his training and followed him the whole time, but at some point the trail went cold.

Since the mouse familiar wasn’t attached to him anymore, tracking was impossible. In the end, he had to run around searching on foot.

‘He didn’t leave this building.’

The last place was probably inside this central building.

He had gone in to support a hunter’s training, so it should be ending soon. Not knowing if that spot was in a surveillance camera exposure area, Ja Hyeong-woo waited outside for Gang-hyeon for quite a while.

As the waiting time gradually grew longer, a dark shadow fell across Ja Hyeong-woo’s eyes.

‘What if he collapsed?’

No, there was also the possibility he had moved using the teleportation function. However, since he had already confirmed he wasn’t home, he probably got tired of walking and used Sa Ye-ho or someone else as a teleportation waypoint……

At that moment, Ja Hyeong-woo’s eyes caught sight of a boy coming out of the building.

It was the regular student Gang-hyeon had gone to help train. He strode over and blocked Kwak Ji-hong’s path.

“Gang-hyeon…… Do you know where Gyeon Gang-hyeon is?”

A man as large as a bear rather than a mouse had appeared. A man who had forgotten even his habit of deliberately relaxing his expression in front of young people so they wouldn’t be afraid of him.

Kwak Ji-hong knew who the man in front of him was, which was the only reason he managed to barely hold back from screaming.

“Ah, he left earlier. I…… I’m not really sure.”

“What time?”

It was a time that was already 30 minutes past.

Finally acknowledging that he had completely lost track of him, Ja Hyeong-woo steeled himself for Gang-hyeon’s future annoyance and texted him directly.

When there was no reply even after a long while, he even resorted to the group chat.

==

Mouse Successor: Has anyone seen Gang-hyeon?

==

Soon answers came back one by one. After confirming that only unhelpful messages saying they didn’t know appeared next to each animal name, he looked up at the central building.

After pushing open every training room door and searching the building like hunting for mice, he finally found him. White hands and sleeves that had fallen beneath a chair.

Rushing in so hastily he nearly broke the hinges, Ja Hyeong-woo was blocked once more.

There was a presence standing there, preventing him from approaching any further.

A brown body. A blunt form that wasn’t human. Even though its features weren’t clearly visible, Ja Hyeong-woo recognized that the other party was glaring at him fiercely.

At that moment.

“……!”

A strange neural stimulus rang through his mind, like tinnitus drilling into his ears.

The eerie feeling soon disappeared to the back of his mind, but Ja Hyeong-woo couldn’t take his eyes off the boy for a while.

While thinking ‘Could it be……’, he also nodded in understanding and muttered.

I see.

“You were Gang-hyeon’s familiar. You’ve been by Gang-hyeon’s side all this time.”

He had somewhat expected it when he heard the voice last time.

Since the boy was relatively small in stature, even with his body fully spread out, he couldn’t completely hide Gang-hyeon behind him. Therefore, Ja Hyeong-woo was able to see in full the pitiful sight that completely stole away his composure.

However, the dog familiar was preventing him from getting close.

“Don’t block me. Gang-hyeon is hurt. You know that too.”

The dog familiar said something else. In a very insincere and displeased tone.

{You came to do weird things to Hyeon-i.}

Gang-hyeon’s breathing that reached his ears was faint. Ja Hyeong-woo attempted an urgent persuasion.

“That’s the treatment method. Through those acts, I draw out my yang energy and deliver it to Gang-hyeon through the places we touch, like breathing life into him.”

{Then why didn’t you tell Hyeon-i?}

At the child’s sharp reproach, Ja Hyeong-woo’s movement came to an abrupt halt.

Hah. Only the short breath that Ja Hyeong-woo let out of his own accord remained where his words had been cut off.

“……I was scared. Scared I wouldn’t be accepted.”

Heavy emotions like earth were carried in the boy’s deep exhalation. His faintly visible arms crossed.

{I don’t like you.}

The dog familiar had been rejecting Ja Hyeong-woo from the beginning. Ja Hyeong-woo understood the dog familiar’s feelings well. Because they were his own feelings too.

“I don’t like myself either.”

The two figures soon looked at each other face to face. Their gazes tangled and their perceptions mixed together. As if defying time, space, and existence all at once. As if two became one, and one became two.

{What does it matter whether he accepts you or not? It doesn’t matter at all. What matters is Hyeon-i.}

“Yeah. What matters is Gang-hyeon.”

Different voices overlapped as one.

However, to someone, that sound seemed like Ja Hyeong-woo’s soliloquy from the beginning.

Jin Seol-woon was holding his breath and listening to the sound flowing out through the crack in the closed door from beyond the door.

* * *

When you experience something too incomprehensible, your thoughts stop instead.

Pop, when Ja Hyeong-woo sucked on my lips once and released them, I could painfully realize that fact.

Transparent mucus stretched long from that bastard’s smooth lips before dropping.

His sea-black pupils stared intently at me without even blinking. My dazed eyes were contained within his irises.

The feeling of my heart stopping with a thump. A sinking feeling struck me as if my whole body was plummeting underground.

I pushed against his chest with whatever I could grab and hurriedly backed away. A wall touched my back that I had raised in panic. Cold chill flowed down refreshingly from my back through my whole body.

“What…… what are you doing?”

If that bastard babbled some excuse, would I really be convinced by it? No. That couldn’t happen.

Yet I repeated meaningless words. Ja Hyeong-woo’s silence was unbearable.

“Treatment.”

Ja Hyeong-woo, who was kneeling, had lowered both arms and was gazing at me steadily. Thanks to his long loose hair, he looked like a criminal at first glance, but that was just my wishful thinking—his eyes were calm.

‘Why are you so confident?’

My head spun round and round like before falling asleep.

I raised my hand to cover my lips and rubbed them hard repeatedly, only then realizing my face was burning.

“Crazy bastard. ……You’re a crazy bastard.”

I forced strength into my trembling hands.

Smack! Even after being slapped hard enough to turn his head, Ja Hyeong-woo remained silent. Only my palm stung more.

Now even the back of my neck was all hot. I couldn’t remember when I had felt cold.

Because of that bastard’s attitude—not turning his twisted head back, keeping his gaze in that same spot, as if he would obediently take the beating.

“Sorry I couldn’t tell you beforehand.”

Whether it was an apology or what, I couldn’t even hear such bullshit, and only the lips that spoke those words caught my eye. Remembering how that flesh had held mine made it hard to concentrate. My heart pounded even harder.

“You don’t have to look at my face again. You don’t have to forgive me.”

Ja Hyeong-woo slowly turned his face. Our eyes met.

My heart, which had been beating hard, stopped abruptly.

My breath caught, and an incomprehensible tension seized my body. The breath I slowly and consciously exhaled sounded pathetically ragged even to my own ears.

“But let’s do it just once more right now.”

You’re still hurting. The moment Ja Hyeong-woo whispered earnestly.

I teleported home.

Only after the air changed did I barely open my tightly shut eyes. My head felt dull as if overworked. I felt the contradictory emotions of wanting to do something to ventilate my mind, while on the other hand not wanting to do anything at all.

But today’s rude bastard wasn’t the end of it.

In the place I had fled to without thinking, Jin Seol-woon was leaning against the wall with darkness draped over his shoulders.
