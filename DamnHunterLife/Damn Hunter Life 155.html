Damn Hunter Life Chapter 155

Hae Mi-jun quickly smiled brightly. Gang-hyeon hyung didn’t seem to notice anything strange about his appearance, and since he was smiling, Gang-hyeon raised the corners of his mouth in response.

He was always such a cool person. Even when sad feelings arose, they would fade away, and Hae Mi-jun bit his spoon and smiled weakly.

They finished eating around the same time. Gyeon Gang-hyeon began gathering the trays and utensils with clattering sounds. Hae Mi-jun grabbed Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s hand once and smoothly pushed him aside.

“I’ll clean up! Gang-hyeon hyung, go take care of your work!”

“Really? Thanks.”

If it were before, even knowing Gang-hyeon hyung was busy, he would have said “I’ll bring dessert!” and tried to see him for even a few more minutes.

And Gang-hyeon hyung too—.

“Why aren’t you going?”

“I don’t want to go there because Ja Hyeong-woo is there.”

The image of Gang-hyeon hyung sprawled on the sofa and grumbling was still vivid.

Hae Mi-jun pointlessly looked at the sofa once. Naturally, it was completely empty.

‘Gang-hyeon hyung isn’t angry because he dislikes Hyeong-woo hyung, but because he’s embarrassed.’

He had firmly resolved that he couldn’t give up without even confessing.

Not yet, his skills couldn’t completely heal Gyeon Gang-hyeon yet.

Even if it meant trying to beat Ja Hyeong-woo, someday he wanted to push him aside and claim the spot next to Gang-hyeon.

But even if he really could win someday.

‘…This is right. Gang-hyeon hyung should be with Hyeong-woo hyung.’

Gyeon Gang-hyeon wouldn’t want him.

It’s okay. I’m glad it’s before confessing. Hae Mi-jun constantly repeated to himself.

I would have just made Gang-hyeon hyung uncomfortable, and hyung would have been at a loss, feeling sorry for me.

Hae Mi-jun didn’t want an ending where they’d just watch each other’s reactions and eventually grow distant.

Hae Mi-jun sat alone at the empty dining table. It was the exact spot where Gyeon Gang-hyeon had been sitting. The fact that the warmth remaining in the chair had completely cooled due to the passage of time was unbearably melancholy.

‘I couldn’t do anything for him, couldn’t even convey my feelings.’

‘It’s fortunate that it’s before doing so.’

Two hearts fought and clashed within his chest. It felt like his heart was being torn to shreds. Hae Mi-jun couldn’t stop his emotions from flowing wildly.

Just one more month and he’d be 18.

His height had already grown to match Gang-hyeon hyung’s.

Hah, the air he breathed deeply felt sharp as it blocked his throat. His nose stung. His dazed eyes turned downward, then his mouth opened.

“Hng, huuk, huung…”

Tears came pouring out.

His head felt heavy, and unable to bear it, he buried his forehead in his arms. He sobbed as sadly as a flood. Salty tears flowed endlessly from his poorly twisted mouth.

So that someday he could smile while remembering this moment, he cried to his heart’s content today.

To get straight to the point.

The stage number that went backward wasn’t an error, they said.

When I inquired with the system, it said that number was correct. They didn’t tell me the reason. Systems are always like this.

It was my job to bang my forehead on the desk and agonize.

‘What am I supposed to announce to the public about this…’

I briefly imagined myself making an official announcement.

The numbers are somehow going backward but it’ll probably be fine, I’ll do my best. What else could I say besides the same old nonsense?

After gathering the Twelve Zodiac in the conference room, I shared information about the stage. Since I’m the leader, I maintained as calm an expression as possible.

Everyone was surprised and puzzled, chaotically offering various opinions. It was no different from a class meeting.

“From 1 to 44. It was always consecutive ascending order. Isn’t it a bit early to be shaken just because that rule broke? We should at least see the next number before discussing it.”

Myo Cho-hui, who lacked emotion and didn’t possess fear, currently the eldest.

“Weren’t you very surprised? Are you okay?”

Ja Hyeong-woo, who didn’t know what the point was…

“If what should have been 45 became 33, could there be a numerical correlation between the two numbers?”

And model student Hae Mi-jun, who seemed to be drifting off topic somehow.

“Hae Mi-jun, don’t think about other things and just stay put. This is something for me and the big guys to figure out.”

While scribbling notes with my pen, I habitually stroked Hae Mi-jun’s head with my other hand.

Flinch.

“…Why?”

Hae Mi-jun noticeably startled, so I whipped around. Ah, no sir. Hae Mi-jun flailed and quickly waved his hands.

His downcast expression bothered me somehow and I was about to look more closely when Yang Pyo’s gloomy voice stopped me.

“The system must be broken. Or maybe we who enter the stages are broken. Something’s definitely broken…”

As expected, he was scared out of his wits and talking nonsense.

Because of Yang Pyo, I had to pull myself together and put on airs. Pretending that strange numbers were no big deal. Pretending to be prepared.

Then Ma Cheon-ah from the side crossed her arms and glanced at Yang Pyo.

“Why do you only think negatively? The previous stage 33 might appear as is. Then it would be super easy, right? What were the monsters back then?”

In situations like this, Ma Cheon-ah’s baseless confidence is helpful. I looked at Ma Cheon-ah fondly.

“Well said, Ma Cheon-ah. You review stage 33 again and bring me a summary.”

“Ugh…”

After giving a glance to Won Ha-ri, who was keeping her mouth tightly shut and clearly anxious, I immediately turned to Sa Ye-ho.

“Ye-ho, call Woo Sae-geon and ask what he thinks about this.”

“Hyung said if I ask one more future-oriented question like that, he’ll spit and disappear.”

“Just try it. I’ll chase him to America.”

Finally, I stared in Jin Seol-woon’s direction to hear his opinion.

Our gazes met quietly for a while. His lips, which had been staring at me intently, slowly parted.

“I’m starting to think the end might be approaching.”

The kids who had been chattering distractedly stopped abruptly. Heads turned toward him one by one.

Jin Seol-woon looked around at everyone with a businesslike attitude.

“It went from 44 to 33. If this isn’t an error… my next predicted number is 22. After that, 11. The last would be 0.”

0, meaning nothing.

A number that had never been used anywhere. Jin Seol-woon mentioned the possibility of stage 0.

“…It would have been good if Leader Woo Sae-geon were here.”

Yang Pyo said weakly. Though he immediately got his mouth slapped by Ma Cheon-ah’s palm.

Even hearing words seeking the previous leader’s presence to my face, I wasn’t particularly bothered.

Actually, I too think of that old fart whenever I’m at a loss.

Except for me, Sa Ye-ho, Ja Hyeong-woo, and Myo Cho-hui, everyone seemed to think Jin Seol-woon’s hypothesis was plausible. The small eyes all looked at me uniformly.

I half-rose from my seat while raising the corners of my mouth.

“Whatever, whether it’s stage 0 or whatever, we’ll just smash it all. It’s good if we can see the end.”

Though I said that.

My true feelings could only be brought out when the meeting ended and I was alone in the office.

‘Could we really be heading toward the original story’s final chapter?’

That very final chapter I hadn’t been able to read.

‘Moreover, now that the cheat key called the original story has been exhausted… couldn’t it be said that I’m finally truly living as Gyeon Gang-hyeon?’

Because ordinary people don’t know the future that will befall them.

Not knowing is natural order, and rather, I who had known the future had been deviating from providence all this time.

‘But what the hell is the number 11?’

The stage numbers estimated to decrease in units of 11.

It’s an ambiguous number. If it were 12, I would have immediately thought of the Twelve Zodiac.

Whine. The doggy who had been serving as a foot warmer under my feet squirmed sleepily.

I looked down at the brown fur ball under the chair. Just as I was only watching since patting with my foot seemed inappropriate.

Suddenly, a realization struck my mind. My heart dropped with a thud.

11th. The dog’s number in the Twelve Zodiac.

‘It was… pointing to me.’

The first stage number I faced after becoming leader was 44.

And the numbers began rewinding backward. Centered on me.

The office scenery began spinning round and round.

The ceiling and floor mixed together. Furniture like the sofa lost their forms.

My vision, which had gone completely white for a moment, soon drew out a different scene.

A black gate that looked like a black hole. Ja Hyeong-woo standing alone.

It was a sight I’d seen before. A memory I’d put aside because I couldn’t understand its meaning. Some scene that might be a prophetic dream or might not be, or perhaps a disappeared future.

‘Could there be a reason for seeing the same scene multiple times?’

I thought that maybe this was the latter part of the original story I hadn’t been able to see.

Only after properly looking around at the surrounding environment did I realize.

This isn’t South Korea.

This was inside a different space, inside a stage.

‘Then what is that black entrance? Is there another gate inside a gate?’

Important information. Though I couldn’t be certain since I hadn’t read it, if this really was the original story’s final chapter, I had to see everything without missing anything. I forced my brain to remember.

But something was strange. This is a stage, a place only successors can enter.

Then these bloodstains scattered everywhere…

A chill rose in my heart. That vivid color approached as terror that could stop one’s breath. I raised my hand to cover my mouth. I wanted to cover my eyes more than watch.

I absolutely didn’t want to believe that my team members could die.
