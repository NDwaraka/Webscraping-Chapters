Damn Hunter Life Chapter 132

The handwritten text was persuading the Association in an extremely calm tone about why Gyeon Gang-hyeon should succeed him, and why he had personally chosen Gyeon Gang-hyeon.

====

  1. Successor Gyeon Gang-hyeon is familiar with all successors’ skills and excels at utilizing skills appropriately according to combat situations.
  2. Successor Gyeon Gang-hyeon possesses fusion skills and has skills comparable to the advanced skills of former Korea #1 Successor Woo Sae-geon. While there are concerns about insufficient physical abilities, this can be supplemented through characteristics.
  3. Successor Gyeon Gang-hyeon is the only one capable of emotional exchange with all successors.

====

Woo Sae-geon put down his pen and briefly recalled the faces of others. The young successors who all froze up just from seeing his face.

In contrast, Gyeon Gang-hyeon got along comfortably with the young successors and put them at ease, brought Jin Seol-woon into training. He gained Myo Cho-hui’s acceptance and Sa Ye-ho liked him.

Crucially, he could handle the next Korea #1, the most outstanding healer.

‘When it was time to catch mice, that’s finished. Now that it’s stabilized, it’s time to hand it over to a new flow.’

However, Woo Sae-geon didn’t know. That now wasn’t a stable period, and the current peace was merely the calm before the storm of what would come later.

Of course, even if he had known, in his situation of having to leave, he would have chosen Gyeon Gang-hyeon again.

* * *

“Hehe.”

On Woo Sae-geon’s last day, Sa Ye-ho finally succeeded in forcing his way into his house.

Woo Sae-geon’s house maintained the original hanok structure intact. Perfect for supporting the scholar image he had been portraying.

As soon as he entered his private space, Sa Ye-ho looked around eagerly.

Sa Ye-ho’s persistent gaze, which had been that of someone indulging, stopped abruptly upon discovering Woo Sae-geon sitting in a chair with his legs crossed.

On his exposed bare shoulder blade were bird wings, and on the trapezius muscle leading to his neck were a few sprigs of unnamed wildflowers. With his hair loosely disheveled, holding a drawing pencil and legs crossed, Woo Sae-geon wasn’t hiding himself, wearing only a white undergarment.

Sa Ye-ho became curious about who had tattooed those designs. But he didn’t ask.

“…Are you drawing?”

It was a line he couldn’t cross, a curiosity he shouldn’t have about Woo Sae-geon.

To be allowed by his side.

On his last day as a successor, finally putting down training, he spent time drawing on canvas on an easel. A black and white drawing with intricate patterns was carved on the white paper. It was a tattoo design.

Sa Ye-ho played beside him as always.

“Today marks the end, and Sae-geon hyung returns to being an ordinary boy again…”

Playing magical boy games too.

“No, listen. Hyung, so I said really loudly, ‘Why the hell would I say such bullshit?’ But it’s not like it’s the first or second time I’ve raised my voice on broadcasts. GO Ye-ho the human siren, right? So—”

Chattering away with one-sided conversation.

As if bothering Woo Sae-geon was his job, he fully displayed his natural talent for being charming beside him.

Then, receiving Woo Sae-geon’s look that said ‘pack up and get out,’ he answered “Okay” as he always did.

Though he acted as usual, Sa Ye-ho actually knew well that he wouldn’t be at the school anymore.

He got up and showed his back to Woo Sae-geon as he left, then looked back slightly as if he had forgotten something.

“Hyung, happy 24th birthday.”

Returning home, Sa Ye-ho fidgeted for a long time with the high-proof bottles in his wine display.

He picked one up, put it back down, went to the bathroom and came back to take it out again, then just left it, repeating this.

Finally deciding, he grasped a bottle and burned open the cork. He trudged over and settled on the sofa. He brought the opening to his lips.

Through his half-closed eyelids, Sa Ye-ho saw his back.

“…!”

Suddenly Sa Ye-ho bolted upright and poured all the alcohol into the sink. His face was severely distorted as he threw the bottle with a clang.

[‘Teleporting to ‘Ox’ Successor.]

With his head burning white, he couldn’t even see the other letters clearly as he brought up the status window.

[Invalid command. Cancelled.]

“Ah, fuck…!”

Sa Ye-ho turned and ran out.

He tore the annoyingly obstructive hanbok skirt that he wanted to kill and immediately kicked the door. Fabric fluttered wildly behind him.

While running across the courtyard, past scenes unfolded in Sa Ye-ho’s mind.

That special moment of that day, not faded at all, remaining intact like a photograph.

‘Successor? What’s that? Fucking interesting. Like a game.’

Maybe because he lived playing games so much, even when others were shocked by gates and such, he adapted quickly.

While wandering around the chaotic school looking for other humans called successors, his body was suddenly yanked behind a corner.

The hard, strong grip that covered his wide-eyed mouth.

Black hair poured over Sa Ye-ho’s face as he looked up.

“Don’t make a fuss. You must be one of those successor things too.”

A fully exposed forehead and straight black hair. Thick, masculine neckline, and tattoos slightly visible outside a black turtleneck.

“From now on, follow my instructions. Answer what I ask and bring what I tell you to bring.”

Sa Ye-ho looked up at the face with dark features that would make him look like a complete kid standing next to him, then nodded without a word.

Having lived in America all along, he spoke Korean well but didn’t know much about how Korea worked. Sa Ye-ho filled in his empty parts from beside him. And followed him around.

Woo Sae-geon probably didn’t know the guy he grabbed in passing would be such a chatterbox.

To others’ eyes, they probably looked quite close.

…But all of this was a relationship that would end when being successors ended.

Soon someone’s back came into Sa Ye-ho’s view.

Wearing only the clothes he had when he first came to school, quietly heading toward the entrance, refusing anyone’s send-off.

‘There’s a line that absolutely shouldn’t be crossed. The moment it’s perceived as genuine, he’ll stiffen and avoid me.’

Feeling a rare heaviness in his life, Sa Ye-ho pulled a shout from his throat.

“Hyung!”

In the scene of him turning around, Sa Ye-ho felt fear for the first time in his life.

And even greater thirst.

“Don’t you have to go to America?”

Silence came to the empty space. The sound of Sa Ye-ho gasping for breath echoed in the air.

Woo Sae-geon’s seemingly emotionless gaze cut through the space between them.

* * *

His hands and feet were all trembling. Gyeon Gang-hyeon was in a state of seclusion due to unprecedented anxiety symptoms.

Because Woo Sae-geon had declared he would pass the leader position to him.

‘No. It’s not fucked yet. There’s the successor vote! Ma Cheon-ah and kids like that will oppose it.’

Gyeon Gang-hyeon, clinging to one strand of hope, bolted upright.

After some time passed, a notice arrived with the signature results of all successors. Gyeon Gang-hyeon opened it with more tension than college entrance exam scores.

And despair overwhelmed him.

==

Ja Hyeong-woo [Agree]

Myo Cho-hui [Agree]

Jin Seol-woon [Agree]

Sa Ye-ho [Agree]

Ma Cheon-ah [Agree]

Yang Pyo [Agree]

Won Ha-ri [Agree]

Gyeon Gang-hyeon [Disagree]

Hae Mi-jun [Agree]

==

‘Why… why?’

Though he had lived as a scorned delinquent until now, he had never felt such alienation. Gyeon Gang-hyeon was so shocked he trembled as if about to faint.

But to go question the others…

Sa Ye-ho would fan the flames saying ‘Giggle congratulations!’, or Hae Mi-jun would say ‘I also voted for Gang-hyeon hyung!’ with such a blissful sunny expression that he couldn’t curse at a smiling face, or he’d get furious seeing Ma Cheon-ah ruining the school with her reckless vote saying ‘Well, the dog seemed most manageable.’

In a situation full of landmines, Gyeon Gang-hyeon realized there was only one person from whom he could hear a properly serious answer.

He forcibly used teleportation on Ja Hyeong-woo.

“Hey!”

A face suddenly encountered.

Water dripped from Ja Hyeong-woo’s drooping hair as he blinked his wide eyes.

Precisely because of such emergencies, mutual prior agreement for teleportation between successors is usually strongly recommended.

If Gyeon Gang-hyeon had been just one minute earlier, Ja Hyeong-woo would have been completely naked from head to toe. By a hair’s breadth, he had just finished showering and wrapped a towel around his waist.

‘Should have not worn the towel. …No, it’s good he did.’

He had hoped to see his entire naked body and make him flustered, but thinking again, it was better this way. He would have been caught getting aroused.
