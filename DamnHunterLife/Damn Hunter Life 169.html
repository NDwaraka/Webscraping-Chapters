Damn Hunter Life Chapter 169

There still aren’t any signs of something trying to attack me yet.

I examined the front and rear more closely while asking Myo Cho-hui if exploration skills were possible.

[“No good. Something is blocking power like in illusion gates.”]

Just like a stage with a thousand-year-old fox.

Team members scattered by the boss. In a situation where they don’t know their own location or others’ locations, they need to gather in one place.

At least it was fortunate that I alone didn’t get bounced to another place. I was still securing the stage entrance from where I originally was.

‘So I should gather them using myself as the reference point.’

I thought of a good idea.

Even someone as unusual as me can’t summon successors, but I can call familiars, right?

“Everyone call out your familiars and get on top. I’m going to make them all return to where I am.”

Going beyond controlling others’ familiars, it’s temporarily attributing them to me as if I were their owner.

Have you ever seen a familiar look at maps to find their way back to their owner? Of course they return on their own.

When the explanation ended, the team members stirred once.

[“Is that possible? Since when?”]

“It’s possible.”

Because after nearly dying and coming back to life, all my abilities expanded beyond before.

I need to call six in total, right? After counting the order, I emanated energy from my body and shouted at the top of my lungs.

“Squeaky! Hoppy! Draggy! Neighy! Monky! Piggy! Come back!”

The result was success.

I can feel the familiars moving from far away. At this rate, they’ll smoothly gather where I am.

But even though things are clearly going well, the communication is strangely buzzing.

[“The rabbit is Hoppy, and the horse goes neigh so it’s Neighy?”]

It seemed like no one except me liked the names.

At best, the sounds Jin Seol-woon, Hae Mi-jun, and Ja Hyeong-woo made were closer to just laughter than mockery…

[“I’m shocked right now.”]

[“Be honest. How many seconds did it take you to think up those names?”]

Ma Cheon-ah and Won Ha-ri spoke up. Jeers poured from the two, but I didn’t yield.

“What’s wrong with them? Aren’t they cute?”

[“They’re cute.”]

It was a comment Ja Hyeong-woo, who had been keeping his mouth shut, tossed out.

A brief silence flowed. The atmosphere suddenly became strange.

In my flustered state, I said “Those kinds of names are cute? What’s wrong with your sense?” to recover.

Anyway, I waited for the familiars to arrive in the forest that had become a complete mess from Won Ha-ri’s rampage.

Not even a few minutes passed before a bright blue form burst out from between the trees. I doubted my eyes the moment I saw it.

‘The mouse arrived first…?’

The mouse is the smallest among the Twelve Zodiac familiars, so it can’t carry Ja Hyeong-woo.

So the best Ja Hyeong-woo could do was run following the mouse familiar as it rushed toward me.

That’s why I thought their joining would be the latest.

But unexpectedly, even while the mouse rubbed its head against me, Ja Hyeong-woo was nowhere to be seen.

“Crazy bastard.”

Even during this, the mouse was showing me all kinds of affection.

Isn’t this character collapse…? The image of the proud blue boy crumbles more and more in my mind day by day. I wonder if that was all a dream.

“Are you being affectionate because your body is far away? Why are you acting so unlike yourself.”

I rubbed the mouse’s head with my hand.

How long was I doing that? Suddenly, the mouse turned its back and blocked in front of me. It bristled its fur while fiercely spewing water energy.

“……”

I didn’t waver. I silently nocked an arrow to my bow and drew the string. I aimed in the direction the mouse was looking.

The string trembled at my fingertips as if ready to spring out at any moment.

A pure white fox was among the trees. The blue energy it emitted was like poison.

{I finally meet you, whom I’ve only heard about in stories.}

A androgynous voice that was neither male nor female echoed in my head.

This boss monster also spoke to me. Moreover.

‘It heard a lot about me…?’

My spine felt eerie. A vague fear struck me that something was happening somewhere I didn’t know about.

Nine lush tails swayed from the fox’s body. The mouse trembled as it showed even more wariness, but the fox’s voice continued.

{Humans called me by the lowly name Maegu, but in truth, a fox that has lived a thousand years is a god.}

It had just spilled important information from its own mouth.

‘God, my ass. More important than that bullshit.’

A god is ultimately a ghost too. This boss’s nature is spiritual.

No different from the water ghost that stared at me, the fire ghost that avoided me, the boss that spoke to me.

The fox’s bulging eyes stared at me. Feeling like I’d be sucked in, I put more strength into the hand holding the bow.

Twang! I released the string pretending it was a mistake.

However, the arrow imbued with intermediate skills was lightly blocked. The arrow that had reached right before the fox’s nose fell powerlessly with a thud.

{You live up to your bewitching appearance. Isn’t the power you gained by offering your body to a healer troublesome?}

The skin from the edge of my cheek began hardening stiffly.

To hear such insulting words right to my face, from a monster no less, not even a person.

{Why do you maintain life so difficultly? You should have laid down your flesh long ago.}

The mouse’s body jumped. It was right before springing out. The will to try biting even if it, a healer’s familiar, died painfully was widespread.

I forcibly stretched my unmoving legs to block the mouse.

…Should I be thankful that it’s a ghost’s voice so others can’t hear it? I was alone anyway.

But then, someone walked out from behind.

“Why should my leader have to hear such words? From a filthy monster?”

I turned my head stiffly.

It was a bright red horse. Its mane made of sparks fluttered.

Ma Cheon-ah, who jumped down from the horse familiar, dragged her butcher knife across the ground with a scraping sound. The knife was soon lifted horizontally. She aimed at Maegu.

“You… heard?”

I hadn’t realized. That Maegu’s voice could be heard by someone other than me.

My heart pounded as if a big secret had been discovered. Ma Cheon-ah glanced at me as she passed by. She stood protecting in front of me.

Fire attribute that handles mobility among the five attributes. Among them, Chollima is the fastest.

Excluding the mouse that wasn’t carrying its owner, the horse would have to arrive like lightning and stand at attention in front of me next.

Fast enough for Ma Cheon-ah to interrupt the conversation.

“…How much did you hear?”

As soon as another successor appeared, Maegu shut its mouth like a lie. The light in its eyes also disappeared, so now Maegu just looked like a wild beast.

“The recently surging power was strange, but even without that power originally, a leader is a leader.”

It seems she heard everything.

But Ma Cheon-ah was calm. While it was unpleasant that she had an unsuitably serious face, heat rose to my head.

“You suit each other. There’s nothing strange about it.”

My cheeks were on the verge of turning red. I barely managed to turn my chin away.

“Talk with me after the clearing ends.”

“As you wish.”

Signs that familiars carrying other successors were also gradually arriving could be seen. I buried what Maegu had said in the back of my mind and prepared for battle for now.

* * *

The familiars arrived one after another. Especially when Won Ha-ri, the natural enemy of wood, arrived, we could capture Maegu—or god—without much difficulty.

‘It only had a big mouth, it wasn’t very strong.’

Just because it said meaningful things to confuse me and left me with annoying problems as a bonus.

‘Have I ever heard someone say I offered my body in my life? And where there were ears to hear it too.’

After Stage 22 was safely completed, I ended up calling Ma Cheon-ah aside.

I explained what the thing about doing something with a healer meant, and how I was maintaining my body and living.

Even for me, it was quite bewildering to confess to a woman, especially someone I’d fought and growled at like that.

Even after hearing everything, Ma Cheon-ah didn’t show any particular reaction. I thought she’d call me a pervert, or find me disgusting…

“Well, that can happen. I’ll keep it secret.”

Instead, only I was bewildered.

Even considering that she’d matured a bit, what is this matter-of-fact attitude?

I was anxious because I couldn’t properly grasp Ma Cheon-ah’s thoughts. When I squinted and stared at her, she somehow took that and suddenly said:

“If you’re really anxious, want to trade for my secret?”

Then she started telling her own story without being asked.
