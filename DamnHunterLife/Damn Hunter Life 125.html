The legendary Korean monster Gangcheol was an imugi. A dragon that failed to become complete.

The characteristics described varied by document—in some places it was depicted as an imugi that brought heavy rains to kill crops, while in others it was shown burning things to death with fire. Thanks to this, even before entering there had been much debate about whether Gangcheol was water attribute or fire attribute.

Since it was impossible to know which was correct until seeing it directly inside the stage, Woo Sae-geon had gathered both earth and water attributes.

And the result was:

“It’s fire attribute. Ja Hyeong-woo, focus on debuffs. Hae Mi-jun will handle healing.”

Woo Sae-geon commanded.

On the white snowy mountain, imugi as large as anacondas reared up into the sky and crashed down to the ground repeatedly. Wherever their tails struck, flames erupted and snow melted instantly, revealing sticky bare earth.

“If the small fry are this formidable, how big will the boss be?”

But before that, there was a more serious problem.

‘Wouldn’t it be less cold if I went close to the imugi and fought in melee?’

The heat that the black durumagi had been holding was now completely cooled. I was pretending to be calm while drawing my bow and shooting at the imugi, but my arms were trembling from the cold. My aim kept slipping slightly.

I tried to stick close to Ma Cheon-ah with her fire attribute because of the cold, but Ma Cheon-ah was the type to dart around everywhere like Hong Gil-dong and had already disappeared.

‘Then at least Hae Mi-jun…’

I turned my gaze while shivering. Hae Mi-jun, seeing my face, was startled and quickly embraced me to comfort me against the cold. During this time I kept feeling gazes from behind, but I stubbornly didn’t turn around and just sent chats instead.

Anyway, I had a separate purpose. While pretending to be annoyed, I whispered in Hae Mi-jun’s ear.

“Hae Mi-jun, give me some water.”

“Huh? Ah, yes.”

As if I would ask for water because I’m thirsty in this urgent situation.

Actually, I was deliberately pressing my body against his excessively to receive Hae Mi-jun’s water energy. How fortunate that it’s Hae Mi-jun who’s yin-natured, not Ja Hyeong-woo.

Hae Mi-jun quickly understood my meaning and placed his hand on the cold skin of my wrist. He didn’t forget to cover it with his wide sleeve so it wouldn’t be filmed. A cool sensation spread through the warm energy.

It’s clearly water energy that feels cold even in the middle of this winter wind blowing fiercely, but strangely I don’t feel cold. Does it not steal my body’s heat?

Anyway, there was a reason I specifically asked Hae Mi-jun for energy. Even <Grave of the Dead>, which I’d used so much that its effects were already familiar, becomes completely different like a whole other skill when water energy is fused with it.

‘It used to be a skill that could match Woo Sae-geon’s intermediate skills even when used alone, but Woo Sae-geon’s intermediate became advanced and advanced became supreme, so only I became trash…’

But it’s different when the premise of mixing other energies comes in.

An arrow of an unheard-of color with water and earth mixed in shot out among the usual fire skills.

Ma Cheon-ah, who had been riding her horse well through the burning fields, suddenly hesitated. She looked up at my arrow in surprise, then quickly came to her senses and focused on battle again.

Indeed, the imugi that hadn’t budged at regular arrows were being pushed back hard by each fusion arrow. The flames burning on their bodies died down as if cold water had been poured on them.

Here, additionally:

‘Should I add it there?’

I nocked a bare arrow and shot it to lightly graze Ma Cheon-ah’s greatsword just as she was about to bind and subdue an imugi.

‘I’m guessing that while fire attributes are similar to each other, if I mix earth into it, it’ll gain solidity.’

If I add my skill to someone else’s skill on the spot, the opponent’s skill becomes more powerful and I don’t have to worry about my body getting hurt from receiving someone’s yin energy.

The greatsword that absorbed the earth energy from my arrow successfully captured the imugi. Flames erupted from the imugi’s tail tip and attacked Ma Cheon-ah, but she succeeded in enduring without being pushed back even by fire hotter than her own.

“I’m going to do it alone! Can’t you stay still?”

Ma Cheon-ah seemed to be grumbling about something, but it’s not my concern. I pretended not to know and played dumb.

Around that time, Woo Sae-geon, who had declared he would increase the clearing speed, looked at Ja Hyeong-woo.

“I’m going to dive in now. Alone.”

It was just Woo Sae-geon speaking in his characteristically domineering way, but I suddenly stopped at a certain sense of déjà vu.

“Focus on me. Don’t miss any attack. If even one grazes me, I’m finished.”

A strategy where he would change the system so Ja Hyeong-woo would do wound recovery instead of stamina recovery—specifically predictive healing—while Woo Sae-geon himself would pierce more sharply between the monsters.

It was natural that Ja Hyeong-woo was familiar with it since he’d been refining it all along… but this time what felt familiar to me was Woo Sae-geon’s lines. After searching my memory briefly, I soon remembered what it was.

‘I’ve seen this in the original work.’

The original story had been greatly twisted due to my existence, and reality had roughly caught up to the mid-story content I knew, so my reserves were almost exhausted. At such a time, this was a rare taste of foresight.

‘Right now it’s Woo Sae-geon doing the dangerous thing, but surprisingly it’s the part where Ma Cheon-ah causes a major accident.’

While pretending to shoot arrows appropriately, I watched Ma Cheon-ah.

“Cheon-a! Please move around moderately!”

That lightning bolt was getting excited because the opposing imugi looked cool, ignoring Yang Pyo who was breaking out in cold sweat trying to cover for her while she ran around wildly.

‘When Woo Sae-geon starts slicing up the imugi with his twin swords, she’ll also get excited seeing that and bounce around more until she gets seriously injured.’

Everything that happens in stages is fully disclosed to the public. And in that particular stage, Ma Cheon-ah suffers severe injuries with her right arm ruptured and half her back covered in burns.

By fire, her own attribute, no less.

“Aaaaah!”

In the original work, Hae Mi-jun’s anesthesia skill hadn’t manifested, so there was no way to ease Ma Cheon-ah’s pain. Because of this, Ja Hyeong-woo drew up all his energy to heal and broke his limits once more.

Ma Cheon-ah, who became completely cowed after that, ended up bowing her head to Ja Hyeong-woo who had treated her. Ultimately, this entire process was an episode designed to make Ja Hyeong-woo the next leader.

‘It’s an important point where Ma Cheon-ah, who never listened even when others told her to be careful, first feels a threat to her life and grows. It helps Ja Hyeong-woo too.’

From any angle, it’s beneficial to leave things as they are. So I shouldn’t interfere…

“…Gang-hyeon hyung, are you alright?”

Hae Mi-jun, who had been spreading stamina heals, suddenly asked me with concern. Only then did I relax my stiffened expression. I shook my head roughly.

Right, this won’t do.
