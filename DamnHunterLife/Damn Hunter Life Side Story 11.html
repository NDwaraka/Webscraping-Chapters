Damn Hunter Life Side Story Chapter 11

GO Ye-ho arrived a bit late to the opening ceremony, saying he suddenly had something to discuss with the video editor for his broadcast. GO Ye-ho, who entered the VIP seats with light steps, widened his eyes as soon as he saw me.

“Whoa.”

I felt a bit embarrassed and pushed Ja Hyeong-woo forward instead of myself.

“How’s our boy?”

I had deliberately stopped by a hair salon and made Ja Hyeong-woo expose half his forehead and even get his hair blow-dried. GO Ye-ho looked Ja Hyeong-woo up and down.

“What’s this? You actually made him presentable? I thought he’d come in a tracksuit.”

Only then did GO Ye-ho’s eyes return to normal. GO Ye-ho ignored Ja Hyeong-woo’s gaze and sat in the seat next to me on the opposite side.

We planned to return after just watching the opening ceremony. After exchanging brief greetings with several Association people, while Ja Hyeong-woo and I were resting in the waiting room, GO Ye-ho went to request teleportation from the staff.

“Gang-hyeon!”

Right then, a young man’s voice with clumsy pronunciation burst in.

Seeing the man approaching quickly with an obviously delighted expression, I suddenly felt dazed as if I’d returned to two years ago.

A tall foreigner with green eyes. It was Agni. The fire-attribute gunner hunter.

The guy who got entangled with me badly during the friendly match, and after I somehow learned about his family circumstances, suddenly confessed and hit me hard in the back of the head.

“Wait—”

Agni, who had approached in almost a single bound, lightly embraced me.

Fire flared up in Ja Hyeong-woo’s eyes as he failed to stop it in time. I broke out in a cold sweat. I struggled weakly and escaped from Agni’s embrace.

“It’s been a long time. You look really cool today. Did you come to watch the opening ceremony?”

Does this guy still like me…?

Agni smiled brightly. His eyes were dripping with obvious affection toward me.

Ja Hyeong-woo’s eyes grew increasingly cold. Still, thanks to my training him all this time, he didn’t do anything rash here where he needed to maintain his public image.

…Right, I can’t just vaguely hide this and let it pass. That would be rude to both of them.

Even if this spreads to the public, since I decided to say it, I have to take responsibility.

“I’m dating him now. Don’t just hug me like that.”

I pointed to Ja Hyeong-woo and confessed honestly to Agni.

I was worried about hurting his feelings. But Agni, rather than being disappointed, actually brightened up.

“Back then I didn’t know you could date men.”

“Uh… that’s how it turned out.”

And then he surprised me instead.

Agni just said “I see” without much fuss. He flashed a refreshing smile, and that was the end of it.

“We don’t have each other’s contact information and you’ve retired, but I guess there’s still such a thing as fate. If you ever come to travel to America, contact me! I’ll guide you around with no ulterior motives.”

There wasn’t the troublesome situation I’d expected.

Agni quickly left, saying he had to go to his guild.

It seemed like we parted on good terms, but Ja Hyeong-woo thought differently than I did. As soon as we were alone, he brought his face close to mine and got heated.

“He thinks he has slightly more of a chance with you than before. At least since you’re seeing a man now. That’s why he’s smiling.”

“Isn’t that too paranoid?”

I felt awkward and grumbled back sulkily.

“No, I’m also a black-hearted bastard so I know. Don’t deal with that guy ever again.”

“Alright, alright.”

When I raised my hand and patted his head a few times, his sharp eyes immediately softened. Though his expression still looked displeased.

Soon GO Ye-ho returned. It was fortunate that bastard didn’t see Agni.

“We’re teleporting within 5 minutes. Anyone who needs to use the bathroom should go now.”

“Why are you using teleportation like it’s an express bus?”

Anyway, let’s go home. Ja Hyeong-woo also seemed to want us to be alone soon.

I saw him trying to scrunch up his dress shoes because they were uncomfortable.

“Since we got all dressed up, want to go to a nice bar? We don’t have to watch the match but we can drink.”

But it wouldn’t be GO Ye-ho if he disbanded like this.

I narrowed my eyes.

“Do you want to get me drunk?”

“Now everyone knows that’s not your drunk behavior.”

Meanwhile, 5 minutes had already passed. The three people’s bodies became translucent for a moment.

We were transported with a feeling of being sucked somewhere.

“…?”

I blinked at the unfamiliar scenery.

Had the Korean Hunter Association already demolished their building and built a circular stadium and colosseum on the empty lot… as if.

“What’s this? Where are we?”

GO Ye-ho frowned as he looked around.

It seemed there was an error in the teleportation destination. Though it wasn’t a completely strange place – we seemed to have arrived at one of the empty Olympic stadiums…

‘…Wait, could the competition event be?’

Suddenly my spine went cold. So this Olympics isn’t ordinary competition, is it.

For example, a stadium background that reproduced a vast forest as is.

And the presence of other life forms we could sense besides us.

“Gulp.”

GO Ye-ho swallowed his breath. Ja Hyeong-woo pulled me into his arms without changing expression. His eyes held a sharp glint as he glared over there.

A body that looked completely burned and blackened, with unusually long arms and legs. Green saliva dripped from prominently protruding fangs.

It seemed to be a combat event stadium that used captured monsters. No wonder it felt ominous.

Watching that, I suddenly let out a hollow laugh.

I had unconsciously tried to open my inventory. The embarrassment and emptiness I felt when I realized it were entirely mine to bear.

It felt strange to encounter monsters after so long. Like I’d been max level, deleted my character, and returned to a newbie account.

I poked GO Ye-ho’s side.

“You’re the most active one among us. Do something about it. You were even a leader? Amazing, right?”

“Hey, I’m a civilian, aren’t I? I only became leader because someone forced me into it!”

GO Ye-ho is an attention seeker among attention seekers, but he actually doesn’t like stepping up for weighty responsibilities like being the Twelve Zodiac leader.

This is something you’d never know just from the outside. He liked giving orders in games, but that was just games after all.

“Hey, then you want me to solve this? I had the shortest term in power.”

“But you did the biggest thing! Don’t you remember when the final stage was broadcast, the whole nation was only calling your name?”

Ah, that damn edited version.

Now it’s all black history. Because Ja Hyeong-woo often (no, quite frequently…) plays it at home to watch.

Ja Hyeong-woo recently discovered the internet.

He usually spent his daily internet surfing time attacking old posts that cursed me in the Twelve Zodiac community and clicking ‘like’ on posts that fangirled over me.

==

—Incompetent bastardㅗㅗㅗ

└That would be you.

==

He’s really like a crazy person, so these days I monitor him every time Ja Hyeong-woo turns on the computer. Such is my fate.

Anyway, we cautiously backed away while being wary of the monsters. Had we moved about three or four steps away? The monsters that had been staring at us intently and gauging us suddenly started charging at us.

“Ahhhhh!”

GO Ye-ho screamed like a fragile civilian and grabbed me tightly.

Ja Hyeong-woo, obviously seeing I’d fall behind, suddenly lifted me up and ran while carrying me. I just obediently clung to Ja Hyeong-woo’s neck.

Even though GO Ye-ho had lost his hunter abilities, his mobility was still the strongest among us, so he quickly ran ahead of us. Though it was a breathless situation where every second counted, we three noisily kept trying to push responsibility onto others.

“You’re the most energetic, go do some assassination!”

“Give me a weapon before saying that!”

“Damn it, someone give me a bow! This is so frustrating!”

“If we survive this, let’s go to an archery cafe together, Gang-hyeon.”

“Did Ja Hyeong-woo just set a death flag?”

“Let’s go without GO Ye-ho.”

We passed hundreds of artificial trees. As we ran, somehow the monsters kept increasing.

And we finally came face to face with one wall of the stadium.

Several staff members who had been outside the stadium discovered us and rushed over. But they weren’t awakened either, so they couldn’t immediately do anything to help. The staff members clutched their heads and stamped their feet beyond the transparent wall.

“You can’t come in here! How did you get in! Who are you people?”

“Look here! We don’t want to be here either! Call your superiors! I’m going to file a complaint!”

GO Ye-ho shouted back in fluent English.

Soon the fate of the trio who could only run was decided.

Before we knew it, we were surrounded by monsters on all sides. Ja Hyeong-woo held me tightly and put his back against GO Ye-ho’s.

The appearance of the monsters closing the distance while dripping saliva was too realistic.

‘But rather than being scared…’

I was caught up in feelings somewhat different from what one would normally feel.

My palms kept tingling. Like they were itching. My hands twitched, wanting to grasp something long and curved like a staff.

‘I really tried to forget. I was sincere when I felt relieved leaving everything behind.’

But actually…

I was missing it a little. The time when even I, with my hopeless body, could shoot and take down monsters, glaring fiercely at the distance until my eyes hurt, and be active as one of the combatants.

The sensation of my heart beating heroically, feeling vividly alive.
