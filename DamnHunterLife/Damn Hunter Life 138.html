Damn Hunter Life Chapter 138

The first priority was to loosen up the team members who were frozen with tension.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon quickly typed a chat. Targeting Sa Ye-ho.

==

Me>Snake Successor: Hey I’m going to joke around so you play along

Me>Snake Successor: No matter what happens you and I must not get nervous

==

Right after sending the chat, Gyeon Gang-hyeon immediately put on a carefree voice.

“There’s a tiger over there. Let’s entice one of them to become a successor.”

Sa Ye-ho picked it up as if he’d been waiting.

“Should we scout? Let’s first fight like hell then go with whoever’s left standing at the end.”

“To push me this far. You’re an enemy but I acknowledge you. Become my ally! Is that what you’re going for?”

“But we really became like old-timey people. Climbing a mountain at night! Suddenly a tiger!”

“You’d be good at pansori.”

Gyeon Gang-hyeon felt Jin Seol-woon chuckle behind him and Won Ha-ri loosen her grip on his sleeve slightly. He was relieved.

‘If I can’t be someone who reassures everyone just by existing like Woo Sae-geon.’

At least I must not show any signs of panic or helplessness in any situation.

The next moment Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s eyes completely changed. He dropped a cold instruction to his same-age friend who had just been joking around.

“Sa Ye-ho, secure visibility.”

“Roger.”

Sa Ye-ho made the flames sparked from his needle tip rise to the sky. The dim mountain path brightened as if lit by fire.

A circular area around the successors became bright. The beasts that had been surrounding them from all sides slowly retreated into the darkness.

There were countless large tigers that seemed capable of making someone collapse in fear from encountering just one. At some point the low growling had also disappeared and only tense silence flowed. The predators’ eyes were calm, as if aiming to strike vulnerable necks or sides in one blow.

“Those aren’t just ordinary tigers.”

Jangsan tigers… Jin Seol-woon let his voice trail off quietly.

Actually, he had wanted to respond to Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s joke earlier, but perhaps because of the situation, only serious and unfunny thoughts came to mind so he kept his mouth shut.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon raised his eyes sharply as if trying to extract information somehow from the beast silhouettes in the darkness.

He knows. The identity of those tigers. That’s why, except for the metal attribute which is advantageous to wood, he only brought people with solid mental fortitude.

Because of the Jangsan tigers’ characteristics.

“Anyone hearing strange voices or seeing things right now?”

“I only see tigers.”

“I’m fine.”

At Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s question, Ja Hyeong-woo answered first, then others responded in turn that nothing was wrong. Chats also arrived one after another.

After confirming with everyone that nothing was wrong, Gyeon Gang-hyeon stepped forward.

In the current state of being surrounded in a circle, they must not rashly break this precarious standoff.

Attacks in all directions, all at once. Gyeon Gang-hyeon carefully selected arrows from inventory while being cautious not to provoke the monsters.

‘If the dragon moves wrong, the entire mountain will collapse… Earth is disadvantageous to move. Fitting for a wood attribute stage.’

Pure earth attribute wasn’t good. He absolutely had to fuse two attributes to suppress earth to some degree. Gyeon Gang-hyeon selected fire attribute arrows provided by Sa Ye-ho and prepared for other directions as if laying groundwork.

==

Me: Jin Seol-woon

Me: Sorry but I need to use your dragon just as a dumb shield

Me: When I shoot arrows forward, you surround and block the other three sides with your dragon. Just pure tanking today

Me: Even if the dragon is precious, earth attribute is disadvantageous here so there’s no choice

Dragon Successor: Yes

==

Jin Seol-woon, who prepared dragon summoning, also replied to Gyeon Gang-hyeon via personal chat.

Something he hoped third parties wouldn’t hear.

==

Dragon Successor>Me: It’s okay, the dragon likes Gang-hyeon

Me>Dragon Successor: Where do familiars have self-awareness? They just follow their owner’s thoughts

==

Jin Seol-woon, startled, glanced back at Gyeon Gang-hyeon inconspicuously.

Fortunately, Gyeon Gang-hyeon seemed too focused on imminent combat to dwell further on what he’d just said.

==

Me: 3

Me: 2

Me: 1

==

Gyeon Gang-hyeon lifted his bow to eye level, expanded his chest wide and drew the string. Before the tigers in the darkness could even react to the killing intent, he shot the first arrow with almost no aiming motion.

Drawing a bright red trajectory, the arrow charged forward fiercely, and thump—! The forest center where the fireball struck shuddered.

All monsters leaped up toward the sky at once. Sa Ye-ho’s flames illuminated their wide-open mouths and large, sharp teeth.

Bang! Simultaneously, tigers on the left, right and back were knocked away by the large brown body. The dragon struck them once with its torso and managed to settle down between trees, holding its breath. The dragon familiar sacrificed its own body to create walls behind and beside the successors.

Black fabric fluttered from the shoulders of Gyeon Gang-hyeon as he retreated.

That durumagi that Gyeon Gang-hyeon wore so often he now just put it on like it was his own.

‘Can Gyeon Gang-hyeon really replace Woo Sae-geon?’

It was a curiosity that arose in everyone’s minds. The entire nation watching the stage, and even fellow successors, wanted to see that answer.

The answer was here.

Thud, someone with heavy and imposing footsteps despite their small frame passed by Gyeon Gang-hyeon and came forward.

The black and white layered skirt worn over pants swayed with vibration. The girl who suddenly stood at the front looked so fragile that even observers felt anxious.

The only metal attribute, ‘Won’ Successor Won Ha-ri.

Though half a year had passed since awakening, only now was she experiencing her first stage battle. It wasn’t just because she was young.

Won Ha-ri, with her mouth firmly shut, recalled words Gyeon Gang-hyeon had said one day.

“You’re a secret weapon.”

“That’s childish.”

Even after receiving the young sister’s rebuke, he hadn’t been embarrassed at all. He just stretched his lips and smiled shamelessly.

“It’s good. Enjoy it.”

Shing, a silver-bearing blade slid out from its sheath. Though the blade looked too thin and delicate to face the numerous tigers still in front, nonetheless.

A sharp and stinging spirit that could be felt even through the screen emanated from Won Ha-ri’s every movement. Soon it took substance riding on her cold gaze.

Sharp metal fragments formed around her and floated up. Every piece trembled finely making resonant sounds.

Soon wind made of blades rained down carrying the sound of cutting air.

The moment Won Ha-ri leaped forward pointing her sword, a scene was captured of a smile spreading across Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s lips through her scattered hair.

Though it would be later, the youngest successor’s performance would make all of South Korea enthusiastic.

Everyone wanted to know about her training. How she came to have her current skills, whose help was greatest. Everyone competed to wait for the moment she would speak.

“The person who guided me is the current leader.”

The fact that it wasn’t Woo Sae-geon who taught and left drew surprise from reporters.

Former leader Woo Sae-geon had tried to train Won Ha-ri, but progress was slow because Won Ha-ri found the stern leader, nine years older, intimidating.

Moreover, the lack of a senior with the same metal attribute was also one reason. Won Ha-ri had to figure everything out haphazardly by herself, learning through trial and error.

“It feels like… breathe more slowly… but your heartbeat should be a little faster.”

That’s what would have happened if Gyeon Gang-hyeon hadn’t appeared.

Despite her young age, Won Ha-ri coherently included the current leader’s story in her interview content.

====

Only he could see, understand, and teach my power. All my skills came from the current leader. He has mastered all attributes and theories accurately. The weakness of not being able to physically perform it himself is a minor part.

====

“Before praising me, I hope you’ll recognize Gang-hyeon oppa first.”

The current Won Ha-ri had already surpassed the original work Gyeon Gang-hyeon had seen, except for lacking experience.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon, who was serious about others’ training even if not his own, had been assisting Won Ha-ri’s training every evening.

‘Originally I was going to help for when Ja Hyeong-woo became leader… but I’m the leader.’

Gyeon Gang-hyeon, who had lamented briefly, focused on the present again.

While clearing surrounding monsters with fire attribute arrows, he was also adjusting by shooting the same arrows to Won Ha-ri’s side whenever the storm caused by Won Ha-ri’s blade grew larger or tried to become unstable.

He personally demonstrated how advantageous it was for a successor’s energy to be supported by another more experienced successor.

Countless blades rushed forward as if splitting and re-splitting seconds. Torn tree fragments flew. Swinging rapidly as if crossing swords, curved lines of blades seemed to wrap around bodies from multiple directions.

Sa Ye-ho’s flames collided with the blade, sparking red embers. The sword tip glowed red-hot, shining with a gleaming light.

Whoosh—! Then one sharp metal fragment grazed Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s cheek as it flew by. A bright red line bloomed on his pale skin.

“I was careless…!”

Gyeon Gang-hyeon, who cared more about Won Ha-ri’s image than his own pain, reflexively started to pour out words then stopped.

Because a hand that suddenly extended from behind lightly grasped then released his bleeding cheek.

There was always a healer behind him who wouldn’t allow Gyeon Gang-hyeon to get hurt.
