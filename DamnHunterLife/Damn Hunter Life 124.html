Damn Hunter Life Chapter 124

I gestured that if it wasn’t something that couldn’t be aired on the broadcast, he could just take it here.

Then Hae Mi-jun brightened up as if he’d been waiting for permission and immediately answered the phone.

“Yes, Mom! I’m eating right now. We’re filming a broadcast… All together! Yes, yes.”

His voice was cheerful. What an incredibly affectionate son. Well, it’s not like Hae Mi-jun would lack love anywhere he went. I heard he was a popular honor student at his previous high school too.

‘He only suffered because he ended up at Hunter School.’

Perhaps because I’d been staring at him the whole time, Hae Mi-jun’s eyes met mine after he hung up.

“Song Mi-jun.”

“Y-yes?”

That was the name Hae Mi-jun’s mother had been calling him over the phone. Even though that should be his real name, Hae Mi-jun seemed flustered. I guess it’s embarrassing when I call him by it.

“Come to think of it, your original surname was Song, right? It suits you.”

Hae Mi-jun’s face reddened slightly before he smiled brightly.

Probably after I retire, and much later when he retires too… if nothing goes wrong, Hae Mi-jun and I will still know each other then. At that time, I should call him Song Mi-jun.

I found myself thinking such things.

It was probably because of the sentimental atmosphere unique to evening time, the appropriately bustling noise, the feeling of fullness, and the leisurely relaxed air.

Suddenly, the surrounding scenery began to come into view again.

Hae Mi-jun, who no longer felt intimidated among the successors and was smiling.

Jin Seol-woon, who should have left long ago since he hates socializing, but was still sitting there diligently.

Yang Pyo, playing well with Ma Cheon-ah both in front of the cameras and in front of Woo Sae-geon.

And Woo Sae-geon, who had somehow become a bit more merciful.

In the original work… there were no scenes like this. Scenes of ten people playing in a pool like ordinary young people, or laughing and chattering over several plates of meat at one table.

This is the first moment when all the successors have gathered together for a meal.

‘Have the successors found some stability now? Are they conscious of each other as comrades?’

If I set aside my troublemaker self and thought about it, it wasn’t a bad sight to see.

* * *

“…Did you just tell me to take my hands off?”

Woo Sae-geon’s cold voice echoed through the conference room.

Association President Kim Jae-moon thought to himself, ‘This bastard’s starting again,’ while outwardly showing a bland smile.

[“I never said it that way. I just told Successor Woo Sae-geon a moment ago, ‘Since it would be difficult for you to make time for each case individually, we’ll handle it on our end.'”]

“It sounds the same to me.”

No matter how trivial the paperwork, all approvals had to go through Woo Sae-geon himself. When Woo Sae-geon showed fierce rejection and hostility, the Association President sighed deeply. Then he tried to persuade him with different words.

[“Successor Woo Sae-geon, retirement is right around the corner. Now is the time to distribute work and hand over duties to the next leader.”]

“Whether there are 30 days left or 3 days left, my work is my work. Just because I’m dying tomorrow, does that mean I do nothing today?”

He was the most adult in the school, and had to be the adult, at the age of twenty-four when he couldn’t do so in real society.

Woo Sae-geon had always been on edge. With his characteristically arrogant face, with the arrogance that he needed to know everything that was going on.

Rather than give the Association any opening to interfere in every little thing, Woo Sae-geon chose to control everything at his own level.

He was a tyrant. He trampled on students’ autonomy and will countless times, forcing them to do what he thought was right.

And while doing so—

He took on all the blame for the minor accidents and mistakes that happened at school, and the disgrace of combat and strategic failures.

—Woo Sae-geon doesn’t manage Gyeon Gang-hyeon?

When Gyeon Gang-hyeon was failing to awaken and wasting time, Woo Sae-geon was talked about as much as Gyeon Gang-hyeon.

—Woo Sae-geon must have ordered it.

When Gyeon Gang-hyeon was hiding his familiar, the public assumed there must have been orders from Woo Sae-geon.

—Woo Sae-geon will handle the scolding himself. What’s the point of us cursing?

Everything was under Woo Sae-geon’s jurisdiction, so it was natural. The mistakes the successors made in battle, and the criticisms that quickly died down soon after.

“Please pay attention.”

When troublemaker Gyeon Gang-hyeon caused problems here and there, it was always Woo Sae-geon who got the harsh words from the Association.

The flow that Woo Sae-geon had intended when he moved to become leader was perfectly realized. Success and failure alike. Whatever the outcome, all responsibility was concentrated on one person.

The other students just had to be under him as chess pieces, as subordinate employees who bore no responsibility.

Woo Sae-geon’s psychology that he had never told anyone.

But these things couldn’t be because of affection or concern for others younger than himself. Woo Sae-geon’s behavioral motivation was firm and clear.

Because it was convenient.

Because it was right.

The obsession of someone who trusts only himself.

The successor called ‘Woo Sae-geon’ was an existence that Yoon Sae-geon, who had lived in shadowy alleys and had never been a leader before, created to test himself.

Woo Sae-geon had almost passed Yoon Sae-geon’s test. But no matter how solidly he had built the image of a scholar, if a life bearing everything in the school continued for a whole year, that image would inevitably crack little by little.

While preparing for the next stage battle and checking all the successors’ training, Woo Sae-geon, who hadn’t properly rested for even 30 minutes a day, realized that the twitching around his eyes hadn’t disappeared for three days.

But anyway, it was only a month now, wasn’t it.

The conclusion during that time must not fall apart.

* * *

Since the last meat party, except for a few outsiders, the concept of a group had somewhat formed among the successors.

Like now, when five people—Hae Mi-jun, Yang Pyo, Won Ha-ri, Ma Cheon-ah, and Sa Ye-ho—sat at one table in the cafeteria eating together.

I tried to leave, but Sa Ye-ho grabbed me by the scruff of the neck.

“Don’t you know this is a meal appointment that only works if you’re here? All the kids gathered because of you.”

“What bullshit?”

So he’s saying he’ll use me as entertainment, as a topic of conversation.

“Won Ha-ri, stop looking so pleased while watching me put a spoon in my mouth, like you’re watching a critically ill patient who miraculously recovered after surgery and is eating.”

In the end, I had no choice but to sit in the middle and watch as all the kids except me went back for seconds on their meal trays.

“Gang-hyeon, are you going out on another stage?”

Sa Ye-ho asked while licking his ice cream dessert.

Before I could answer, three pairs of eyes except Ma Cheon-ah’s fixed on me. Whether the main emotion in them was surprise or mockery, there was at least worry mixed in.

“I said I wanted to go out. I’m planning to stick close to Woo Sae-geon before he retires. Woo Sae-geon needs to be there to carry a troll like me.”

One month until Woo Sae-geon’s retirement. There were about two more stage schedules set until then. Woo Sae-geon would naturally run both at full capacity.

Then I need to earnestly squeeze in beside Woo Sae-geon and stick to him. After Woo Sae-geon leaves, it might be hard to produce results.

‘They could just leave one spot empty, but still, Woo Sae-geon included me…’

When talk of Woo Sae-geon’s retirement came up, Won Ha-ri suddenly became tense and picked at her food hesitantly.

If Woo Sae-geon leaves, the one who would probably fill that vacant spot would be Won Ha-ri, the only metal attribute among the current Twelve Zodiac.

Won Ha-ri had officially never been selected for a stage yet. Under the pretext that more careful preparation was needed because she was so young, she was mainly active in gate support like Hae Mi-jun.

I gave Won Ha-ri a look.

“Think about the training you’ve done with me so far. When your first battle is revealed, people will say Woo Sae-geon is washed up.”

“People will sneer.”

Even though she said that, I could clearly see Won Ha-ri’s facial muscles relax a little.

And while listening to my conversation with Won Ha-ri, I also saw Ma Cheon-ah clattering her utensils loudly, as if she didn’t want to acknowledge my abilities.

Inside the stage was a snowy field covered in white snow.

I tucked my bow under my arm and hunched my body. My body shook like I had tremors from the unexpected severe cold.

“Does this gangcheol thing or whatever originally live in cold places?”

“No, I’ve never heard anything like that…”

Here, gangcheol isn’t a piece of metal, but a legendary animal said to breathe fire and harm crops. It’s a monster that’s incompatible with plants, and it’s in a snowy field. If Myo Cho-hui had been here, who knows what would have happened.

Yang Pyo looked at me with pity as I was about to freeze to death.

Even while chattering my teeth and shivering, I kept my mouth moving.

“If you’re thinking of hugging me, get lost.”

“Who would think such a thing?!”

Before Yang Pyo could finish speaking, something warm from behind me suddenly covered and wrapped around my body.

It was a black durumagi. One warmed by Ja Hyeong-woo’s body temperature.

“Hah…”

Without realizing it, my body relaxed and a languid moan escaped.

But immediately after, I couldn’t help but stiffen, because Ja Hyeong-woo’s arms had come from behind and were tying the durumagi strings over my chest.

Imagining what Ja Hyeong-woo and I must have looked like just now, my fingers and toes twitched from embarrassment.

Ja Hyeong-woo calmly secured the durumagi and stepped back. Then he immediately pointed at my sobok and made a comment.

“Even if it’s underwear, I know it has some cold-weather protection.”

“I’ve told you how many times this isn’t underwear but clothing? And do you think general cold-weather protection would even register with me?”

“Be quiet. This is a stage.”

Woo Sae-geon’s low voice brought the topic back to the starting point.
