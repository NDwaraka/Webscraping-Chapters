Damn Hunter Life Chapter 186

My dog familiar, which had been my last remaining strength, gradually slowed down too. The horde of ghosts drew closer and closer.

Even at this moment, ghosts continued endlessly rising up. In the clearing that seemed almost infinite in size, that many ghosts had appeared.

By now, such questions had become all too familiar.

‘What are those ghosts? Why are they so desperate to devour me?’

But in this world I had possessed into in the first place, there wasn’t much I could know.

In a situation where I understood nothing, I strangely experienced my mind becoming clear.

Then I saw a small light.

What had been too small a dot to recognize at first gradually grew like a star as I approached it.

“Something occurred to me. But I don’t know what will change even if I know this.”

The reason spirit monsters only existed inside stages.

“Stages are places we have to block, right? If we can’t block them, it means spirit monsters will spread out into the world.”

Then all these ghosts would spread out too.

I didn’t check if Ja Hyeong-woo understood what I said. Instead, I opened the status window.

For the first time in a long while, I read the original skill description as it was.

==

<Boundary Between Life and Death (No Attribute)>

Origin: The body that became incomplete as the energies of the Twelve Zodiac gathered came to straddle the boundary between the afterlife and this world.

Description: Temporarily cuts off vital signs and goes into hiding.

==

The one and only ‘no attribute’ skill I possessed.

I instantly modified this skill so it could be used on other beings besides myself.

A skill that cuts off vital signs. Though it’s a skill that brings living beings close to death, somehow the skill’s name meant both ‘life and death’ simultaneously.

Then conversely, wouldn’t something work if I used it on dead beings too?

I moved below the dog familiar. I began using the skill one by one on the ghosts reaching out to me.

The female ghost right in front of me disappeared. I eliminated them one by one with each skill use.

And hundreds and thousands of ghosts remained.

“Gang-hyeon.”

Ja Hyeong-woo called my name with difficulty, as if being strangled.

Throughout this stage, I had been cold to Ja Hyeong-woo until the very end.

“Just heal from behind. I might really become a ghost.”

It was something I said without much meaning, but at that moment my heart sank.

‘……Could Beom I-ryeong’s intention in approaching me have been……’

I gritted my teeth and forcibly stopped thinking.

My hand using the colorless skill, and the ghosts falling away one by one.

Ja Hyeong-woo, who had been looking back and forth between both, embraced me from behind and emitted blue light. He increased the healing output.

“Gang-hyeon, stop…… Retreat leaving only me behind. Recover and come back……”

His pleading voice cracked hideously.

The mouse familiar had exhausted its strength long ago. Ja Hyeong-woo’s body was also driven to its limits. He was in a state where even the energy to preserve his own body was precious. It made sense, since he had remained until the very end even after all other team members had returned.

But more than all of that, my body, which wouldn’t stop using the Boundary Between Life and Death skill, was growing faint at an even faster rate.

I couldn’t hear my own voice. I could only feel Ja Hyeong-woo’s agonizing breathing and his trembling touch slowly groping over me.

“How could I accept your words about trading your life to save me?”

I could see he was on the verge of doing something I didn’t want.

I threatened him preemptively. When I harshly raised my voice, a cough mixed with blood burst out.

“If you heal by cutting your lifespan, you’ll just die immediately. I’ll die as quickly as your lifespan decreases. Do you understand?”

I’m saying, do you think I’d just stand by and watch you give up chunks of lifespan to break through skill limits like in the original work?

Ja Hyeong-woo’s eyes opened wide. Blood vessels tightened tensely within his eyes.

Ja Hyeong-woo couldn’t even ask me how I knew about that ability. Ja Hyeong-woo’s body, which had never trembled, shook pathetically. He showed without filter that he was afraid of losing me.

Even knowing this, I couldn’t bring myself to reassure him.

I slowly peeled off the ghosts clinging to the dog familiar’s rear. Blood seemed to be flowing from the corners of my mouth. Ja Hyeong-woo’s hand repeatedly felt around my lips.

Like sinking into water, Ja Hyeong-woo embraced me.

If healing was cut off even slightly, my life would be extinguished.

I had committed a sin against Korea’s number one healer that should never be committed again. I had placed too great a burden on him.

My breath grew faint. All my senses gradually distanced themselves from me.

Time slowed down, as if it would stop completely. Only the precarious silence just before fading away filled the air.

The reason my exhausted body from the long battle still wouldn’t let go of life was due to the duty to send back even one more ghost.

‘To where they should originally be.’

I suddenly moved my mouth and exhaled. Fortunately, my voice flowed out.

“Hey.”

“Gang-hyeon. Don’t speak.”

I don’t know what state I was in, but Ja Hyeong-woo made me close my mouth while holding back tears slightly.

But to avoid losing consciousness, I had to speak.

“These ghosts. Do you know what they really are?”

It felt like a long-standing riddle was finally being solved. While using the Boundary Between Life and Death skill, I had gained insight into all of this.

That everything surrounding me was actually connected as one. That it had guided me right to this point.

“They’re real ghosts. They’re truly souls formed from the deaths of humans and monsters.”

Stages were places close to being inexplicable mysteries. They were strange phenomena that occurred only in Korea, which even the system couldn’t predict.

Gates within gates. Because Earth couldn’t handle this, the rules of the world gradually crumbled without our knowledge.

That’s why spirit monsters appeared.

Hunter School was a safety device prepared by the system to prevent the chaos that gates within gates would cause.

“We said that if we can’t close the stages, ghosts would be released into the world. So that means…… the afterlife and this world would be connected.”

Ja Hyeong-woo had eyes that seemed to vaguely understand, but still looked confused. He looked at me with crinkled eyes.

That makes sense. Including Ja Hyeong-woo, it was normal for no one to realize this simple truth.

But only I……

Had encountered death up close, and this body I possessed had accumulated long suffering both before and after possession.

Perhaps this was why I was chosen as a no-attribute successor and ‘Hunter Who Achieves Harmony.’

“……!”

The dog familiar seemed to have collapsed and been reverse-summoned. My body rolled on the ground several times hazily.

A chin harboring hot body temperature embraced me and exhaled rough breaths.

Ja Hyeong-woo, thrown down on the ground, tried to shield me and draw his sword.

I turned that Ja Hyeong-woo around.

“It’s fine. I won.”

I opened my eyes.

Ja Hyeong-woo was looking around rather than at me.

All those ghosts had disappeared like a lie. It was the original empty clearing. The scene where Juk-yeop-gun corpses lay scattered.

“Hah……”

I let out a long sigh and sprawled on the ground. I spread my limbs, not caring about getting covered in dust.

Ja Hyeong-woo, who blinked several times, made a strange expression that might have been laughter or tears. Then he toppled over beside me.

Ja Hyeong-woo couldn’t get up for a while. I turned over to check on Ja Hyeong-woo.

“You’re all injured.”

I told him not to cut his lifespan, so instead he seemed to have consumed his own blood and fluids. His dusky skin was pale and dry like sand.

I took out an ion drink from my inventory and fed it to Ja Hyeong-woo. Ja Hyeong-woo frantically swallowed the liquid.

Ja Hyeong-woo, who immediately sat up, wrapped his arms around me first.

“Gang-hyeon……”

“Don’t call me.”

I was embarrassed, so I snapped at him harshly for now. Ja Hyeong-woo rubbed his lips against my forehead.

The status window blinked above my head.

==

/Successor Gang-hyeon. Congratulations on your victory.

==

“Wait.”

I quickly glared into the air. I pushed Ja Hyeong-woo away.

==

/And you also won the bet with me. It was a bet you had to win. I’m sincerely happy too.

==

The detestable bastard who only now appeared was spouting things I couldn’t understand.

I read the status window comments that only I could see aloud to Ja Hyeong-woo too. Since Ja Hyeong-woo now knew everything.

And hoping to sound threatening, I raised my eyes sharply.

“Explain in detail.”

==

/I cannot speak directly. In principle, I do not intervene.

==

Irritation surged up. My blood pressure rose and I staggered for a moment, so Ja Hyeong-woo quickly embraced me.

“What the hell are you? If you’re going to say nonsense—!”

==

/I’m a neutral party who hopes that different worlds won’t get mixed up.

==

“Different worlds…… the afterlife and this world?”

==

/All the things you’re inferring are mostly correct. It took neither too long nor too short to reach this point.

==

Right at this moment, the fact that the entity I was conversing with was only visible as a few lines of text was extremely infuriating. I was openly displaying my bristling emotions, but I couldn’t know the other party’s emotions.

No, did it even have emotions?

==

/Although you nearly abandoned your given destiny of your own will, including the trouble I deliberately took, it was ultimately right.

==
