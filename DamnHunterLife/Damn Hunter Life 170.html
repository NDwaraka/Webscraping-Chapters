“When I first became a successor, I was incredibly excited. I thought I was born with athletic abilities for this day.”

This is getting longer than expected.

“I felt like I was the protagonist of the world, like I’d become a hero from a comic. My colleagues also seemed more like comic or movie characters rather than actual people. It didn’t feel very real.”

You call that kind of thing a secret? At this late stage?

“Sorry, but everyone already knows you think that way.”

“Don’t interrupt me.”

Ma Cheon-ah’s true nature came out for a moment. She shot back mercilessly then returned to reminiscence mode again.

“Somehow everyone looked so clean and neat…… like characters. I wasn’t in my right mind either, thinking of living people as characters.”

What is she trying to say with all this?

Ma Cheon-ah, who had been rambling on, suddenly stood up from the table.

What? Why is she kneeling? Is she crazy?

“I’ll confess. Leader. I’m a diligent member of the official Twelve Zodiac community.”

Why the sudden formal speech? And she called me Leader? Not Dog?

I just stared blankly at her, still unable to understand the context.

“So…… I’m a member who plays around pairing the Leader with Mouse oppa…… Hyeong-woo oppa, Ye-ho oppa, and Seol-woon oppa.”

Ma Cheon-ah started calling the successors by their real names instead of animal names…… but wait, what?

“What the hell!?”

What I had witnessed in the community just before becoming leader came flooding back. I now know what kind of delusions that community is full of.

Why on earth do they pair me with men, and why not just one person but several people, and why is Ma Cheon-ah doing this?

While I was gaping in disbelief, Ma Cheon-ah shamelessly straightened her knees, stood up, and grinned wickedly.

“If this gets out about me, my image will hit rock bottom. So Leader, keep it secret too. I’ll keep yours secret too. Now do you trust me? Isn’t this enough to call it even?”

I had only one thing to say.

“Quit!”

“No way!”

Ma Cheon-ah squirmed as if hugging something invisible. I stomped my foot in front of her.

“How can you do such a thing? Are you really crazy!?”

“Then don’t be so pretty!”

“Are you crazy!?”

It feels like Ma Cheon-ah and I became closer…… like hell, we became more distant. In the midst of this, Ma Cheon-ah went even further and said flowery nonsense.

“But then are you really dating Hyeong-woo oppa? Was this ship actually real?”

It’s treatment! I clearly said treatment, but that bastard is hearing only what she wants to hear!

I fired off harsh criticism asking if she was crazy, how she could do such a thing, and whether she treats me like a human being. When my cursing got long, Ma Cheon-ah also got worked up.

“We said we’d keep each other’s secrets! This is breach of contract!”

“Keeping secrets is one thing! As a leader, can I just let a member engage in unwholesome activities? There’s successor dignity to maintain. Quit immediately!”

In the end, Ma Cheon-ah looked like death and deleted her community account in front of me. She defended like a fortress, saying she’d rather die than show me things like search history or viewing lists.

What’s gotten into her head? To think I raised a tiger cub, no, a horse cub!

“Don’t talk to me from now on!”

“I never talked to you anyway?”

There wasn’t even a drop of affection between us who shared secrets. Only harsh words were exchanged. At least I had sent everyone 50 meters away from the office, thank goodness.

After grumbling for a long time and banishing Ma Cheon-ah from my space, my head finally cooled down.

‘Sigh, right. Ma Cheon-ah isn’t the problem. The problem is that I’ve been getting carried around everywhere because I lack energy. They say you can’t even drink cold water in front of children.’

This kind of thing is just a simple happening.

Actually, I’m anxious to death.

Seeing Maegu made it even more certain. I could see signs that something strange was about to happen around me. But I couldn’t get a sense of what it was. I need to know what it is to at least try to deal with it.

I slowly retraced the situation.

‘The original work’s final chapter. ……But there was no Gang-hyeon in the original.’

Or perhaps it seemed…… like there wasn’t one.

Lost in deep thought, I briefly stopped by home and found Beom I-ryeong, who was staying at my house.

“I was taking a break during training. I like resting at hyung-nim’s house.”

Seeing the boy who ran out to greet me, my heart somehow sank with a thud.

I think I know what I should be suspicious of.

During the 22nd stage boss strategy.

The ice ability that Hae Mi-jun newly created not only effectively captured the cursed dolls but also helped in eliminating the boss.

His attack skill could have manifested as a partial anesthesia skill.

However, Hae Mi-jun’s current disposition rejected that change. What suited him was blocking and protecting something, not directly causing harm.

The technique that originated from his desire to earn a place beside Gang-hyeon.

Now became a technique that allowed Gang-hyeon and Ja Hyeong-woo to be together.

Jin Seol-woon handed over the main healing position to Ja Hyeong-woo and checked on Hae Mi-jun, who was contributing to combat, throughout the battle.

To him, the feelings that his younger colleague harbored for Gang-hyeon were immediately visible.

Rather, to Gang-hyeon himself, it probably wasn’t visible, hidden by their initial relationship of training together.

‘That person has no awareness in that direction.’

After a long time, a soft smile spread across his lips.

For someone who chatters quite well, he never talked about old times at all.

It was because he had spent a long time in the hospital.

After that fact became known, Jin Seol-woon had casually asked Ja Hyeong-woo about it. He was curious what the man who was Gang-hyeon’s childhood friend would say.

He said that while Gang-hyeon was often sickly and couldn’t do physical activities well, there were no symptoms in elementary school. If there had been, there’s no way he wouldn’t have known.

And at that moment. A cold voice giving orders was heard. Jin Seol-woon’s reverie was broken.

“Jin Seol-woon, give me your dragon and jump down! Mountain skill!”

Jin Seol-woon gladly entrusted himself to him. Without even a moment’s hesitation, he suddenly jumped down.

The day after the strategy was completed. Jin Seol-woon received his contact.

[Can you meet me today?]

Gang-hyeon came to find him first. He made time despite being busy.

Although he was someone who had shown burdensome feelings toward him, Gang-hyeon wanted to show courtesy to Jin Seol-woon.

As if Jin Seol-woon was also precious to him to some degree.

Jin Seol-woon exhaled deeply, feeling the deepest tension of his life.

“I wanted to return this.”

Gang-hyeon took out a broken S-grade sword.

Jin Seol-woon’s eyes took in the sword that closely resembled his own, but was broken in the middle and split into two pieces. Also the thin, white fingers that set the sword down.

The relationship between the two began with this sword and ends with this sword.

The events of that time passed by like a revolving lantern.

At first, he had only given it as a gesture of gratitude.

When Gang-hyeon first swung this sword and spat blood. He was so shocked when he heard about it later. He even thought he shouldn’t have given it to him.

The fact that this sword caused Gang-hyeon and himself to become the subject of people’s gossip was initially bewildering. But gradually it became pleasant.

If only it were possible, he had hoped this sword would never leave Gang-hyeon’s side until the day he died.

The sword returned too quickly.

“Sorry for breaking it.”

“I saw everything about how it got broken.”

This sword broke its own body instead of its owner. If the sword hadn’t broken, that impact might have collided directly with Gang-hyeon and killed him instantly.

“I’ll make you a new one.”

Jin Seol-woon didn’t move his eyes and only moved his lips slightly. He spoke a bit quickly. Perhaps some urgency had mixed in.

And when Gang-hyeon met his gaze looking at him gently, all the impurities surrounding him crumbled.

“You know how I’ll answer.”

In those eyes, Jin Seol-woon’s heart settled more than he himself was surprised by.

“Yes. I know.”

He’s grateful to the one who came to listen to his words first.

“But you can’t stop my mouth right now.”

Gang-hyeon, who had winced around his eyes a little, soon straightened his back and sat upright. He seemed to be conscious of the atmosphere, which looked quite amusing.

“It’s because Gang-hyeon-ssi is attractive.”

So he blurted that out.

Gang-hyeon’s face became as if he’d been slapped. Jin Seol-woon leaned his upper body forward and continued speaking.

“It seems like it wouldn’t hurt even if I put you in my eyes, and it makes me smile. As an idol, I liked Gang-hyeon-ssi so much that I dreamed of dating another man.”

The other person is trying his best to manage his expression. But his eyes kept twitching. Jin Seol-woon wanted to laugh.

But instead of laughing, he drew out the power he had used to captivate others in front of cameras.

“Think carefully about what I said. If you’ve thought about it, and if you think you know yourself a bit……”

He wanted to leave today as an unforgettable scene for Gang-hyeon. Now his mission was only to let him know that.

“Take a look at the person beside Gang-hyeon-ssi then.”

The place he returned to was shrouded in darkness.

Shadows created by furniture stood up jaggedly. Jin Seol-woon sat on a small dark chair among them and melted into part of the darkness.

Flutter, he lifted the sheet music that had been sleeping in the drawer above his head to read it.

In the end, he couldn’t let him hear this song.

Quietly looking at the notes and lyrics written on the sheet music, he lay down lengthwise on the chair and placed the sheet music on his chest. The sheet music didn’t slide off and stayed still on his body.

It’s something he can’t take outside Hunter School anyway.

Then when he retires in a few years, he can just forget this too.

But.

‘The song’s melody and all the lyrics are already filling my whole body.’

Even if he leaves it behind, he won’t be able to forget it.

Even when he goes out and returns to daily life, he’ll remember it note for note without a single mistake.

He might end up writing it down again somewhere because he can’t forget it.

‘I can’t say I don’t hope he’ll come to me if he breaks up with Ja Hyeong-woo-ssi.’

Since he’s already become pathetic, he conjured up another wish with his closed eyes.

That the song he composed would be the best among the self-composed songs he’d create in the future.
