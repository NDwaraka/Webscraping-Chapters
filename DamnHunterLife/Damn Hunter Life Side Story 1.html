Damn Hunter Life Side Story Chapter 1

With the official end of the stages, the successors’ burden was reduced. Now they could live a bit more comfortably as leaders of hunters, lounging around more than before.

Of course, regular gates still existed. The threat of high-level gates similar to stages also remained.

Therefore, regardless of other things, the association’s opinion was to continue the successors’ training as before without reduction.

“But you know what? That has nothing to do with me now. What are you doing grabbing a civilian?”

[“Ahhhhh don’t hang up!”]

I abruptly hung up on Sa Ye-ho’s call. Then I quickly turned off my phone before Sa Ye-ho could call back.

As the call ended, silence quickly filled the empty officetel where I was alone.

I stared blankly into space.

No status window appeared. There wasn’t a single notification.

…I don’t know what I’m waiting for. I suddenly came to my senses and moved my body. The silence felt uncomfortable, so I turned on the TV too.

Listening to the sound filling the house, I newly realized that now I could lie down comfortably since there were no clothing ties on my chest.

I suddenly reached for the table. I picked up and looked at the ID card I had received through Park Yoon-seok.

==

Cheon Gang-hyeon (千强炫)

991206—×××××××

==

Now ‘Gyeon Gang-hyeon’ is just the name of a retired hunter like Woo Sae-geon. Even if all people, including me, haven’t adapted yet.

I put down the ID and touched my shoulders that had felt empty since earlier.

I had gone to the salon earlier and cut my hair, which used to come down to my shoulders, up to my nape. Manager Park Yoon-seok accompanied me.

Honestly, I know long hair suits me well, but living alone, it was bothersome to take care of my hair.

But somehow the news leaked, and I got a call from Hyeong-woo asking to see my hair.

“I told you not to contact me during training hours.”

After giving him a fierce warning, I took a selfie with my phone and sent it to him.

The response came 10 minutes later. The read receipt appeared as soon as I sent it… don’t tell me he stared at it for 10 minutes.

[Cute.]

[You always had it that length before. Since it’s short, your handsome features stand out more.]

I was about to curse at the “cute” text, but after seeing the follow-up message, I deleted the curse I was typing.

A few days after I was kicked out alone, Hyeong-woo, who had been reading the mood, casually asked me.

“I’m asking in advance because I might make a mistake. Would you prefer I not talk about old stories? If you say you don’t want to hear them, I’ll be careful.”

Actually, it was a topic I didn’t want to think deeply about. I wanted to just live with an empty head for even a few days. Even if that was really stupid.

However, just as I had the right to enjoy what Cheon Gang-hyeon possessed, I thought Hyeong-woo also had the right to miss Cheon Gang-hyeon.

“Say it if you want to. I should listen too.”

Then Hyeong-woo didn’t decline.

[“During elementary school, you received confessions once every semester. That’s just what I saw or heard, so there were probably actually more, right?”]

But somehow, it seems like even a child he birthed himself wouldn’t be praised less than that…

“Wasn’t that just being friendly, and you distorted it because you were jealous?”

[“Yeah, I was jealous.”]

“Hey, that was 10 years ago…”

[“There were definitely boys who had feelings for you too. They just hid it. You were more popular with boys than girls.”]

“Damn, you call that a compliment? If you’re going to say shit like that, don’t even open your mouth!”

It was nonsense worse than not hearing anything.

Just curse at Cheon Gang-hyeon instead, no, curse at me.

Since Hyeong-woo was making cringeworthy sounds as he pleased, I grumbled as I wanted to. But actually, I was wrapped in a strange feeling.

The day I vomited all the resentment I didn’t even know I had, the day we both shed buckets of tears.

I thought I’d become awkward with Hyeong-woo and couldn’t even make eye contact. Or that I’d become speechless with mixed feelings as soon as I saw his face.

[‘Photo’]

[What’s all this junk]

[I moved everything you used to my house.]

[If you do anything weird with it, I’ll kill you]

What melancholy, it was just like before.

No, since I was fierce before, is it different from the past? Anyway, it’s more ordinary and peaceful than I thought, so even I’m flustered.

Since people don’t really change dramatically, it’s also true that I still can’t speak gently to Hyeong-woo. But this is what, the old Cheon Gang-hyeon was like this too.

‘Everything’s comfortable and good, but… what do I do with my life now?’

I had never deeply thought about what to do after retirement. I just thought anything would be fine.

Whatever it was, for Cheon Gang-hyeon, it would be things he couldn’t properly try while staying in the hospital.

Now I have no obligations, and the world will be protected by those below me.

Who would tell me to study, who would tell me to earn money…

There was exactly one thing I did with a sense of duty.

I took a taxi by myself without asking Manager Park Yoon-seok. I thought that gentleman would secretly feel sorry if I told him the destination. I didn’t like that.

It didn’t take very long by car. It was within Seoul.

I entered a quiet and clean columbarium. I wore a hat pulled low, worried someone might recognize me.

The burial niches of unfamiliar man and woman greeted me.

The man in the photo next to the flower basket had the same surname as me and similar eyes, while the woman had snow-white skin like me and identical features.

“…Mom, Dad.”

I deliberately pronounced it aloud as if I were someone learning to speak for the first time. The pronunciation came out very stiff.

No words came out, so I just endlessly looked at the photo of young Cheon Gang-hyeon smiling.

“I’ll come again.”

In the end, I couldn’t say any proper words and turned my back. I felt like my legs would give out and I’d collapse if I stayed any longer.

I still feel like this is all a show.

Cheon Gang-hyeon felt like a stranger to me, too naturally. There were no such optimistic things as memories returning or suddenly realizing that was me.

It just felt like being shown someone else’s memories and hearing claims that it was me.

Even when I heard about my parents at Hunter School, I never once thought of them as my parents. So there’s no way this columbarium would feel related to me.

I’ve never felt strange feelings either…

Suddenly my chest tingled. My throat became stuffy as if my lungs had narrowed. I stood with my hand against the wall and quietly caught my breath.

‘I’ve felt this sensation with Hyeong-woo before too.’

But that’s all.

I should remember. I absolutely shouldn’t forget memories of the people who gave birth to me, regardless of anything else.

What am I exactly?

…What else, an empty person.

I came home and forced myself to eat dinner. Although a housekeeper comes, I ran the vacuum and washing machine myself. I had to prove I was okay.

My phone rang once and I knew a text came, but I didn’t check it.

It was better being alone without Hyeong-woo. I couldn’t show him this state. I needed time for things to sort themselves out.

I ended up vomiting all the food I had eaten before going to bed. I must have had indigestion.

* * *

“Sob…”

I heard a strange sound by my ear.

I was so startled that I bolted upright. Then I immediately collapsed back down.

Before I could understand the circumstances of the situation in my confusion, tears started falling.

I seemed to have dreamed something, but I had no memory of it.

I felt like this had happened before.

Deep fear washed over me. Without even fully waking up, I curled up like a shot animal. Covering my mouth with my hand, I sobbed, not knowing what was so sad.

‘I don’t want to cry…’

I tried to hold it back, but I only got exhausted faster.

Suddenly the light from my phone screen attacked my eyes. When I frowned, more tears poured out.

Only after hearing the dial tone from my phone did I gasp and come to my senses. I had done it without knowing.

But I couldn’t immediately press call end because I wasn’t in my right mind.

[“Gang-hyeon, why?”]

Unfortunately, the call connected before I could hang up.

My heart sank. I pressed the pillow hard against my mouth and held back my sobs.

The silence flowed for only a moment.

[“Gang-hyeon.”]

…He seemed to have guessed just from my breathing.

I bit my lips even tighter. I clamped my mouth shut so air wouldn’t get in and my crying wouldn’t leak out. Then something welled up in my chest and I felt painful pressure that made my heart ache.

[“It’s okay. It’s okay…”]

At the whispering voice, I finally made sobbing sounds.

I buried my face in the pillow, and Hyeong-woo quietly listened to my sounds.

The guy who usually asks about everything wanting to know, at times like this, recognizes it like a ghost and says nothing. Hyeong-woo waited patiently until the sounds I was making completely subsided.

[“…Drink water. Is there water nearby?”]

I tried to ignore it and sprawl on the bed, but somehow Hyeong-woo knew and kept talking.

I didn’t want to get up, but I had no choice but to get up eventually.

After that, Hyeong-woo asked if it was okay to switch to a video call, and I hesitated but gave in to weakness and accepted.

He turned on the stand light on his side so I could see him. I didn’t turn mine on. I just hid in the darkness and looked at Hyeong-woo’s calm face lying down.

Strangely, just looking at his closed lips and gentle expression, I could fall asleep.
