Damn Hunter Life Chapter 159

Tsk, Myo Cho-hui clicked her tongue lightly. Her burned fingers were blistered, so she couldn’t properly grip her spear with strength.

‘Still, thanks to Yang Pyo absorbing most of the heat…’

The sheep familiar’s majestic appearance grew faint. Yang Pyo, kneeling on the ground and gasping for breath, was already in tatters. His whole body was charred and his senses seemed dulled.

Thanks to that, I, Ja Hyeong-woo, and Jin Seol-woon didn’t suffer serious injuries from the recent fire.

Ja Hyeong-woo gritted his teeth and drew up as much cool water energy as possible. The exhausted-looking mouse familiar quickly circled around again.

Ja Hyeong-woo somehow endured without drying up in the hot flames, but the other healer seemed unable to do the same.

“Hah, huk.”

Hae Mi-jun exhaled roughly. His pig familiar, whose body had almost completely evaporated, was reverse-summoned. The large water familiar disappeared.

“Huk…!”

The hot wind that the pig had been blocking came rushing in strongly.

The successors, helplessly pushed back by the hot heat, were crowded together tightly. Myo Cho-hui blocked in front of me and drew out plants to the maximum. She was trying to block the harmful smoke.

I barely caught Hae Mi-jun as he collapsed. As a result, we both slumped down together.

Hae Mi-jun, his face all injured, looked up at me from my lap. Eyes that seemed about to cry turned toward me.

“I’m sorry. I was lacking.”

I shook my head. I swept Hae Mi-jun’s bangs aside.

“You’ve always been too good for me. Your abilities and your heart.”

I don’t know if I should feel desperate about this moment or not. I felt no emotion at all.

I just had the thought that this eighteen-year-old boy in front of me no longer looked like a child. It was quite a strange feeling.

I gripped Hae Mi-jun’s hand tightly and forcibly stole his energy. I breathed in his unique energy deeply until my body was completely full.

“Gang-hyeon hyung…?”

Hae Mi-jun called out to me in surprise. I pretended not to hear, ignoring even Hae Mi-jun’s injured arm trying to stop me, and gathered the water energy I’d scraped from him inside my body.

It was power I should conserve, but if I suffered injuries here that prevented me from getting back up, what came next would be meaningless. So I didn’t hesitate.

The refreshing power of water poured over me endlessly like a waterfall. The force swept through me as if I’d forget what color I originally was.

I tried not to lose consciousness. While doing so, I breathed deeply in and out to draw up the fundamental, warm attribute that lay beneath all attributes.

Even though it was negative energy harmful to me, perhaps because it was uniquely pure, I felt no pain at all in summoning this cardinal deity.

The people around me turned toward me one by one. Surprised expressions arose here and there as they gradually realized what was happening.

I opened my eyes wide and glared at the sky.

“Let’s create an ocean first.”

I used the life force Ja Hyeong-woo had given me without reserve to shape my energy. The abundant energy caused a massive storm inside. The moment the energy took form, I brought it outside.

Dark blue light scattered in all directions.

Though not as splendid and ever-changing as Suzaku, the most mysterious and profound legendary animal from myth rose up into the sky, extinguishing the fire energy like a candle flame.

==

<Northern Guardian Water Deity Xuanwu>

Origin: Water guarding the north fuses with earth that builds up from the center, manifesting a god from mythology.

==

The turtle head and snake head each breathed out beautiful water energy. The acrid smoke had somehow turned into clear water mist.

“Hah…”

Raindrops began falling gently on our faces, opening our airways.

Yang Pyo’s entire body was clean as he stood up in bewilderment. The energy Xuanwu scattered changed the environment of the entire area and healed all our bodies.

Setting aside Ja Hyeong-woo looking at me as if I were a god… everyone gazed at me with wonder. Meanwhile, Ja Hyeong-woo worried about my body through chat.

I got up hastily while helping Hae Mi-jun to his feet. Though I staggered slightly, I didn’t forget to stay alert to our surroundings.

I’d consumed one cardinal deity. The cooldown for the cardinal deity I’d already used was still far off, and the metal attribute cardinal deity was incompatible with Won Ha-ri’s yin-yang, so it was excluded from the start.

In the end, I now had only one cardinal deity left that I could summon.

‘I hope Xuanwu’s duration lasts as long as possible, so it can fight alongside us even after we find the boss.’

This boss monster is a ghost that detection skills don’t work on.

However, at this point I held a strange certainty.

Since there was no other method anyway, considering that in the end I could only trust my instincts.

‘The ghost will come looking for me, or become interested in me.’

The boss would appear on its own.

A being far more powerful and vicious than any fire demons we’d faced so far.

The black head emerging from the thick, hard shell made a sharp hissing sound and raised its head. The long tail swam nimbly in the sky.

I watched to make sure Xuanwu didn’t disappear while frantically looking around to gauge which direction the boss was coming from.

“Is the boss coming? I can’t see it in any direction!”

Jin Seol-woon shouted.

Everyone was only looking at me, but even I couldn’t find it. Sweat flowed again on my barely cooled head.

The energy the boss monster was emitting was scattered throughout all directions. No matter which way I looked, I could sense its energy. In the ground, the sky, even in the terrible air flowing in and out of my lungs.

‘I have to find it!’

The moment I shouted internally, a light shock occurred in my chest as if something had cracked and broken.

An impossible field of vision opened up. There was ‘something’ in front of me. No one else seemed to know such a thing existed, but I could clearly sense it.

A ghost, no… an existence even more unclear than a ghost.

An opponent that couldn’t be seen, heard, or speak turned to look at me. The lips of that impossible existence opened.

{When the next sun rises, your victory is assured.}

“…I know what the boss is.”

Immediately, all attention focused on me. Everyone waited for what I would say.

“The boss might have been here from the beginning.”

The temperature too high for fire demons to have created, the smell of something burning vibrating throughout all directions.

The boss was this hellishly hot place itself.

And as soon as I grasped that fact, the heat surrounding us began getting uncontrollably hot as if launching a full-scale offensive. The feeling of all flesh cooking, even my eyes barely opening.

“Ah!”

Screams burst out from everywhere.

Dozens of fireball meteors began falling toward our heads from the sky.

Xuanwu vibrated painfully. Xuanwu extinguished the fireballs falling on us, but it had little effect. Even that cardinal deity couldn’t withstand this temperature that blocked our very breath regardless of range.

A dazed sense of despair emptied my insides completely black.

‘Even though I summoned a cardinal deity… it’s not resolved.’

Water kills fire, but if fire is too strong, it dries up the water. The strong fire ultimately pushed Ja Hyeong-woo back. The water flowing through Ja Hyeong-woo’s body dried up.

I bit my lips hard to come to my senses.

You said to endure. Until dawn breaks the next day inside the stage.

The sky was entirely crimson. I don’t know what time it is now, but…

‘There’s only one method.’

A thin S-grade sword was summoned from inventory. A sword of some earth attribute successor, with characteristics perfect for splitting the ground.

The S-grade sword Jin Seol-woon had once given me, and even at this point absolutely refused to take back.

‘Jin Seol-woon, I don’t have the right to do this after hurting you… but I’m borrowing it.’

Ja Hyeong-woo, having exhausted all his water, approached as if dying out and wrapped his body around me. I burned up all the remaining life force inside me to bloom one skill.

<Grave of the Dead (Earth Attribute)>

Kugugugung—! With a thunderous roar, the ground beneath us split open. Everyone was sucked inside.

I ground my teeth tightly, gripped the sword with both hands, and drew it horizontally across to close the earth above our heads.

A skill name that always seemed ominous whenever I saw it.

However, I would not make this place my team members’ grave.

If it had to become someone’s grave, the portion of just one person would be enough.

The greatly enlarged dog familiar supported the earth above me. The soil gradually covered the sky.

What I witnessed in the sky that was briefly visible at the end was a massive fireball pouring down onto the earth.

Thump! Eventually the ground closed completely like a lid.

Something shattered to pieces in my head. That enormous fire was sucked entirely into my body as impact.

The S-grade sword made a spine-chilling crack sound and broke in half in my hands.

After a long time, something hot surged up from inside.

My mind, my body. Everything that made me up was turned upside down and torn apart. While helplessly watching the extreme pain swallow me, I somehow raised my face.

Where I turned my head as if drawn, there was one person. And one person was running toward me.

The two healers I held dear.

They were shouting something, but I couldn’t hear it because of the ringing in my ears. I’m sorry. I wanted to say it out loud but couldn’t.

Of all people, you two had to see me again. I’m showing you once more the scene from your nightmares, aren’t I? What should I do?

But I couldn’t think anymore. From now on, thinking wasn’t my right. The thoughts that had been connecting to my distant head were cut off abruptly.

The last thing I perceived was that blood poured out like a fountain from where I’d opened my mouth.
