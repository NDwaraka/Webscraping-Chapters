After dozing a little and waking up, my mind became clear.

The blanket I’d been covered with was neatly spread out. I kicked it with my feet to crumple it up like my room’s blanket should be, and looked around the room that was mine alone.

Then I looked down at my clothes. I was wearing a white t-shirt and pants that I didn’t even know I had.

I hadn’t put them on myself.

I opened the closet and changed into my white sobok, my identity that I hadn’t worn in a long time. I erased the traces of Ja Hyeong-woo’s care one by one.

I wasn’t sleepy anymore and my head was completely clear. It was a signal that I was ready to return to normal.

I roughly tied up my hair that was long enough to reach my shoulders. I turned on the desk lamp and sat down.

I took out the original work notebook I’d hidden under the bookshelf. There were no traces that it had been discovered by Ja Hyeong-woo or the guys who had been coming in and out of this room.

The notebook itself is top-secret information, but I’m not too worried about it leaking.

The original content from before would just look like simple memos or a diary from the current perspective, since so much time has passed and I’ve messed up the dates.

I read the notebook carefully from the beginning.

The information notebook that had once been used to record original work information had changed from some page onward into a memo pad where I wrote down things I experienced directly.

From when I became leader, it was used as a notebook to research the successors’ skills and functions.

====

—Fusion skills

—Yin and yang characteristics

—Personal advanced skill ‘When dead, all become mere earth’

—Kids’ skill coordination

—Familiar management

—I’m the leader. I’m screwed.

====

I had periodically undergone heterogeneous changes. Then I experienced upheaval in Stage 33.

On the surface, I nearly died.

But inside, where no one could know, something had changed.

{When the next sun rises, your victory.}

I can’t forget the voice of the spirit boss I heard in the middle of that burning hell.

‘Have you ever heard a monster speak?’

Probably not. There would be no point asking other successors.

‘Maybe only I could hear it from the beginning.’

The world inside gates is full of unknowns, and when you learn one thing, other unknowns arise.

I’m gradually becoming distant from ordinary hunters. Even among fellow successors, I’m isolated alone.

I exhaled lightly and leaned back against the chair. Like when looking into other awakened ones, I focused on myself.

An incomparably clearer energy than before was circulating in my body.

Since I can’t express this with information from the original work or existing concepts from school.

I organized the energy I possessed with new words.

‘Mu (無/Void) attribute. An attribute that can accept anything or can accept nothing.’

It was different from simply having no attribute. Something exists. That’s probably why the <Boundary Between Life and Death> skill doesn’t disappear.

I’m close to spirit monsters, can touch soul stones, and have an extremely weakened body.

Instead, I break through awakened ones’ rules one by one and can directly touch the system.

I can’t prove anything with this right now…

But at least I won’t create another situation where I collapse so painfully or can only hide team members underground.

The existence of the final stage felt real, as if it were somewhere in the school. I could feel with my skin that the end was approaching.

“Gasp.”

During that short time walking to the training ground, I heard students gasping fourteen times.

“Congratulations on your return!”

“Congratulations!”

“Thank you!”

I’m really going crazy. What’s with saying thank you?

Which successor told them to do this? To salute when I pass by? Are they insane?

Students with moved tears welling in their eyes looked up at me admiringly. Some looked at me with complex eyes, then bowed their heads when our eyes met.

Many idiots deeply moved by Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s life.

‘Starting tomorrow I’ll borrow Sa Ye-ho’s clothes to wear. Throwing away the sobok.’

I’d never been ashamed of being Gyeon Gang-hyeon no matter what curses I received, but now I’m embarrassed. I should at least wear a disguise.

After experiencing this commotion even before entering the training ground, I was getting scared.

Inside the group training ground, I peeked at the familiar figures from behind the door.

‘What should I say when I go in? Should I ask if training is going well and enter confidently? Ugh… It’s cringey. I’m going crazy from embarrassment.’

“What are you doing?”

I jumped at the voice from behind me.

Jin Seol-woon, wearing his sword, was standing with his arms crossed, looking at me hiding with interest. The front of his hunter uniform shirt was slightly wet with sweat.

‘Uh…’

Sudden awkwardness rushed over me. I avoided his gaze for a moment, then gathered courage and faced Jin Seol-woon again, looking at his languid face.

“I was worried.”

A picture-perfect smile bloomed at Jin Seol-woon’s lips.

“I’d like to be alone with you a bit more… but we’ve already been caught.”

Jin Seol-woon muttered while glancing over my shoulder. While I was turning around going “huh?”, Jin Seol-woon’s hand instantly stretched out beside me.

Bang. Before I could do anything, he pushed the door open violently, making a sound. Jin Seol-woon pushed me forward.

“The leader is here.”

I was startled. The gazes that had already been gathering toward me turned into something like a herd of buffalo or rabbits at Jin Seol-woon’s shout.

“Gang-hyeon hyung…!”

Starting with Hae Mi-jun, who hugged me tightly with thick teardrops hanging from his eyes.

Yang Pyo waited for his turn beside us, then hugged me as soon as Hae Mi-jun let go. His thick arms quickly released me.

Quite surprised by that expression, I said something disappointing.

“Disgusting, man.”

“Me too.”

Yang Pyo, whose emotions had fled belatedly, looked disgusted.

Then when I looked behind, Sa Ye-ho was giggling while placing his hand on my shoulder. Jin Seol-woon, who was watching me quietly from a step behind, was a bonus.

“Ah really, ah. It was more comfortable when Woo Sae-geon was the leader. Really.”

Ma Cheon-ah walked over and spoke loudly as if for everyone to hear. She shook her head.

The moment I thought there was no peace of mind, I saw a small figure in front. I forgot everything I was thinking.

Won Ha-ri’s eyes were so sad. My heart stung as if I’d done something terribly wrong.

I can’t fathom Won Ha-ri’s feelings when she learned that her older brother had been hospitalized for a long time, and that I, whom she’d been projecting her brother onto, turned out to be in the same situation.

I’d hurt this sixteen-year-old too much.

“I think my luck is pretty bad.”

“I think so too.”

I just laughed carelessly and agreed.

I didn’t say anything special. Won Ha-ri spread her arms and hugged me tightly.

I gently placed my arms on Won Ha-ri’s back.

The recovery period was almost over now.

When I wake up tomorrow, I’ll go to the office first, gradually join training, and slowly get my condition back.

Before that.

I sat on the bed and glared at the doorway.

“You go home too. Stop staying over.”

Ja Hyeong-woo, who hadn’t been at the training ground, had been sulking at my house all this time.

I need to secure my territory. These past few days were special, but I can’t let him come and go from here like his own house normally.

If that happens, that bastard will think of it as cohabitation.

“…Nothing you forgot?”

While he was looking at me like “what am I supposed to do,” he immediately followed up with:

“Yang energy treatment.”

At those words, my neck stiffened as if I’d been hit in the back of the head.

“Wait, hey. Do you want to do that to a sick person?”

“You just said you were all better.”

“Is that what you call logic?”

Ja Hyeong-woo pulled himself closer to me. Flustered by the close distance, I tried to stop him with words since I couldn’t be the only one flustered.

“Just a little. I’ll do it gently.”

But our lips were already overlapping.

The atmosphere in the room was very still. Unusually, Ja Hyeong-woo barely moved even while kissing.

He who carefully pressed his lips to mine savored the faint vibration. Exchanging breaths, he lightly held flesh between his lips.

I can hear a pounding sound. It’s not me—Ja Hyeong-woo’s heart is beating too loudly.

Before tenderness, pure excitement travels through the hand cupping my cheek, seeping from trembling lips. I felt like I might become strange too.

The lips separated with a soft sound.

I opened my closed eyes.

“Feel better?”

Ja Hyeong-woo whispered near my lips with our foreheads touching.

The touching eyelashes tickled, making me frown first. I subtly avoided his gaze.

“You could heal with just this much, you bastard.”

What was all that stuff you did before then.

Feeling guilty, Ja Hyeong-woo avoided my gaze. Still, his hand remained on top of my head.

“Can you really sleep alone?”

“Do you want to get seriously cursed out and leave, or just leave?”

I gave Ja Hyeong-woo the finger. Ja Hyeong-woo saw that and still smiled slightly as he left.

I made a big show of falling asleep alone…

But I didn’t sleep properly. I woke up in the middle of the night as if someone had forcibly dragged me out.

‘…What?’

Not even a few seconds passed before I bolted upright.

Without much thought, I burst out of the house.

Not far away, a small figure of a person had collapsed.

The strange feeling I just had must have been sensing this kid’s energy. When I hurriedly tapped his shoulder, the boy woke up.

“You’re not a student at this school.”

More precisely, he wasn’t a student until just a few minutes ago.

The child looked slightly younger and smaller than fifteen, the school’s minimum age requirement. His dazed focus immediately fixed on me.

“What’s your name?”

“Jeong I-ryeong…”

I wrapped the boy, who looked cold, in spare clothes.

I don’t even need to check the system. I could see the boy’s identity.

“Starting tomorrow, the name you’ll use is Beom I-ryeong.”
