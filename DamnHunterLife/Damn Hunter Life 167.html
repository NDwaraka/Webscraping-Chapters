Damn Hunter Life Chapter 167

Going to the cafeteria would obviously draw attention to Beom I-ryeong. So I headed to my house.

Beom I-ryeong already went and sat familiarly at my house’s dining table. He even helped me move the dishes.

No matter how I look at him, he’s really well-behaved. He looks like a doll too. On top of that, his dining etiquette was perfect. This is my first time with a guy like this.

I secretly watched Beom I-ryeong as he quietly swallowed his food.

‘His mental state seems fine and everything looks okay, but he talks too little, which is a bit concerning. Maybe that’s just his personality.’

As soon as we finished eating, I went into Hunter School’s shop and checked how much of the Tiger successor’s house had been built. The progress had almost reached 100%.

“I’ll go to the bathroom for a bit.”

“Okay.”

After Beom I-ryeong left, my phone rang. It was a call from Hae Mi-jun.

[“I heard I-ryeong succeeded in manifesting his attribute?”]

It hadn’t even been a few hours, but news of Beom I-ryeong’s training results had already spread to all the successors. It probably went from Myo Cho-hui to Ma Cheon-ah, then from Ma Cheon-ah to everyone else.

The successor network is quite active now too. They play, eat, and do everything together even without me.

[“I-ryeong is really amazing. That’s great!”]

Hae Mi-jun gave sincere praise over the phone.

Looking at Beom I-ryeong might remind him of his past self, but rather than showing inferiority, he offered pure congratulations. That kind heart came through completely.

‘He’s better than me, really. The world turns because of you.’

After that, Won Ha-ri also called. Why are you calling too?

[“Don’t make me train together with him, okay?”]

I wondered what this was about.

Won Ha-ri, who has trauma(?) about joint training, suddenly clung to me. I chuckled.

“You’re not a newbie anymore, why worry about that?”

[“Oh, right. Anyway! I’ll work harder. I’ll be more useful.”]

“Don’t burn with competitive spirit against your junior.”

I smiled quietly and casually turned around, but a boy in white clothes was standing right in front of me.

“Gasp.”

My spine felt eerie for a moment.

Beom I-ryeong has no presence and is quiet, so I didn’t know he was behind me. Did he hear what I was saying?

…Well, I might talk about him a bit. After hanging up, I soon relaxed my posture again.

“Your quarters are finished now too. Go there and customize it according to the school shop’s guide. You can live alone, right?”

“…No. I’m scared.”

Beom I-ryeong’s eyes as he muttered that were utterly dry. I frowned slightly at the strangeness I could vaguely sense.

“Please let me stay with hyung-nim.”

* * *

“That’s how it turned out. Don’t come to my house at night anymore.”

It was a bolt from the blue announcement.

“Even if he looks mature, he’s still a kid. Maybe it’s because I’m the first person he met after falling into the school. I guess I’m the most comfortable to him. I’m going to keep him at my house for a while, then send him out when he adapts.”

The round, non-sharp tone couldn’t comfort Ja Hyeong-woo either.

If it weren’t for what Gyeon Gang-hyeon said next, he would have felt like the world was collapsing.

“Treat…ment will be done during the day in the office. Just briefly. When Sa Ye-ho isn’t there.”

The point was that there absolutely couldn’t be any hint about what he and Ja Hyeong-woo were doing to the young successors. (His face turned bright red when saying this.)

“If it’s because I-ryeong is at your house, you could come to my house to do it.”

“No. My schedule is all set during the day. Even with teleportation, I only have 30 minutes free.”

Ja Hyeong-woo actually had the desire for the whole world to know what kind of relationship he and Gang-hyeon had, but Gyeon Gang-hyeon strongly avoided it.

“Then at night at my house…”

“I have to watch the kid at home. Leave a child alone at night?”

Though he tried being clingy, Gyeon Gang-hyeon slipped away from his hands like sand grains. Left alone, Ja Hyeong-woo felt a gnawing hunger like someone who had starved for an entire day.

‘At least he didn’t say to eliminate meeting time altogether…’

At Ja Hyeong-woo’s feet standing in the training ground, a sensation like damp water pooling arises.

Water thoroughly soaks his feet, then climbs up his ankles to pull him down. It’s wariness close to anxiety.

Even though there’s nothing in front to glare at, Ja Hyeong-woo’s eyes sharpen fiercely.

“That guy’s expression is nasty again.”

The owner of a slightly husky voice compared to his smooth features appeared smoothly.

Sa Ye-ho clicked his tongue and positioned himself against the wall opposite Ja Hyeong-woo. Ja Hyeong-woo got straight to the point as if he’d been waiting.

“I don’t like Beom I-ryeong. It seems like he’s hiding something.”

Sa Ye-ho’s expression was ambiguous. From the start, the two weren’t close enough to readily believe what each other said.

“Evidence?”

“None. It’s intuition.”

Even the reason was absurd. Sa Ye-ho swept his hair behind his ear once.

“If you’re so confident, why don’t you go consult with Gyeon Gang-hyeon? Why are you telling me and making a fuss?”

Ja Hyeong-woo stared intently at Sa Ye-ho’s grumbling face. If he directly told Gang-hyeon that he suspected Beom I-ryeong…

“He might say I’m making false accusations out of jealousy.”

“Are you two still like that?”

Ja Hyeong-woo glared at him fiercely, meaning don’t act like you know. Soon he dropped his eyes down again.

“…That’s one reason, but it would be troublesome if words without evidence reached Beom I-ryeong’s ears.”

“Still, there should be at least some reason. If it’s not just jealousy over losing Gyeon Gang-hyeon, as you say.”

The answer to that took some time even for Ja Hyeong-woo. After choosing his words for a while, Ja Hyeong-woo slowly raised his head.

“His soul is unclear. Hard to see.”

What bullshit? Did you eat something wrong? Sa Ye-ho said with his expression. Ja Hyeong-woo corrected himself.

“I’m saying he doesn’t seem like an ordinary kid.”

Sa Ye-ho recalled the face of Beom I-ryeong, whom he’d only passed by or briefly met a few times.

Maybe because he’s unfamiliar, he seriously lacked social skills.

“Myo Cho-hui noona directly guided his training but didn’t say anything.”

However, Sa Ye-ho also…

“I do think he’s not childlike.”

If pressed, there was a sense of incongruity in personality aspects. After recalling Beom I-ryeong, whose ego seemed blurred, Ja Hyeong-woo’s expression of ‘no soul’ became somewhat understandable.

Eventually Sa Ye-ho replied with a slight snort mixed in.

“Fine. Either you or I will definitely be nearby. If circumstances don’t allow it, at least we’ll know his location.”

Sa Ye-ho was ultimately also on the side of protecting Gyeon Gang-hyeon. And this kind of caution could never be too much.

The small-framed boy suddenly opened his eyes on the bed.

“……”

Before his eyes, the scene of Ja Hyeong-woo talking with Sa Ye-ho was clearly visible. As if they had surveillance cameras attached to them.

Beom I-ryeong heard all the conversation they had.

This stage’s appearing monsters were estimated to be wood attribute.

It was a perfect place for Won Ha-ri, the natural enemy of wood, to go in and wreak havoc. So I put Won Ha-ri as first priority, and while choosing which successor to exclude, I let out a small sigh.

It felt like I had much more leeway than last time.

‘It’s fortunate that Beom I-ryeong appeared.’

I saw the number 10 for the first time in a while. It was a number where everyone up to the personnel limit could enter and still leave three reserve successors.

Besides the comfort that simply increased numbers give, the advantages Beom I-ryeong provides are significant.

Even considering that growth is fast in the early stages, that guy’s results were amazing. His stats increased meaningfully with just basic physical training.

Moreover, the boldness of not being particularly scared even when facing Ja Hyeong-woo, who’s about three times his size.

If trained well, Beom I-ryeong could become an excellent successor. These days I go to the training ground for the fun of raising that guy’s stats.

“Wait well until I come back.”

“Have a safe trip.”

As soon as I stepped into the stage, my thoughts returned to reality.

To call it ‘reality,’ this stage filled with lush landscaping is more unrealistic though.

Won Ha-ri walked briskly forward in front of me. Fluttering her black and white skirt, she drew her sword.

Up to here, the pattern would be the same as the previous Jangsan Tiger stage, but Won Ha-ri had prepared one more thing.

Behind Won Ha-ri, a smooth silver spirit appeared.

It was tall, had a round head, and a thin, long curled tail. And above all, it stood on two legs.

The first metal attribute familiar.

It had been months since Won Ha-ri manifested her monkey familiar, but it was unstable so she hadn’t properly brought it out until now.

But starting from when I nearly died and came back, Won Ha-ri became able to properly maintain her energy.

It’s something to be grateful and happy about… but I feel responsible.

‘Sorry. For making you work hard after seeing me in a near-death state.’

I remember when the monkey familiar first appeared. We were all expecting a comical appearance.

But, they say the evolutionary point of primates is bipedal locomotion. They say the remaining two feet became hands that could handle tools.

The moment a large monkey appeared holding a sword handle as long as its body, all laughter disappeared from us.
