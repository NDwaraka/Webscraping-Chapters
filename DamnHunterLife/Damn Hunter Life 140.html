Damn Hunter Life Chapter 140

Even if six of them joined forces to face it, that thing was the source of the hallucinations. If they carelessly provoked it, it might show even more horrific visions than now and try to destroy their minds. The risk was too great.

They couldn’t attack now.

At least, not unless they recharged their stamina to create a slightly more advantageous situation.

‘I need a stamina recovery skill.’

The problem was that the only healer here, Ja Hyeong-woo, wasn’t specialized in stamina recovery.

Should he have brought Hae Mi-jun instead of Ja Hyeong-woo? No, then dealing with injuries would have been difficult.

The sight of the A-rank boss leisurely watching them gave Gyeon Gang-hyeon a sense of humiliation. These monsters knew how to read their opponents’ momentum and killing intent.

The enemies could probably see that their side was currently drained of strength.

‘Four Guardian Deities skill…’

Gyeon Gang-hyeon immediately dismissed the thought that briefly arose. His only supreme-grade skill had to be saved for greater dangers.

‘No. …I can do that now.’

Even driven to the worst situation, Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s eyes didn’t lose their light. There was still another move.

Sa Ye-ho, wiping still-wet sweat from his forehead, grinned wickedly. It was a smile that wasn’t intimidated even in the difficult situation.

Perhaps gaining some strength, Sa Ye-ho broke the standoff with the boss and drew out his attribute skill first. Myo Cho-hui and Won Ha-ri followed him.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon barely managed to steady his trembling limbs and quietly assessed his condition. He was barely keeping his eyes open.

‘Ah, my stomach hurts…’

Then from behind, a thick forearm squeezed between his side and arm.

Ja Hyeong-woo’s hand gently rested over his clothes as if stroking his stomach. Despite having expended quite a bit of skill, the blue light reaching Gyeon Gang-hyeon was still deep and rich.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon, who couldn’t even curse due to pride, awkwardly raised his arm and groaned.

‘Now he’s touching me completely openly.’

Because this bastard is doing this shit, the community is also going crazy and crazy is everywhere… Gyeon Gang-hyeon calmed his brain that was about to pour out light-hearted complaints like before. He bit his lips to act serious.

The blood clot that had risen to his throat went back down. The searing hot pain in his stomach was also washed away like water. Gyeon Gang-hyeon had almost resigned himself to his body’s condition of having to depend on Ja Hyeong-woo’s healing.

But his thoughts didn’t stop.

Even though his body trembled with anxiety that he might die. Even though his situation suddenly felt pitiful.

Now, it was time to send him away.

‘Squeaky.’

When Ja Hyeong-woo finished treating him and headed toward the front battle scene, Gyeon Gang-hyeon secretly whispered.

Even if he was later criticized by people for having team members block the boss while the leader just stood around blankly, it didn’t matter.

Because reversing the predetermined order was more important. Though it was too late. At least now.

‘Go to Ja Hyeong-woo. And never come back.’

A nightmare he had once had came to mind. Many people dying, and Ja Hyeong-woo standing alone at that scene.

‘A time when high-grade gates open and many people die might come. From now on, coordinate with Ja Hyeong-woo in advance. Save other people.’

Once a master and familiar achieved perfect harmony, they would never again be able to stay attached to someone else while consuming energy like this.

In other words, it was farewell.

And if he sent away the mouse familiar, Gyeon Gang-hyeon would now have to perform his role as leader while letting go of the lifeline wrapped around his body.

The mouse familiar appeared in the form of a blue boy. It grabbed Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s arm.

{I don’t want to. Because master doesn’t want it.}

No, this excuse could no longer serve as a reason to change Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s mind. Just as Gyeon Gang-hyeon, who had already hardened his resolve, was about to say harsher words to make the boy turn away.

{…I don’t want to either.}

Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s face went blank. His stunned eyes stared at the boy only he could see.

Tears began flowing down the boy’s chin, drop by drop.

The second time seeing tears.

He remembered how young and impulsive the first mouse familiar had been. There was a time when it cried loudly thinking it had been completely abandoned by its master, Ja Hyeong-woo.

The mouse familiar at that time was desperate to kill Gyeon Gang-hyeon. Because it thought he would be an obstacle to its master.

‘Squeaky’s foresight…’

But now things were different. Gyeon Gang-hyeon acknowledged that he had been an obstacle to Ja Hyeong-woo.

And the mouse familiar was crying for Gyeon Gang-hyeon, its worry for him greater than returning to its master.

He thought this might be the last time seeing the blue boy’s form. Gyeon Gang-hyeon tried to find the two eyes in the faint features. He faced the boy squarely.

‘Thank you for taking care of me all this time.’

He could see his comrades fighting the boss in the distance gradually becoming exhausted. There was no time to delay. Gyeon Gang-hyeon gently pushed the mouse familiar back as if nudging it, severing his connection with it.

‘Come to think of it… I can do this kind of thing now. I didn’t learn it anywhere.’

Then, Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s eyes widened.

‘Huh?’

His dog familiar popped out from him.

The brown boy with tears beaded on his chin ran over and embraced the blue boy.

‘When did they…’

The mouse familiar didn’t push away the dog familiar either. It obediently buried its body deeply in that embrace.

The larger brown boy soon lifted his head and raised his hand to wipe the blue boy’s tears. The transparent tears both shed fell endlessly to the ground.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon watched the scene unable to avoid it. Wrapped in a dizzy feeling as if the entire sky and earth were spinning, as if he had become a celestial body in the universe.

‘…Strange. What’s strange about this?’

Something seemed about to surface. His throat was endlessly choked, and while wanting to tear at his chest from frustration, on the other hand his strength was completely drained.

“Sa Ye-ho! To your side!”

Sa Ye-ho, whose quick mobility had been his lifeline, became noticeably sluggish. The moment he passed by the fierce tiger’s side, the tiger twisted its body faster than Sa Ye-ho could steady his posture.

“Ahhhhh!”

Finally large claws struck down at his body.

On his fluttering floral hanbok, even redder flowers bloomed. The tiger’s teeth were about to embed in Sa Ye-ho’s small head the next moment.

Then Won Ha-ri flew over rolling and kicked the tiger away. Since metal attribute’s stat was strength, she was now the strongest among the successors after Woo Sae-geon left.

However, in a state of having lost much stamina, even Won Ha-ri was merely placed under the tiger’s giant claws.

Blood vessels stood out all over Ja Hyeong-woo’s body as he cast healing with the feeling of squeezing all his blood vessels to cure Sa Ye-ho’s fatal wound.

At that moment.

“…!”

Ja Hyeong-woo’s eyes opened wide.

Massive energy rose before his eyes as if a deep blue sea was spreading out.

Blue light surged up the dark mountain as if a tsunami was striking. The light was so enormous that even the large-framed Ja Hyeong-woo looked very small.

But it was unmistakably power emanating from one human’s body.

Power to save other living beings. Power worthy of being called a ‘miracle’ by people.

In that instant, Jin Seol-woon, who caught Sa Ye-ho, turned over his unconscious body.

Sa Ye-ho’s tattered back was cleanly healed just from Ja Hyeong-woo’s waves brushing it, revealing even the vivid muscle texture.

The explosively generated power blurred even the boundary between stamina and injury. Without meaning to distinguish between the two, it was truly power that lifted everything. Even Sa Ye-ho, who had collapsed, opened his eyes before long.

The shape of a large mouse was reflected behind Ja Hyeong-woo. Standing tall in the storm that made all observers hold their breath and stare, Ja Hyeong-woo was…

Completely inappropriate for the situation, he was only staring blankly at the wrong direction behind him.

A devastated face that looked like it might shed a tear simply gazed at Gyeon Gang-hyeon.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon also quietly faced Ja Hyeong-woo. After months had passed, it was finally the scene from the original work’s Busan Gate that Gyeon Gang-hyeon remembered.

Stage 44 would become known as the place where Ja Hyeong-woo, the world’s 2nd-ranked healer, manifested his mouse familiar and rose to 1st place.

When doing partial anesthesia, if he anesthetized the brain.
