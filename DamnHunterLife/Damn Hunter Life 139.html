Damn Hunter Life Chapter 139

When Won Ha-ri cut down monsters individually at the front, the monsters she dismantled were finished off by the remaining successors who kept a certain distance.

Though slightly unstable, Won Ha-ri completely unleashed her power through Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s support. While swinging her sword without rest, exerting influence as if Woo Sae-geon were there with them—

{Jump down from there!}

Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s voice rang out clearly.

Won Ha-ri flinched. A crack appeared in her movement for a moment.

“…!”

Half the successors also stirred together. Their movements became slightly disturbed, creating gaps.

“Ugh!”

Jin Seol-woon barely avoided having his arm bitten by a tiger.

The boss had appeared. In the middle of fighting small fry.

It wasn’t visible to their eyes. Rather than overwhelming them with massive size, this stage’s boss hid in that darkness disguised as other small fry.

It had become much more troublesome.

==

Snake Successor: Jangsan tiger bullshit starts

==

Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s eyes carefully swept the surroundings.

Even after hearing with his own two ears that vivid voice so identical to his own, his expression showed no change.

==

Me: From now on I’ll only give orders through chat

Me: The voice you hear from now isn’t me

==

Jin Seol-woon had just been lucky, had excellent reflexes in that moment, and barely avoided injury. If he’d been off by even an inch, he could have been immediately mauled by the tiger. To avoid danger, there couldn’t be even the slightest confusion.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon rather instructed everyone to keep their mouths shut. Starting with himself using only chat.

==

Me: Keep track of your teammates’ locations every moment, look at their energy not their visible appearance

Me: Don’t waver even if monsters take human form

==

Myo Cho-hui’s eyes, as she strangled tiger bodies with vines, intermittently moved toward other directions.

Sometimes her mother stood among the tigers. Sometimes her father’s arm came trembling out from behind a tree as if in pain.

Both parents reached out to Myo Cho-hui while being trampled by tigers.

{Ahhh, Cho-hui!}

{It hurts so much, help me!}

Myo Cho-hui’s face fixed into numbness. Her glaive spun once above her hand, then mercilessly stabbed that spot. The tiger’s death cry rang out.

‘Disgusting. How dare you copy whose face and voice?’

Jin Seol-woon, who had withdrawn to the rear instead of fighting as a dealer with a sword, was grinding his molars from the impact spreading through his body occasionally.

Maybe because they were A-rank, the monsters’ intelligence was quite high. They recognized that the dragon blocking their path had limits—that it was an existence that would disappear when its stamina ran out if they kept attacking.

The tigers’ teeth and claws attacking the dragon familiar’s body were steadily wearing down its master, Jin Seol-woon.

‘Still, with Ja Hyeong-woo-ssi’s healing coming in, it’s manageable. I need to join up quickly…’

Jin Seol-woon, who unconsciously looked toward the middle of the monsters, momentarily forgot what he’d been thinking.

There was another Gyeon Gang-hyeon there too. Wrapped in white clothes, standing precariously.

His plump, soft-looking lips parted.

{How far can you give up? To date me.}

The fact that the being the Jangsan tiger was imitating wasn’t an Innate member weighed heavily on Jin Seol-woon’s chest.

He turned his head this way and that to shake off the hallucination. He absolutely mustn’t be charmed, mustn’t respond even carelessly. It was being recorded.

It would be troublesome if people inferred who the target of his hallucinations was.

==

Dog Successor: Jin Seol-woon, are you okay?

==

Then the status window floating before his eyes became clear. Jin Seol-woon, who stared intensely at the text, unconsciously let out a big breath.

How comforting a single cold text window could be.

Only Gyeon Gang-hyeon remained unswept away by anything, endlessly shooting arrows. He continuously sent chats to everyone without rest. He redirected his dazed teammates’ gazes back to combat.

Except for Myo Cho-hui, everyone else was a hindrance. Whatever they were seeing in hallucinations, even Sa Ye-ho occasionally had delays in his movements and moved his lips as if saying something.

‘It’s hyung.’

Even with monsters charging at them, they were getting disturbed in front of them, so only Gyeon Gang-hyeon, who had to save everyone, could reach the extreme of exhaustion.

One thing—though it didn’t linger long, there was a question that definitely crossed Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s mind.

‘The Jangsan tiger isn’t showing me anything or talking to me. To me it just looks like the original tiger form.’

==

Me>Mouse Successor: Hey

Me>Mouse Successor: Answer

==

Calling out to Ja Hyeong-woo last among the four, Gyeon Gang-hyeon recalled yesterday’s conversation and twisted his lips slightly.

Yesterday, before the stage, he had called Ja Hyeong-woo aside separately. There was something he needed to ask only of him.

“If the Jangsan tiger mentally attacks you… don’t make it obvious it’s me. Please.”

What Ja Hyeong-woo muttered after a long while still remained in Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s mind, tormenting him.

“You seem confident I’ll see you as a hallucination.”

At that time, Gyeon Gang-hyeon wanted to squeeze his eyes shut if his heart could have its way. He’d known he would say such things. He knew, but couldn’t help it.

“Do you know now? How much I care about you.”

“Hey.”

Even as Gyeon Gang-hyeon jabbed at him harshly as if startled, Ja Hyeong-woo softened his eyes gently as if not taking even a needle’s worth of damage. He even spouted worse words.

“You seem happy about it.”

Gyeon Gang-hyeon had somehow felt like the guy was deliberately trying to fluster him.

“If you don’t promise, I’ll remove you from this stage roster and put in Hae Mi-jun instead.”

“Is that a threat?”

“Yeah.”

Ja Hyeong-woo, who had been having the same recollection as Gyeon Gang-hyeon, soon returned to reality.

A large voice laughing brightly kept embracing Ja Hyeong-woo’s mind as he tried to concentrate on healing skills.

Making him want to give his whole mind over.

{Hyeong-woo! Han Hyeong-woo! This mountain is huge! Wow! Look at this! Here!}

If I look, I’ll definitely run over there.

Ja Hyeong-woo didn’t look that way. Then another voice was heard.

{Stupid, that’s fake! Don’t listen to that and come here. Ja Hyeong-woo!}

It was exactly like Gang-hyeon’s adult voice now. Even though he’d gone through puberty the same as himself, how could his voice be so pleasant to hear?

‘Two hallucinations…’

Then the situation changed drastically once more.

“Cough!”

Due to the monsters’ persistent attacks, the dragon familiar was reverse-summoned. The protective barrier that had been guarding them disappeared.

As a result, Jin Seol-woon breathed roughly and staggered. His body tilted once then returned with Ja Hyeong-woo’s support.

At that moment their gazes briefly collided, not entirely pleasantly…

Soon Jin Seol-woon, who had erased personal feelings, accepted Ja Hyeong-woo’s help to stand up.

After urgently telling Myo Cho-hui and Sa Ye-ho to secure space, Gyeon Gang-hyeon focused on Won Ha-ri.

Won Ha-ri’s posture while cutting down tigers was gradually crumbling. But that wasn’t from exhaustion.

==

Me: I told you not to look! It’s a hallucination!

==

Gyeon Gang-hyeon desperately held back his urge to shout for Won Ha-ri with all his might, only biting his lips.

Right, there had been an aspect of pushing too hard from the beginning. Won Ha-ri was too young.

And for her age, she carried too great a sorrow.

“…But you know, I couldn’t do anything for my brother. If he’d lived healthy like that for just one more year, then maybe I wouldn’t have regrets. There were so many things I wanted to do for him…”

Seeing tears finally streaming down her small cheeks, a deflated sigh escaped Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s mouth.

Enough. Won Ha-ri had fought well enough.

==

Me: Won Ha-ri, retreat now

==

Only then did Won Ha-ri’s consciousness snap back. Won Ha-ri, who had stabbed three tigers in front of her to death, showed confusion. Her shouted voice cracked.

“What do we do if I don’t block them? What do we do!”

==

Me: It’s okay. Trust my words

==

In the end, Won Ha-ri retreated according to Gyeon Gang-hyeon’s order. Immediately the tigers from the side she’d been blocking poured out like a flood.

Their attention focused on Gyeon Gang-hyeon. He faced the tigers calmly without a hint of tension.

‘The attributes don’t match, but.’

He released the energy he’d gathered strongly from within his body. In the form of a skill.

“…!”

An impact like being struck all over by a heavy club. A sharp scream burst from his lungs.

But the skill activation succeeded.

<When You Die Everything Becomes Earth (Earth Attribute)>

The tiger that had been charging with its mouth wide open turned to earth with a thud.

An advanced skill he’d manifested but tried not to use unless absolutely necessary.

But now, even if it meant suffering major internal injuries, he had to show his teammates and people. There was a need to reverse the flow once.

To prove that even if the current leader was physically weak, he could kill dozens of A-rank monsters with purely the power of his attribute skills alone.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon swallowed the hot pain rising from within and targeted one spot in his vision.

‘It’s there.’

While all surrounding monsters returned to handfuls of earth and scattered, only one tiger maintained its original form.

However… no successor had preserved enough energy to face an A-rank boss.
