Damn Hunter Life Chapter 176

Actually, there was a separate reason Park Ho-gyeong was at school despite it being vacation.

Because his father had appeared on a broadcast a few weeks ago.

[“There were really many people curious about what kind of person the 2nd generation successor leader Gang-hyeon was.”]

His father, who he thought was just part of the Hunter Association’s administrative team, turned out to be personally assisting the person Park Ho-gyeong found most disagreeable.

“Dad was Gang-hyeon’s manager? Why didn’t you tell me! Ah!”

His twin sister Park Ho-jeong’s booming voice made his ears feel like they would burst.

“Dad took care of our Gang-hyeon and even made him awaken! Amazing, well done!”

She’s excited. Watching Park Ho-jeong cheerfully squealing with renewed energy, Park Ho-gyeong felt uncomfortable and turned his head.

Then his eyes met with his father’s.

“Are you surprised? This is my job. I’m proud of myself for looking after Gang-hyeon-nim. I hope you can also do work that satisfies you.”

His father knew that he had been cursing Gang-hyeon loudly like a joke with his friends. Yet he smiled at him.

“I’ll go get some fresh air.”

From that day on, he wasn’t at home and often went out.

Park Ho-gyeong was upset that his father was completely devoted to the successor he had mocked and criticized so much.

Even when they said the delinquent had changed and become a leader, he had just sneered, saying the past wouldn’t disappear anyway.

Of course, the past doesn’t disappear.

……The years Gang-hyeon spent fooling around don’t disappear, but the longer years he struggled and fought illness also don’t disappear.

Park Ho-gyeong looked at Gang-hyeon leading him out of the collapsed department building and handing him over to rescue workers.

‘He must have seen father’s face for a long time.’

Park Ho-gyeong looked a lot like his father. But Gang-hyeon didn’t recognize him. He quickly carried him up like lightning and hurried away without exchanging a single word.

While his guilt lightened somewhat, he simultaneously felt strange. Park Ho-gyeong’s heart squirmed and swayed strangely.

What should he do if he was uncomfortable with his father bowing his head to someone his own age?

‘The answer. I should also serve and revere him.’

Our family can just become a full-fledged secretary household. From now on, I’ll call him hyung-nim. Anyone who curses Gang-hyeon in front of me can die.

Park Ho-gyeong decided to become a new person.

* * *

The empty road at night sparkled with the bike’s illumination.

Yoon Sae-geon was straddling a newly acquired bike instead of the broken one.

With small lights on and wearing a helmet, the bike turned its wheels with small noise and raced down the road.

The dim scenery with occasional streetlights quickly passed through his vision. Within that, Yoon Sae-geon saw something strange.

A small person was standing in the grass.

But when he blinked, nothing was there. Was it an illusion? Yoon Sae-geon turned the corner and raced down the expanded lanes. The cold wind gave a refreshing sensation without penetrating his thick clothes.

At that moment, the same figure flashed by from grass in another direction.

A small force entered his temples inside the helmet.

He didn’t believe in ghosts. So he dismissed spirit monsters as merely simple monsters. He trusted only his own intuition.

That’s why he had to stop. The air surrounding him was ominous. His instincts warned him to turn back.

The moment he thought he should change direction.

A small-figured person appeared right in front. It was in the middle of the road.

Yoon Sae-geon turned the handle to the maximum. Screech— the wheels left rough marks on the road. With athletic abilities far from ordinary people, he barely swerved away.

If only the boy hadn’t thrown himself in front of the bike.

Thud!

The bike crumpled like clay. It was damaged and even corroded to the extent that if the bike had life, it would all have been taken away.

That force even collided with the driver’s body.

Yoon Sae-geon, who crashed onto the road, didn’t move. The small figure shuffled its feet.

Beom I-ryeong crouched in front of him. He pulled off the helmet and threw it on the ground.

Gripping the other’s neck like a handle, he dragged him away.

* * *

The lights in the successor training ground finally went out.

“Why do you guys volunteer for overtime? Are you suckers?”

“Unlike the Leader, we have energy to spare, so what?”

“I’m dying of sleepiness…… I’ll let it slide today but adjust the training amount from tomorrow. Damn bastards.”

They came out of the training ground in a group. They walked down the dark corridor making trivial conversation.

“What?”

Then the lights in an empty conference room suddenly turned on.

Light from the door crack stretched long into the dark corridor. Yang Pyo got scared first and grabbed my arm.

With a strange feeling, I slowly moved in that direction. Then Ja Hyeong-woo went in ahead of me.

There was nothing strange inside the conference room.

But the screen was different.

[“You’re late. I almost killed this person waiting.”]

There was a familiar face beyond the screen. Beom I-ryeong, sitting with crossed legs in a dead-end room, smiled at me.

The dialogue was the same as last time. The difference was that Beom I-ryeong was outside and I was trapped inside the school.

Even though it was a space I’d never seen before, I was certain that place was outside.

After seeing the person lying face down at Beom I-ryeong’s feet.

While everyone froze and couldn’t say anything, Sa Ye-ho pressed close to the screen.

Sa Ye-ho’s pupils gradually dilated. I urgently reached out to Sa Ye-ho.

“Aaaaah!”

Sa Ye-ho let out a death-like scream.

His eyes became bloodshot and he thrashed wildly. He tried to rush at Beom I-ryeong, who he couldn’t possibly reach, and destroy the screen. I hugged Sa Ye-ho tightly.

“Ugh!”

However, I soon got hit and bounced away.

At this moment, solid arms supported me so I wouldn’t fall. Ja Hyeong-woo gently set me down and immediately strode over to grasp both of Sa Ye-ho’s arms. It was strength of a completely different class from just before.

“Aaaaah!”

But where did such strength come from? Even when Ja Hyeong-woo expanded all his muscles, it wasn’t enough.

Seeing Ja Hyeong-woo who seemed to be seriously considering knocking him unconscious by hitting him, I presented a more certain method.

“Yang Pyo!”

Yang Pyo, who had been standing dazed, was startled by my call.

Yang Pyo had a knockout skill. He took away consciousness with the skill that had the fewest aftereffects.

Only then did Sa Ye-ho become quiet. Ja Hyeong-woo handed Sa Ye-ho’s body to Yang Pyo like a piece of luggage.

I regulated my breathing evenly and glared at Beom I-ryeong.

“That person isn’t a successor, he’s a civilian. Do you even know?”

Calm but not refined.

“This fucking shameless bastard.”

The detestable face that still maintained a boy’s appearance recited words as if bored.

[“That’s right. Let’s talk again after cleaning up the corpse.”]

“Don’t! Stop!”

However, the large metal rod Beom I-ryeong held drove down hard into Yoon Sae-geon’s lower back, precisely where his spine was.

There was a chilling sound of bones breaking.

I quickly checked from behind. I saw Ma Cheon-ah covering Won Ha-ri’s eyes.

Jin Seol-woon’s expression was distorted but he had strong mentality so he’d be fine. Myo Cho-hui was obviously fine.

Hae Mi-jun and Yang Pyo couldn’t block it because they didn’t have the capacity, but they were holding up without collapsing.

I glared at the screen again. I hoped luck would follow. I prayed for Yoon Sae-geon to breathe.

But…….

Yoon Sae-geon was conscious.

As soon as I confirmed his intermittent twitching movements with clenched fists, breathing became difficult to control.

Just as I was about to ask what he wanted, Beom I-ryeong first narrowed his eyes toward me.

[“Come here.”]

The same words without fail.

But this time, it sounded like he literally meant to come to that place where he was.

‘If a gate didn’t open, there’s no way I could go there. That’s Korea, and I’m at Hunter School.’

I thought it might work with the dispatch function since Beom I-ryeong was a monster, but that function didn’t work either. The cause isn’t exact.

Perhaps…… it’s because Beom I-ryeong messed with the system while disguising as a successor. Plus he would have damaged the system more by secretly leaving school.

While thinking such thoughts, a certain idea suddenly flashed.

If there was a crack in the previously perfect system.

“You want me to come there?”

[“Yes. Come here.”]

“Fine.”

I readily agreed.

Beom I-ryeong wasn’t very surprised. Only the kids around me seemed shocked. Especially Ja Hyeong-woo gripped my arm tightly.

‘Getting scared first without even knowing how I’m supposed to get out.’

I chose. To follow Beom I-ryeong’s demand.

Because an important person’s life was at stake, I brought out the secret I had been saving and took a step into danger.

[<Boundary Between Life and Death (No Attribute)> is activated.]

The half-complete skill that first appeared when I had just awakened.

But now I know. This skill is also complete.

Because the rules of this school are me.

Using the energy I felt while fixing the system as a guide, I groped to find the crack in the system. And I placed my skill there.

Since I am an entity whose abilities, substance, and myself as a person are all unclear.

Crack—! Something like a flat wall I didn’t even know existed shattered away.

As if he didn’t expect it to come so suddenly, Beom I-ryeong’s eyes widened as he looked down at the floor. On that floor, my foot that popped out from thin air was placed.

As if I had crossed dimensions.

“I’m here. What now?”

It was a warehouse like an abandoned building. He must have watched some noir movie. I let out a small laugh with slight mockery.

“But who said only I would come?”

I didn’t just use a trick to cross the boundary alone.

I broke the prohibition of Hunter School. Making myself into the system, I constructed new rules.

The successors also followed me across. S-rank hunters accumulated one by one in the abandoned warehouse.

Beom I-ryeong’s expression gradually crumbled. I sneered and ordered toward the back.

“Wake up Sa Ye-ho.”
