Damn Hunter Life Chapter 162

* * *

It had been three days.

Strength returned to the arm that had an IV needle inserted until yesterday. Color came back to his bloodless eyelids.

Gyeon Gang-hyeon opened his eyes on his own.

The first thing his dim, hazy pupils recognized.

Ja Hyeong-woo’s expression brightened when their eyes met where his gaze reached.

A large hand suddenly appeared before his eyes. Gang-hyeon quietly watched Ja Hyeong-woo’s approaching hand, then gently closed his eyes when the hand touched his face.

The large hand checked his forehead, then slowly caressed his face along his cheek. Gang-hyeon’s eyelids slowly closed and opened. His brow smoothed as if enjoying the stroking touch.

Ja Hyeong-woo was entranced by the sight. The next moment, he felt a sudden tension.

“……Gang-hyeon. Can you tell me who I am?”

Only then did an annoyed expression cross Gang-hyeon’s face. A dry, cracked voice leaked out.

“Ja Hyeong-woo.”

Ja Hyeong-woo let out a light sigh. He helped Gang-hyeon sit up and gave him a few sips of water.

Just sitting up left him breathless. He struggled to swallow the water that Ja Hyeong-woo held to his lips.

Gang-hyeon, who docilely accepted his touch, looked too gaunt and haggard even to be examined with just one’s eyes.

Ja Hyeong-woo took the finished cup and set it down. The arm that had been supporting his back pulled Gang-hyeon’s waist closer.

When he bent his tree-trunk-like torso, their chests gently touched.

He wrapped both arms around Gang-hyeon’s body. With his eyes closed, he used his whole body to remember the other’s heartbeat and breathing, the proof that he was alive.

Gang-hyeon, who seemed a little startled, obediently rested his cheek on his shoulder. Though it was just an embrace, his entire body grew warm without any gaps.

Before long, he dozed off in Ja Hyeong-woo’s arms. Ja Hyeong-woo’s arms gently patted his back.

“Want to try eating some porridge?”

Since he didn’t refuse, Ja Hyeong-woo sat on the bed and spoon-fed him porridge that had been cooled to the right temperature, little by little.

He seemed like a different person. Was it because he had no strength at all? The way he obediently opened his mouth small and accepted the food.

Gang-hyeon silently chewed what was in his mouth. Whether he knew that Ja Hyeong-woo was pleased with each spoonful that went in or not, he remained dazed throughout.

It wasn’t a good sign. If he had even a little energy, he would have constantly grumbled or stubbornly insisted on doing it himself.

“I made the porridge. Is it edible?”

“……”

“You never really liked porridge. Would it be better if I heated up some sikhye instead? Sikhye is hot when you first make it, you know.”

Even without responses, he kept talking to him. The quiet voice that casually said anything was quite chatty. If someone else saw this, they would doubt whether this was really Ja Hyeong-woo.

“It snowed outside. It’s the first snow I’ve seen at school. It would have been nice to see it with you.”

What was there wasn’t ‘Ja Hyeong-woo’ but Han Hyeong-woo.

Gang-hyeon, who had been laid back down on the bed, suddenly mumbled.

“……The status window is quiet.”

Ja Hyeong-woo propped his arm beside his head and leaned down. He listened carefully.

“Since I’m the student council president…… notifications should come. I can’t hear them. Can’t see them…… Have I become strange? Is this a dream?”

He was already half asleep. Even as his voice gradually diminished, memories from the depths of his mind continued to be dragged out.

“What about school? The people outside? The stage……”

Ja Hyeong-woo exhaled deeply in two parts. He stroked Gang-hyeon’s head.

“Other people are watching over the school. Rest more.”

Gang-hyeon doesn’t know even in his dreams. What kind of existence he has become in Korea right now.

‘He’ll be very confused, so it would be better not to tell him for a while and let him focus on recovery.’

Gang-hyeon felt the touch caring for him even in his sleep.

When he came to his senses, Ja Hyeong-woo was beside him. Every time he woke up, he would eat a few spoonfuls of porridge and fall asleep again repeatedly.

When he sat up half-awake, Ja Hyeong-woo would place a pillow behind his back. He never once refused the spoon that fed him. He said he cooked different porridge each meal, but he couldn’t really taste it.

When he stepped his feet under the bed to go to the bathroom, an arm immediately came to his side. When he tried to lie down again right after coming back, Ja Hyeong-woo stopped him. Saying he needed to digest, so he couldn’t lie down for at least 20 minutes.

So when they sat side by side on the bed with his waist resting against Ja Hyeong-woo’s arm, he would unknowingly doze off and rest his small head on the shoulder beside him.

Quiet time as if only the two of them, himself and Ja Hyeong-woo, existed in the world.

He wasn’t aware of anyone else’s presence at all.

It wasn’t that there really wasn’t anyone, but thanks to Ja Hyeong-woo thoroughly controlling who came and went.

He only occasionally let others come to see Gang-hyeon’s sleeping face when he was asleep.

‘To Gang-hyeon, the other successors are closer to objects of responsibility. If they’re nearby, he won’t be able to rest properly.’

One afternoon, the head buried deep in the pillow fell completely asleep.

Ja Hyeong-woo quietly stood up.

He stood in the doorway facing the direction where Gang-hyeon lay. He stood back-to-back with someone standing outside the door.

“……You probably know the story about how Gang-hyeon initially ran away when awakening was imminent.”

“I didn’t see it directly.”

Sa Ye-ho muttered in an uncharacteristically low tone. The sound flowed in through the door crack.

“But what does that have to do with anything now?”

“He’s still sleeping too much. It’s not just a physical problem. He seems mentally depressed.”

Ja Hyeong-woo with a dark face stared at the bed.

“That’s why I’m saying this. Gang-hyeon battled illness for over 7 years, and even after awakening, he couldn’t escape from bodily pain. He was actually afraid of hunter work too. ……I think he might have reached his limit now.”

Ja Hyeong-woo was even considering having Gang-hyeon stop fighting and retreat to the rear. Of course, he would have to ask his opinion first.

The person beyond the door, as if seeing through Ja Hyeong-woo’s inner thoughts, asked a question that skipped several steps.

“Will you enter stages even without Gyeon Gang-hyeon?”

It was a pointed question asking if he would work hard even in places without him. Ja Hyeong-woo responded coldly.

“I’ll approach it more desperately than you, so mind your own business.”

Sa Ye-ho grumbled and cursed something. Ja Hyeong-woo didn’t even listen and flipped through his status window screens.

It was an interview request sent by the association. He had ignored it for three days while staying by Gang-hyeon’s side, and at this rate, they would probably bring lawyers.

Ja Hyeong-woo irritably grabbed the doorknob and pushed the door forcefully.

Thanks to that, only Sa Ye-ho, who had been leaning against the outer door, tumbled over.

“Watch over Gang-hyeon while I’m gone.”

Ja Hyeong-woo had no interest whether Sa Ye-ho fell or not. Sa Ye-ho, who flexibly regained his balance, immediately glared.

“Fucking asshole asking for favors.”

Ja Hyeong-woo’s impatient eyes were still fierce.

“Just touch him or play around and wake him up. I’ll make sure it gets posted online as a violence incident.”

“Wow…… using your brain for once. Fucking bastard with a rotten personality.”

Ja Hyeong-woo passed by without even responding. Sa Ye-ho was dumbfounded and stared at the retreating back of his head for a long time.

He seriously wanted to punch him, but soon remembered. That Ja Hyeong-woo’s mental state was completely messed up right now.

‘I’m the normal one who has to endure this. Ugh, fuck.’

He quietly closed the door.

As if he had turned once, Gang-hyeon was lying on his side facing this way. His cheeks were so hollow that his flesh didn’t even press against the sheets. Sa Ye-ho, looking down at the haggard face, chuckled.

“Our puppy. Your fate is rough, and your boyfriend’s temper is dog-like too.”

Lightly ignoring Ja Hyeong-woo’s warning not to touch him, he tapped the round forehead with his hand.

He climbed onto the bed. Sitting with both legs dangling down, he looked at his phone. His free left hand naturally pressed beside him, and small breaths near his wrist touched his skin. Even knowing this, Sa Ye-ho didn’t bother to move his hand.

Muted game effects exploded on his phone screen. Sa Ye-ho’s right thumb moved busily.

Too absorbed in the game, he didn’t even notice that Gang-hyeon had opened his eyes behind him.

Even after waking up, his vision didn’t become clear. Gang-hyeon blinked his sleepy eyes. Then he discovered the large hand in front of him.

It was similarly large in size, but the skin was much paler. However, his drowsy eyes couldn’t properly see that difference. He moved his weak arm and pulled that hand toward him.

The warm hand flinched slightly but obediently followed. Gang-hyeon buried it between his chin and collarbone. He curled up his body slightly and closed his eyes again.

‘Oh my.’

Sa Ye-ho didn’t pull his hand away and snorted. Game over appeared on his phone screen, but he had already lost interest in that.

From then on, he docilely left his hand there and kept looking toward the door. When would Ja Hyeong-woo come back quickly?

Before long, his waiting bore fruit.

Ja Hyeong-woo’s gaze, upon opening the door, was deeply fixed on one spot. To Sa Ye-ho’s eyes, he looked like a beast.

“Why are you holding it?”
