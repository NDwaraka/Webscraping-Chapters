The timing was so terribly coincidental that I lost my composure without realizing it.

“…Who is it?”

The moment my mouth opened, Mungmung hurriedly hid inside me. Whether it was Squeaky or Mungmung, they never showed their true forms to others – that was the same.

The door burst open.

It was Sa Ye-ho. The tension completely melted away.

“Why are you knocking and then coming in? What kind of concept is that?”

I expected him to giggle, but Sa Ye-ho’s expression was somehow displeased. Moreover, he swept his gaze across the room once as he entered.

“How would I know what kind of mischief you’re up to inside?”

What is he talking about?

Well, I’m working in here, what else would I be doing? Bewildered, I turned my attention to yesterday’s school settlement details.

Thanks to Sa Ye-ho, the fear that had dominated me until just now disappeared. As my emotions settled, I could return to daily life.

The system that seems to be watching me even changing stage numbers… Ah geez, I don’t know. Let’s think about this later.

Sa Ye-ho didn’t go to the sofa but immediately sat down on the edge of the desk where I was working.

I narrowed my eyes and looked up at him.

Sa Ye-ho, still with a displeased expression, suddenly nudged my arm with his foot. It was a white bare foot without even socks.

“My snake eyes skill broke.”

And he spat out words more ominous than his face.

“Snake eyes skill? Why did that break?”

“It rotted. I saw something I shouldn’t have seen and my snake eyes rotted.”

What is he talking about? I looked Sa Ye-ho up and down with suspicious eyes.

There are cases where skills themselves change, whether for better or worse.

My ‘Boundary Between Life and Death’ skill was like that, and when the year changed and Yang Pyo turned 20, some of his skills were enhanced for the better.

‘But Sa Ye-ho hasn’t had anything happen that would cause that…’

No matter how light-headed Sa Ye-ho was, he wasn’t someone who would joke about skills to me, who had become the leader.

But right now, to my eyes… I couldn’t feel any change in energy from Sa Ye-ho’s body. Sa Ye-ho’s fire was exactly the same as before.

So I was about to ask if he was mistaken.

“So it was when we went to support the Cheonan gate.”

After building up something grand, he suddenly cut off his words and squinted his eyes.

Sa Ye-ho abruptly lifted his butt from the desk. The one step that had been soundlessly light became snake-like slithering steps the next moment. He opened the office door, looked around left and right, then closed it.

What is he trying to say that he’s even checking if there’s anyone outside to overhear?

Sa Ye-ho, who returned to my side, still stared at me intently with an expression hard to gauge.

As if he was particularly carefully watching my reaction. As if he would catch even the slightest change in the direction of the fine hair on my face.

“While detecting the boss, the leader and healer disappeared and didn’t come back. Even when we made emergency contact, there was no response.”

No way.

“Of all people, it had to be the sunfish leader and healer who went missing, it’s inside a gate, the kids behind us are just staring blankly after doing something, I had no choice but to use my skill to find them.”

Ah, really no way.

“So I used my snake eyes… and your chat came belatedly. …You had the presence of mind to send a chat?”

Crazy, ah, really no way.

“Even if you’re at the height of boiling blood, isn’t it a bit much inside a gate? Did your head spin because you were too happy?”

Those words were my death sentence and the sound of my life ending.

I froze like stone. I felt trapped inside stone. Sa Ye-ho was waving his hand in front of me, but the motion looked choppy and disconnected.

“You’re bright red. Can I touch you?”

Sa Ye-ho’s eyes flashed. It was the same crazed eyes I’d seen exactly once when we played the game directly like characters.

Sa Ye-ho who had become a snake itself, finding this unbelievably entertaining.

Should I just faint right here? I’m actually on the verge of fainting right now. Should I just die? That seems possible too.

“I, I can explain everything. Listen. There are circumstances.”

Yang energy and whatnot – I’d never wanted to tell anyone even if I died, but reaching this dead end, I had to pray that Sa Ye-ho would please listen. My neck turning red, I explained the whole ordeal.

…Because my body was like that, I was ultimately receiving regular treatment from Ja Hyeong-woo, and the incident in the gate was an emergency situation.

“But you looked like you were enjoying it too.”

I finally squeezed my eyes shut. My face was so hot it was hard to keep my eyes open.

With those snake eyes, what would you know? Screaming would only be digging my own grave.

‘What if he finds me disgusting?’

I’m supposed to be the leader, I don’t want to look defective.

At this moment, I remembered the Agni fight incident. The annoying giggling sound was vivid. When I muttered to myself about how that Agni bastard could do such a thing, Sa Ye-ho had definitely said:

“Such things happen in the world.”

I gave up on hiding my reddened face and lowered my gaze.

“…You said it could happen.”

I glanced up at Sa Ye-ho, but he neither particularly affirmed nor denied that.

There was only the goofy face he’d shown long ago when he first said he liked me.

“Are you dating?”

The question was so one-dimensional and direct that I was rather speechless. It was timing when I should have smoothly deflected, but my head just froze.

To say we’re not dating would make me someone who presses their lower parts together with someone they have no feelings for.

To say we are dating…

“That bastard thinks you’re dating.”

“What about you?”

I tried to roughly get past it, but there was no room to escape. Sa Ye-ho managed to open those slit eyes wide and bright, showing tremendous interest.

While grinning as he watched the blush spread from the edges of my cheeks again.

“Did Ja Hyeong-woo seduce you?”

I nodded, meaning yes. With a slight hint of resignation.

I didn’t forget to subtly imply that I was a victim who was deceived by him, and it was all because that bastard was cunning just like his looks.

“Heh. Would you fall just because he tries to seduce you? You must have let yourself fall.”

But Sa Ye-ho knew me too well.

‘We’ve played together too much. Should I push him away a bit?’

This is do or die.

*Thump*, Sa Ye-ho’s body, pushed obediently as I shoved, crashed onto the sofa floor. I climbed on top of Sa Ye-ho and growled.

“If you spread rumors, know that you’ll die.”

“Are you crazy? Like I’d out you.”

“Your word choice!”

I still couldn’t fully accept the fact that my first physical contact partner was a man. When I yelled, Sa Ye-ho giggled and showed surrender by raising both hands while lying down. It was a clear surrender pose.

“Yessir, leader.”

I had to kick my blanket for several nights in a row.

But thanks to that, I could forget the rising anxiety.

Until Stage 33 was imminent right before our eyes.
