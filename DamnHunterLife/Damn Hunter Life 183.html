Damn Hunter Life Chapter 183

<Flame Brand (Fire Attribute)>

Chollima’s hooves trampled the bamboo leaf soldiers like toys, destroying their formation. Flames erupted from the bodies of the bamboo leaf soldiers crushed under its front hooves.

Ma Cheon-ah, who had been flying around triumphantly, suddenly stopped.

A massive gust of wind arose in front of Ma Cheon-ah. The commander bamboo leaf soldier charged at her with fierce momentum.

Her hair tied in one bundle streamed long behind her. Ma Cheon-ah’s lips, visible between her hair, curved upward wickedly.

Ma Cheon-ah straightened her head and drove Chollima forward. Toward the center of the general.

But at that moment, thunk! An arrow struck perfectly between the general’s helmet and armor.

Ah, really! Ma Cheon-ah’s grumbling could be heard, but I leisurely drew a circle and escaped.

At this moment, seeing me, the commander in the rear raised his hand.

Through the air, through my skin, I could feel the energy of his command. I could tell that the bamboo leaf soldiers had set me as their target.

Woof! The dog barked loudly toward the front. Then the arrows that had been raining down on me and Hyeong-woo weakly broke off.

I quickly turned direction. Then I passed Yang Pyo, who was pushing back the enemy forces at the front center, and leaped to the opposite side.

Into the realm of swords where hundreds of blades sometimes flexibly, sometimes fiercely like torrential rain, pierced through.

I opened my mouth and shouted.

“Won Ha-ri!”

Won Ha-ri turned her body without even looking.

Directly facing me as I rode the dog familiar rushing toward her.

Just before we collided, I narrowly changed course from my side. The wind stirred by the blade grazed my cheek.

“…!”

The soldiers chasing me encountered Won Ha-ri. They tried to change formation belatedly to block the attack, but they couldn’t avoid the sharp blue sword light that Won Ha-ri raised straight up.

The bamboo soldiers shattered into pieces. Fragments flew mercilessly.

I had no intention of wasting even a few seconds. While watching that scene, I shot three arrows in succession.

In turn, I pierced through the nape of one high-ranking commander, a soldier at a central point whose fall would obstruct the path of the charging army, and among the bamboo leaf soldiers, one who was particularly skilled and had exchanged several blows with Ma Cheon-ah.

At this moment, a dragon descended beside me.

“There are archers ambushed at 10 o’clock. They’re not moving.”

As the dragon flew alongside, the rider’s face became clearly visible. I conversed with Jin Seol-woon while running.

Perhaps because we were closer, I could feel Hyeong-woo from behind examining Jin Seol-woon’s body overall and applying healing.

“They can’t move. If they shoot arrows trying to kill just nine people, they’ll hit their own allies too. Let’s make them shoot later. At their own allies instead of us, of course.”

“Please give the order then.”

As Jin Seol-woon finished speaking, a pit formed in the ground. He tilted the dragon to the side and disappeared as if sucked into it.

A few seconds later, the dragon burst up from the ground in a completely different place, splitting the earth. Bamboo leaf soldiers struck by the dragon fell in droves. Jin Seol-woon grabbed his sword in reverse grip and tilted it diagonally. The thin sword cut down all the bamboo leaf soldiers in the dragon’s path.

While the successors belonging to the assault squad stirred up the enemy lines, the remaining people also moved busily.

Myo Cho-hui raised dozens of vines. The bamboo leaf soldiers were completely trapped in the vines that rose like prison bars.

Myo Cho-hui’s small feet stepped diagonally on one of the vines that had grown vertically. She took a big running start so the vine would become taut. The glaive, propelled by the vine’s elasticity, successively knocked off the heads of bamboo leaf soldiers.

The vines that wrapped around the corpses of the fallen bamboo leaf soldiers gathered together and intertwined. It was to build a barricade.

Then, the wild boar that Hae Mi-jun rode bypassed the barricade while avoiding the soldiers’ spears. The bamboo leaf soldiers raised dust clouds as they closely pursued him.

At that moment.

Whoosh—! All the soldiers couldn’t avoid the blue mountain breeze and took it head-on.

The bamboo leaf soldiers all froze from Hae Mi-jun’s ice skill. They stuck to the barricade in orderly rows, becoming an even sturdier wall.

Under my orders, the two built up a large hill on the barren land. So that the numerically inferior successors could lead this battle as a siege rather than being swept away by the legion.

Myo Cho-hui’s body atop the hill glowed green.

<House of Trees (Wood Attribute)>

Thick trees grew densely. The trees formed walls above ground. Underground, they extended roots. They dug into the soil.

The roots wound tightly around the soul stones buried under the earth, then persistently burrowed into the center. The stones cracked and split.

I roamed around the barricade, checking the condition of all successors and carefully reading the energy beneath the ground.

‘There haven’t been any resurrected by soul stones yet. Are they saving them to resurrect as giant soldiers or something? Well, we’re grateful for that.’

Because we’ll destroy all the soul stones before then.

Hyeong-woo rode behind me, prioritizing the management of my physical condition while also watching over the team members wherever I went.

So they could conserve skills and defeat small fry with regular attacks alone.

The bamboo leaf soldiers charged blindly as if they weren’t afraid of death. Weapons with lost arms were thrown into the sky, and armor fell and rolled on the ground.

‘I was worried because there were commanders, but fortunately it’s a manageable level.’

They split the legion into multiple parts to attempt surprise attacks from various directions, and among the nine enemies, they found those with gaps and weaknesses to focus their assault on.

Good, this is it. Among the monsters I’ve seen, this is top-tier intelligence.

‘But you have to do that against opponents it works on.’

If each of us individually has the ability to defeat an army, and if communication and coordination centered on me are perfectly achieved, then it’s a different story.

Boom—! An explosion occurred from the arrow I shot. The bamboo leaf soldiers burst and scattered in all directions as if a giant boulder had been attached to the arrow.

Whew, I repeated deep breaths, trying to regain my increasingly shortened breathing. Since I was controlling the dog familiar, my stamina was rapidly depleting.

“Gang-hyeon, look at me for a moment.”

Suddenly Hyeong-woo made me turn around.

Then he lifted the durumagi wrapped around my shoulders. He covered both our heads with it.

“Mmph—”

In the sudden darkness, something soft and hot devoured my lips. It easily opened the entrance with its tongue and filled me with its breath and saliva.

‘Crazy bastard…’

I didn’t even have time to blush. I quickly pushed aside the durumagi and continued the strategy.

It was the moment when the bamboo leaf soldiers, who had seemed endlessly numerous, were starting to show gaps in their forces here and there.

Rumble—! An ominous earthquake occurred from the ground.

A giant soldier so large it seemed it would cover the entire sky appeared, breaking through the earth. Even allied soldiers were trampled and fell under its feet.

Its head cast a huge shadow over us. I didn’t hesitate.

“Abandon the castle!”

Immediately, Myo Cho-hui’s rabbit, Hae Mi-jun’s wild boar, and the dog I rode scattered in three directions.

Bang! The foot of the bamboo leaf giant struck down where we had been. The entire ground crumpled and fragments shot in all directions.

We lost the wall made using all of Myo Cho-hui and Hae Mi-jun’s skills in an instant. But now wasn’t the time to lament.

Even one alone was threatening enough to make my knees weak, but several more giant soldiers rose up, tearing through the ground.

“Four of them…”

They wore red, yellow, green, and blue cloth tied to their helmets in order.

The next moment, the monkey familiar appeared. The familiar raised its sword hidden in mist and clashed with the huge sword of the yellow giant soldier.

Screech—! The power struggle was evenly matched. A spine-chilling sound rang from the crossed blades.

After confirming that Won Ha-ri was handling one giant with her familiar, I quickly decided on the next move.

“Yang Pyo! Ma Cheon-ah! You two take the red one!”

When Yang Pyo collided with the giant soldier and scattered its energy, Ma Cheon-ah stepped on Yang Pyo’s back and leaped up to attack. The two, who used to growl at each other daily, now knew how to fight in coordination.

‘Then the barricade personnel will take the green one…’

It would be tough for me to defend alone, but if Hyeong-woo takes care of my body and Myo Cho-hui and Hae Mi-jun provide support, the four of us should be able to take down one.

Even so, one giant soldier would remain.

I hastily assigned Jin Seol-woon to lure it.

“Jin Seol-woon! Don’t think about taking it down alone, just draw its attention! We’ll deal with this side first and then come—”

I stopped speaking.

Because feather-light footsteps had landed on the nape of the running dog familiar.

Thin skirt fabric embroidered with red flowers fluttered before my eyes. Sa Ye-ho stood on the dog, looking at the blue giant soldier with his lips twisted in a grin. As if looking at delicious prey.

“Can I have that one?”

Sa Ye-ho was specialized in causing chaos in enemy lines with rapid mobility, but didn’t have the skill to take down strong individuals like bosses.

In normal circumstances, that is.

Sa Ye-ho had a high-level skill that had manifested relatively recently.

When I first saw the activation conditions for that skill, I was left speechless.

‘This bastard is really insane.’

I absolutely couldn’t understand it with my common sense. What was so good about it for that person.

Hiss—! The snake familiar slipped close beside the dog. As soon as Sa Ye-ho mounted the snake’s back, it quickly slithered toward the giant.

Sa Ye-ho ran briefly on the snake. The snake raised its head up to the sky’s end. At perfect timing, Sa Ye-ho stepped on the snake’s head and leaped to an unbelievable height.

The giant’s huge helmet tilted upward. I watched the scene of one human, insignificantly small compared to that giant, plunging down from the sky.

<Yearning for the Sun (Fire Attribute)>

Red tattoos resembling flames wound around his snow-white skin. The tattoos even climbed up to the hand holding the dagger. The dagger turned red-hot like heated iron, then its length suddenly extended to match Sa Ye-ho’s height.

The giant’s hand grabbed! at the empty air where Sa Ye-ho was.

But there was no way the sluggish giant could keep up with Sa Ye-ho’s speed.

In the blink of an eye, the giant’s arm that had grasped only empty air caught fire from hand to shoulder.
