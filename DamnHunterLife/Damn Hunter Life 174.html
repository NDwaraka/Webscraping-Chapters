Damn Hunter Life Chapter 174

At the same time, in southern Seoul, dozens of kilometers away.

The gate here was already in a state where the boss monster had escaped through the entrance. It was quite troublesome with the boss hiding somewhere in the city.

Won Ha-ri sprinted quickly through the alley with dozens of blades behind her back.

A dragon with a divine appearance even in semi-transparent form charged low beside her, following Won Ha-ri. Jin Seol-woon, who was riding with his upper body pressed close to the dragon’s body, shouted.

“Gang-hyeon-ssi, has the boss’s location been identified?”

[“Narrowing down candidates. Catch the mobs in front first!”]

Clatter! Several metal blades embedded in the monster’s body.

“Here……”

Then someone’s voice flowed from the air.

Won Ha-ri only realized Sa Ye-ho had moved in that direction after hearing the voice.

Sa Ye-ho rushed forward then stopped abruptly. His eyes traced the nearby street address signs and scenery.

Ding, at that moment the status window vibrated.

Gang-hyeon, who had finally identified the boss’s location, sent a map. Won Ha-ri checked it first.

“Ye-ho sunbae-nim! Moving toward the boss monster……?”

As she spoke and turned her head, the red figure that should have been there had disappeared without a trace.

“He’s gone!”

Jin Seol-woon also lost Sa Ye-ho despite watching with both eyes open. It was the voice from beyond the communication that calmed the panicking Won Ha-ri.

[“That bastard’s too fast to find you. I’m following the location for now, so just you two move. We’ll meet anyway since we’re going to catch the boss.”]

After casually giving orders, Gang-hyeon, who confirmed the address of where they were once more, let out a hollow laugh.

[“Right, where else would you be chasing to?”]

While there was a noisy team with the leader’s concentrated care overlapping with a team member’s impulsive actions.

There was also an extremely silent practice team that only did their own work.

Swoosh! A sword with a blade as large as a rogue’s sword cut the monster in half.

Whoosh, the flames dwelling in the sword ignited everywhere Ma Cheon-ah passed.

And before the flames could transfer to brick floors or sculptures, thin waves gently covered those places and extinguished them.

Fire and water are opposing attributes. However, since Ma Cheon-ah’s combat style and attributes were likely to turn the surroundings into a wasteland, Gang-hyeon attached Ja Hyeong-woo to Ma Cheon-ah only for mainland Korea deployments.

The healer of the combat team formed this way was currently.

[“……Monster 50m ahead!”]

Looking at the scene with a robot-like blank face, then brightening up and raising his head whenever Gang-hyeon’s voice was heard.

When the voice was no longer heard, he returned to his emotionless expression and did his work.

Ma Cheon-ah watched that sight in disbelief.

‘He should just go be with the Leader. What’s he doing here?’

However, she didn’t voice her criticism out loud. This was unusual for Ma Cheon-ah, who liked giving orders.

Soon Ma Cheon-ah spotted the large boss monster ahead. However, since she had flown on Chollima, Ja Hyeong-woo wasn’t behind her. He had been left behind due to slow speed.

Gang-hyeon didn’t miss that.

[“Don’t rush ahead, go in with Ja Hyeong-woo! While watching the water skills!”]

“I know too. The nagging is.”

The boss monster, which hadn’t noticed Ma Cheon-ah yet, was crunching and eating building debris with its nape fully exposed.

Though impatient, Ma Cheon-ah gritted her teeth and obediently waited for Ja Hyeong-woo like a horse tied to a rope. Broadcast drones buzzed around Ma Cheon-ah and the boss monster.

Around that time, somewhere in Korea, Ma Cheon-ah’s parents, safe in their home, were watching their only daughter’s fight through live news.

They were also quite extraordinary people like Ma Cheon-ah, so even when monster blood and flesh splattered from the sword their daughter wielded, they made absurd comments instead of worrying.

“My goodness, our child has grown up. She knows to endure without rushing in. Did you see her waiting just now? Is it because of that big boy?”

“No, no. Isn’t it thanks to that pretty child named Gang-hyeon? It seems like it’s been since he changed, right?”

That Gang-hyeon was at school with another successor who likewise remained on standby.

“You’re the last bastion with me. If gate cleanup is delayed, we’ll go out to replace the tired ones.”

“Yes!”

Though his answer was still energetic and his eyes sparkled, a slightly wiser and more mature light flowed compared to before.

Gang-hyeon stared at him blankly then suddenly smiled faintly. Hae Mi-jun was a little surprised by this.

“Gang-hyeon hyung, why?”

“It’s nothing.”

It seems you’re graduating from me now too.

* * *

One road in Seoul had an eerie atmosphere flowing with all people having evacuated.

No, should I say ‘almost’ everyone evacuated?

“Huff, gasp!”

One man who couldn’t escape to a safe place in time hastily got out of his stalled car and ran.

Behind him, one vicious life form that wasn’t human chased him, drooling.

As if he might trip over the ground at any moment, the man staggered but kept running. Nevertheless, the monster’s hands only got closer.

The man, who had been running while constantly looking back, finally fell.

“Huh, huaaak!”

Thud—!

Right at that moment, a gleaming black bike violently rammed into the monster.

“Hi, hiik.”

The man forced his weakened legs to move and fled, but couldn’t believe what he saw with his own eyes.

Because even though there was an accident where the bike’s front was horribly crushed, it made no sense for the driver to get off the bike as if nothing happened.

A tsk sound echoed from inside the black helmet. It was while looking at the dead bike, not the monster.

The monster that had fallen got up, spitting out a Grrr…… angry cry.

The helmeted figure’s head slowly turned toward the monster.

Not just bikes, but even guns and swords couldn’t pierce that hide. The hands of one who was once Korea’s top hunter were no different now.

Nevertheless, he had no intention of backing down.

The furious monster approached making thudding sounds. He grabbed a nearby traffic sign pipe with both hands and pushed it up strongly. Crack—! Concrete and ground were pulled up together.

‘If I can’t pierce or wound the hide.’

There was only one place Yoon Sae-geon aimed for.

He lightly held the pulled-out sign like a spear and waited patiently even as the monster grabbed him with its hands to lift him up.

Right now, until the monster opened its maw wide toward him.

The tip of the ‘No U-Turn’ sign thrust into the monster’s mouth.

Kweee—! The monster’s scream echoed throughout the area. Dodging the flailing arms, Yoon Sae-geon persistently shoved the pipe into the monster’s stomach.

With the sensation at his fingertips, he was certain that the sign he thrust in had pierced through the throat and passed through several organs.

The monster thrashed about, frothing at the mouth with Grrr— sounds. Yoon Sae-geon, who had shoved the sign all the way to the head without leaving anything, dropped down and rolled on the ground before getting up.

Having this physical ability yet sitting in a shelter with other people also looked unseemly, so he had just taken the bike to go home.

He didn’t particularly intend to save anyone, but if his juniors were unreliable, he himself had to fulfill his responsibilities.

‘They’re late. Is it because the Leader is in that state?’

Even though other monsters appeared, there was no sign of hunters.

Both monsters looked at Yoon Sae-geon. Grrr, they bared all their yellow, hideous teeth.

That’s when it happened. Bright red fire that shot up from somewhere directly hit the monster’s body.

Even though he had lost his penetrating eyes, Yoon Sae-geon could tell immediately upon seeing it. The form and trail of that crimson flame, that power that burned cheerfully and wouldn’t be extinguished.

From his tensely strained arms, strength slowly drained away.

“Whoops.”

Suddenly his body floated up. Yoon Sae-geon stiffened his jaw slightly inside the helmet.

Arms that looked thin compared to him had lifted Yoon Sae-geon up like a princess.

When he closed and opened his eyes, he was in front of the shelter.

It was a speed that he, now a civilian, couldn’t follow with his eyes. Yoon Sae-geon newly felt the gap between himself and Sa Ye-ho.

“Sir, you weren’t hurt, were you?”

Once Yoon Sae-geon stood on the ground with both feet, the eye level of the one asking sociably came down below his own.

He had been wearing a helmet all this time. Moreover, dressed in thick padding suitable for winter weather and even wearing a scarf, his tattoos weren’t visible either.

Wrapped up to that extent, would anyone recognize him? He hadn’t even said a word yet.

The helmet man substituted his expression of intent by just nodding his head slightly. He tried to slip into the shelter, but…….

Sa Ye-ho, who had been behind him, was somehow standing in front of him now.

“Sir, I can’t let you through without verbal confirmation.”

One-sided persistent conversation continued.

Sa Ye-ho’s eyes carefully examined the round helmet. There was definitely laughter at the end of his words.

“Sir, please just say one word. Are you really okay? Are you feeling very bad? Can’t you even say one word? Were you perhaps very scared? We should have rescued you faster, I’m sorry. Sir—.”

Eventually the helmet man stopped without being able to move even one step.

A low voice suppressing anger was finally dragged out.

“Go follow and catch the boss.”

Sa Ye-ho grinned, showing all his neat teeth. He tilted his head to take a pretty angle and looked up at him.

“Show me your face. I’ll go if I see it. Hm? Hm?”

Yoon Sae-geon knew that a couple of reporters at the shelter were looking this way. Their cameras were filming in this direction.

Whoo, a long and irritated sigh echoed.

“Annoying bastard.”

His hand, lifted with restrained movement, slowly lifted the helmet.

Pale sunlight embraced in turn his nape, slightly moist with sweat, his cool-colored skin, and his hair tied up.

Sa Ye-ho’s smile became so intense that it seemed like the corners of his mouth had become fixed that way.
